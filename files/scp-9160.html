Special Containment Procedures: A total of 208 Mobile Task Force (MTF) units are to be deployed three times daily to designated sectors in search of SCP-9160-B13, as well as any items that may be identified as the origin of SCP-9160-A. Each unit is to consist of 30 MTF operatives. Following each deployment, a written report detailing search activities and findings is to be submitted by the commanding officer of every unit.

If any of the aforementioned objects are found, the O5 Council is to be notified immediately. No further action is to be taken until direct orders are issued by the Council.

Laboratories currently studying SCP-9160-B1 through B3 are required to submit a weekly report on the object under study. If new discoveries are found, the O5 Council is to be contacted immediately.

SCP-9160-B5 through B6, SCP-9160-B8 through B12, and the paper documentation of SCP-9160-B7 are to be stored within heat-resistant safes under continuous surveillance. A minimum of four MTF operatives are to be stationed as guards at all times. Authorization from an O5 Council member is required for any access or use of the aforementioned objects.

Laboratories are also tasked with searching for the hypothesized SCP-9160-C. A weekly report is required from all facilities involved in this investigation. If new discoveries are found, the O5 Council is to be contacted immediately.

Description: SCP-9160-A is a radio signal of unknown origin, first detected at Site ██ on ██/██/20██. The transmission is encoded in International Morse code.

The signal is captured purely because of luck,we're testing our radio wave pirating device that day.

By Dr ████████

SCP-9160-B consists of eleven sheets of paper composed of an unknown material combined with recycled paper, a single digital record, and one hypothesized record of unknown existence. The documents contain either historical accounts between SCP-9160-E and humanities or descriptions of the scenarios occurring when SCP-9160-A reaches SCP-9160-C.

The eleven paper documents have been designated SCP-9160-B1 through SCP-9160-B6 and SCP-9160-B8 through SCP-9160-B12. The digital record is designated SCP-9160-B7, while the hypothesized record is designated SCP-9160-B13.

SCP-9160-C, originally named “Binah”, is an entity outside our current dimension. SCP-9160-C consists of power which can create multiple XK class end-of-the-world scenarios at will. Historical accounts indicate that SCP-9160-C alongside another entity once served SCP-343, until the dissolution of a peace treaty established between them. SCP-9160-C remains uncontained and has not been located to date.

SCP-9160-D resembles a sprouting bamboo stalk, possessing white foliage and eight red, semi-oval structures symmetrically distributed along its sides. Each red structure exhibits nuclear energy levels estimated at approximately 4,000 Tsar Bomba equivalents, or roughly 8.4 zettajoules. The root system of SCP-9160-D is presumed to be infinite in length, as the Foundation has not yet located its terminus. No safe or effective method for the neutralization of SCP-9160-D has been discovered to date.

SCP-9160-E refers to a race of entities for which no verified information exists beyond their reported presence within the so-called “Lifeless Land.” Documented accounts describe their issuance of creation commands to Binah Followers regarding SCP-9160-B, their involvement in the destruction of SCP-9160-B7, and their identification as the military force serving SCP-9160-C.

Although SCP-9160-A possesses the anomalous property of being untraceable to its origin, the Foundation is not prioritizing on SCP-9160-A until the subsequent discovery of SCP-9160-B4 and SCP-9160-D.

This text was discovered in the dormitory of Dr ████████. Despite daily cleaning, the text remained undetected until a D-Class personnel assigned to SCP-9160-related duties was cleaning the room.

Dr ████████ was subsequently brought in for questioning concerning SCP-9160-B4. The transcript of the conversation follows:

SCP-9160-D was discovered in ████████, ████████, ████████ on ██/██/20████.

During the discovery of SCP-9160-D, seven self-proclaimed "Binah Followers" were captured. They claimed that SCP-9160-D was "a preparation for Binah’s strike against humanity". This claim was corroborated by the discovery of SCP-9160-B5, which was located five minutes after SCP-9160-D was uncovered.

SCP-9160-B1 through B3 were subsequently discovered across multiple locations.

The events described in SCP-9160-B4 are predicted to constitute nine XK-class end-of-the-world scenarios. Each individual event is detailed in SCP-9160-B5 through B12. As nine scenarios are referenced in SCP-9160-B4 and Dr ████████ had mentioned thirteen prophecies, the Foundation has assumed the existence of SCP-9160-B13.
