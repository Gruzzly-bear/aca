Special Containment Procedures:

No further efforts to penetrate or traverse SCP-9540 are to be undertaken.

Foundation agents embedded across various national space agencies are to cover up any further instances of interstellar objects impacting with the surface of SCP-9540.

Given the possible future threat to the Veil posed by SCP-9540, the Foundation is to make all possible efforts to halt the development of space-faring technology. Should these efforts fail, MTF Sierra-4 " The Bridge Trolls" are to be deployed, and are to detain and amneticise any civilians found within 1000au of SCP-9540.

Description:

SCP-9540 refers to a thaumaturgically reinforced spherical shell enveloping the solar system at a distance of 100,000 astronomical units (au1) from Earth.

SCP-9540 (indicated by the red line) on a map of the solar system. Distances are in au.

SCP-9540 measures 100 meters thick, and is constructed of an unknown metallic material superficially resembling steel. Although having a tangible form, SCP-9540 allows electromagnetic radiation to travel directly through its surface, effectively making it invisible, and allowing for viewers to see beyond the space that SCP-9540 occupies. Whilst this property is active at all times, when observed from 1000 kilometres or closer, the surface of SCP-9540 becomes visible to the viewer.

The observation of meteoroids entering the solar system via SCP-9540 supports the theory that SCP-9540 may serve as a one-way wall. This is further supported by the fact that the opposite has yet to be observed.

Discovery:

The presence of SCP-9540 was first detected by National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) researchers working at the Haleakalā Observatory on the 20/05/2018. Astronomers had been tracking T3-Pallin, an interstellar object measuring roughly 30 kilometers wide that was on an exit course from the Solar System. When it reached a distance of 100,000au, it suddenly disappeared from monitoring systems without explanation.

The documentation of this incident was flagged by Foundation agents embedded within NASA as a potential anomaly. All documentation was successfully retrieved without incident, and all civilians involved in the discovery of SCP-9540 were amnesticised.

Based on its hyperbolic excess velocity, Foundation researchers subsequently confirmed that T3-Pallin was a true interstellar object and that it had already crossed this threshold as it passed into the solar system.

Whilst the O5 Council approved an expedition to the last known location of T-3 Pallin, it was not until 2031 that Foundation space travel technology, specifically FTL2 technology, was sufficiently advanced to permit travel to this location.

Addendum 9540.01.01 - Initial Survey of SCP-9540

Report type: Compilation of discoveries during initial FERV3 expeditions to SCP-9540
Author: Dr. Michael Lennox
Date: 10/03/2031

  • SCP-9540 was first reached by the FERV Beckett, which described the physical properties of SCP-9540, and noted its anomalous interaction with electromagnetic radiation.
  • The FERV Canberra (the second vessel to arrive) utilising onboard radar tools, mapped this section of SCP-9540 as it travelled along its surface, failing to find an end to the structure.
  • The data gathered by the Canberra indicated a slight curvature to SCP-9540's structure indicated it may be spherical in nature.
  • Based upon this suspicion, another FERV was sent 100,000au at a skewed angle, which also encountered the surface of SCP-9540, noting the same curvature.
  • Whilst mapping of the entirety of SCP-9540's surface is effectively impossible, the section mapped by these two ships strongly support the conclusion that SCP-9540 is indeed spherical in nature, and likely surrounds the known solar system.
  • Following the discoveries of the Canberra, the Rainmaker was ordered to attempt to create a breach in SCP-9540. All onboard kinetic and direct-energy weapons failed to affect SCP-9540.
  • The Canberra reported anomalous readings in their radar mapping, indicative of an artificial structure close to the surface of SCP-9540. Further information on this structure is available under Addendum Two.
  • The Canberra also reported encountering 8 total runes carved into the wall of SCP-9540. Further information and analysis of these runes are available under Addendum Three.

Addendum 9540.01.02 - Structure Expedition

Date: 09/06/2031

An expedition of the structure found it to be devoid of occupants and in a state of disrepair4.

Foundation researchers undertaking testing on a recovered part of the structure's external shielding.

The structure featured a multitude of highly sophisticated communications and electronic equipment that appeared to have been intentionally sabotaged.

Exploration of the base's exterior revealed the presence of a large, high-powered telescope directed at Earth. Similar to the other equipment found in the base, the telescope had been sabotaged, and was non-functional.

Whilst non-functional, data retrieval from the telescope yielded one locally stored image of the Vostok I5, depicted exiting Earth's atmosphere.

Addendum 9540.01.03 - Rune Analysis

Date: 11/07/2031

SCP-9540 rune - Variant 1.

SCP-9540 rune - Variant 2.


Despite the combined efforts of the Department of Thaumaturgy and Department of Linguistics, the runes could not be directly translated. Cross analysis of these runes with similar runes previously encountered in Wanderer's Library entries has yielded insight into their meaning.

The first variant appears to indicate the idea of containment, or imprisonment of a group or body. It should be noted that the interlocking of the surrounding hexagon and octagram suggest a concerted, outside effort or force responsible for this.

The second variant has been theorised to indicate an organised effort at suppression, very likely of a current or future threat. The outward forks of this central symbol indicate an ability to spread, or propagate.

The Department of Thaumaturgy, via the use of Thaumaturgic Resonance Dating, was able to estimate the age of these runes to be roughly 70 years.

D-9901: This thing's only got enough juice to do one more cut, and I want to use that to get into the cockpit.

The probe reaches the end of the hallway, and turns right through the open doorway, revealing a small room featuring a bed, table, and locker. Small figurines and stuffed animals depicting unknown species float lazily in the room, in addition to a few instances of empty food packaging.

D-9901: Someone was here.

The probe turns and exits the room, traversing down the hallway to the sealed door at the end. It initiates the same cutting procedure, carefully cutting out a circular opening in the door to the cockpit. Once more, the drone pushes onto the circle of cut metal, and enters through the newly created aperture. The cockpit is entirely coated in the black weblike growth, though just in front of the pilots seat, a reflective surface, barely noticeable through the growth can be seen. The drone approaches this surface, and retracts its arm, before extending out another one fitted with a dextrous manipulator claw. It uses this tool to grab at the vine-like web, pulling it off what is revealed to be a screen.

D-8823: Better get a sample of that.

The claw retracts back towards the probe and out of view, before once more extending outwards, clearing off the vines around the edges of the screen, revealing a series of buttons and controls. The claw folds away one of its fingers, leaving only one finger extended, using it to press onto the leftmost button. Flickers of light come from the screen, before it eventually turns on fully, revealing a message.

Contextual Notice: The remainder of this video log utilises AARTT (AIC-Assisted Real-Time Translation) technology to provide a partial translation of the computer's content. Though the number of characters allowed for an increased accuracy when compared to the translations of addendum 01.03, the technology remains experimental, and the Geminid's onboard database was limited, thus the translations remain partially inaccurate.

The message indicates the ship is currently in an extreme power-saving mode, and that only the ship's data storage was currently available. After a few seconds, the notice fades away, revealing a list of separate entries. The mechanical claw tries several of the buttons, before eventually selecting the correct one to open one of the entries. The drone hovers in place for several minutes, clicking through each entry:

Entry 1: Vanguards returned, fascinating organism recovered from investigation of nearby body. Assigned to investigate. Properties of growth: incredible, highly resilient, adaptable. Potential applications in engineering, medicine possible. Conservatory Council providing expanded resources for study.

Entry 2: Growth truly incredible: Growth shows no end, digestive properties yet to show limit. Applications in waste management: assured, once means to slow discovered. No as of yet discovered physical barrier can prevent spread. Personally concerning.

Entry 3: Northern sector of lab overgrown. Director to sit before Conservatory Council, though solution to immediate issue unavailable. Physical symptoms detected in technicians. Infection to individuals prompted intervention by Planetary Guard - though no greater luck by them. Relieved of duty - team silenced in fear of possible panic.

Entry 4: Public news of growth detection at star-port at (untranslatable) despite no intentional transport of organism. Significant population alarm. Original lab entirely lost - alongside all research notes belonging to team. Director guilty of treason, intention to destroy planet despite no involvement in retrieval, lies. Council guilty, diverting.

Entry 5: State of planetary emergency enacted by Planetary Guard, space ports guarded by military - fears of further spread to colonies. Situation bleak. Conservatory Council to discuss future course of action. High echelon individuals evacuated, rest locked. Personally terrified. Family to die, locked away, Council incapable, squabbling, corrupt. Further colonies gone, all efforts to date: failed, adapting quickly. To attempt to reach vessel under darkness. No hope here, only among the dark sky, where it isn't. Will upload to ship if successful - document there. Dangerous, no other choice, habitation minimization methods uncertain to lower echelons.

Entry 6: Reached vessel with mate and offspring. Offspring seen by Planetary Guard, too slow. Minor damage to underside from Quarantine Enforcement Platform - able to get away before full activation. Future uncertain. Colonies compromised, no hope there, many dead. Conservatory Council discussing planetary neutralization, though many planets - impossible. Planetary Guard ships amassing, likely "habitability minimization", success unlikely, none so far, none now. Council remains still divided, some guilty, discussing planetary quarantine efforts for healthy planets - guarded, jailed, mistakes unrepeated. Perhaps they realise no solutions now. Heard from others - some guards deserting, leaving the walls.

Entry 7: Noticed power leak - damage indicator. Quarantine Enforcement Platform damage unlikely - effects occur too late, escape many moons ago. Didn't want to look, fears realised. Cannot return - execution or re-assignment to frontlines clearance worse. Heard from others - oxygen harvesters converging on (untranslatable). Planets burn and freeze, choked of air. Colonies worse. Situation remains worsening.

Creep on board. It hid aboard. Festering. Doesn't make sense, growth so slow, should've taken over ship long ago. Growing fast now, reason: unsure.

Out of options, nowhere to go. Do not want little ones to go slow, seen that - painful. To override airlock when all asleep, quick, painless - no suffering.

After several minutes, the drone backs away from the computer.

D-8823: Jesus…

D-9901: We can't bring that sample back with us.

D-8823: Look, I get it - but I need this alright? The Foundation'll be able to deal with it, I'm certain.

D-9901: I'm sorry, 88, you need to put it back.

D-8823: A discovery like this will…

D-9901: Get everyone killed. You think the Foundation will want you back when you bring that back into our solar system? You wanted to show them you're not a loose cannon, right? Then put it back, get some photos, and let's go home.

The drone retracts its arm, removing a sample container containing the black growth and releasing it.

[End Recording]

Addendum 9540.08 - Communication Log

Despite minor technical difficulties, the Geminid successfully returned back via SCP-9540. The following communication exchange was recorded between the Geminid and Canberra following the return of the Geminid via SCP-9540.

Addendum 9540.08 - Communication Log

Date: 10/12/2031

[Begin Log]

D-8823: Lennox! Lennox! Please tell me you're still here.

There is no response.

D-8823: Len!

Dr. Lennox: D-8823, welcome back.

D-8823: We found something out there man, it… If we can trust the source we found, man… You need to tell… I-

Dr. Lennox: Spit it out.

D-8823: The logs, it's all there. It's like a grey goo. It eats… and we nearly brought it back with us. I put myself in real danger to get these answers, alright, please make sure the disciplinary committee hears that.

Dr. Lennox: Did you make contact with whatever is responsible for SCP-9540?

D-8823: We found some logs and a ship, nothing living, I was going to bring back a sample but…

Dr. Lennox: Should've gotten a sample. You both know pictures won't cut it for your retrial.

D-8823: What was I supposed to do? Bringing it back could've - would've been dangerous. The last time I put people in danger to help with your projects I…

Dr. Lennox: You were the one behind the wheel, 88, let's not neglect that fact.

D-8823: We both know it was more than that.

Dr. Lennox: All that matters is what the committee saw.

D-8823: You really think I should've brought it back?

Dr. Lennox: Like I said, the gears of the Foundation are oiled by risk-taking, it's how anything gets done.

D-8823: What exactly were you risking here?

Dr. Lennox: If things went wrong I'd be to blame, but if they went right we'd…

D-8823: There never was a we, was there, Lennox? If things went wrong, you'd throw me under the bus, God knows you can't talk me into lying to Ethics again.

Dr. Lennox: The FTL program was more important than any of us, losing you was the least-

D-8823: I should've fucking seen it earlier, you never wanted to help me, this entire time I've been your personal labrat, doing the reckless things you needed done for your career. I lost my job for you, for your grand fucking visions. Then you come along with what you said was an olive branch, and… only now I realise it was just signing up for another fucking job of yours. Fuck you, Lennox.

Dr. Lennox: I'm sorry if that's how you feel, Michael.

D-8823: I thought you were my friend. I thought I had someone looking out for me. I felt like I was doing the right thing when I stood in front of that committee and lied to them. Fuck you.

Dr. Lennox: Security will meet you at docking point three.

[End Log]

Addendum 9540.09 - O5 Council Meeting Transcript

Date: 27/03/2032

[Begin Transcript]

O5-1: I warned you, Thirteen, for Christ sake I warned you.

O5-1 pauses, taking a few moments to regain his composure.

O5-2: What's happened?

O5-1: I received word today from Site-140 that the FTL fleet assigned to 9540 was detected enroute for re-entry. Site-140 hadn't got any warning or communication from them.

O5-13 begins to show visible signs of anxiety.

O5-2: What's that got to do with Thirteen?

O5-1: We never ordered the fleet back, so I looked into it. Thirteen made the unilateral decision to, in secret, approve an expedition through SCP-9540.

The Councillors all look over at O5-13.

O5-1: That's not all, though, is it Thirteen?

O5-13 doesn't respond.

O5-1: They found something out there, a sort of destructive all-consuming growth. It even made contact with the scout ship. You were informed of that, weren't you, Thirteen?

O5-13 once more fails to produce a response.

O5-1: That entire fucking civilization gone, Thirteen! Gone! They had a Dyson swarm for fuck sake, and this thing was still enough to take them out.

O5-13 responds, faintly.

O5-13: The ship was… went through quarantine, the whole ship was sterilised, they didn't even bring back a sample. I just wanted to get it back before… Look, I fucked up, alright, but what's done is done, the ship'll be home in two weeks time and it'll be like-

O5-1: Not another fucking word!

O5-2: One-

O5-1: You want to know what I think? That thing was waiting, it wanted someone to come along and carry it home. Those Class-D walked straight into a pile of world-ending shit and tracked it back into our solar system. If you'd read those logs they found a little more thoroughly, you'd know that thing hides itself well.

A brief wave of silence washed over the room, before O5-1 speaks up again.

O5-1: Do you have any idea of the risk this thing poses if those logs are to be believed?

O5-13: Yes…

O5-1: If this thing reaches Earth, it'll almost certainly be a K class scenario, Thirteen. At minimum a WK9, and more likely an XK10. Do you have any idea what that means? Billions dead. You'll wish you were dead with that much blood on your hands.

O5-4: Do they know?

O5-1: No. Only O5-13 had the credentials to view the logs. I think Thirteen ought to be the one to tell them the danger she's put them in.

O5-1 reaches down beside his chair, and pulls a black leather suitcase onto the table. He removes a laptop from it, placing it atop the table, before sliding it over to O5-13.

O5-1: Tell them, Thirteen. Tell them what happened out there, that they may never get to return to Earth.

O5-13 tightly screws her eyes shut, before regaining her composure, and signing into the laptop. She clicks through several tabs and types of the keyboard briefly before beginning to meekly speak. The entirety of the Council watch O5-13.

O5-13: This is… O5-13. FTL Group Alpha, do you read me?

O5-13 waits in anticipation of a response for several seconds.

O5-13: FTL Group Alpha, this is O5-13, this is urgent.

She awaits a response, the colour draining from her face, and her eyes beginning to open wide.

O5-13: FTL Group…

O5-13 pushes the laptop away from herself. The room fills with silence.

O5-6: Maybe the comms are broken, it wouldn't be the first time those piece of shit systems played up.

O5-13: Can we… Is it possible to… neutralize the ship?

O5-1: No, not while it's in FTL, and if we shoot it down as it re-enters then it could… break apart over the Earth.

The Council falls into a brief moment of silent deliberation.

O5-13: They couldn't stop it either…

O5-7: That's not helpful right now Thirteen, we need to find a solution to your little-

O5-13: We survived because they sealed us away as they died, we were spared. If… if the ship really is carrying it, and it reaches Earth… we couldn't possibly stop it.

O5-1: This is not what I want to hear right now!

O5-13: Perhaps we can find our answers externally. They couldn't beat it, but they've already shown us how to survive it.

O5-2: And how's that then?

O5-13: We… amputate… to stop the rot. We can't hope to eliminate it entirely, but perhaps we can consolidate our resources, and stop it from reaching our sites, that buys us time at least. We can live to fight another day. Push comes to shove, we have the non-FTL fleet left.

O5-2: Planetary evacuation? Christ woman, a moment ago you were breaking down at the thought of this thing reaching Earth.

O5-1: She has a point, Two. If this thing is as bad as we think, we can't afford to stretch ourselves thin evacuating civilians or fighting it globally. Hell, we'll already be spread thin enforcing a planetary no-fly zone, shutting down travel corridors.

O5-2: You're talking about turning populations into petri dishes for fuck sake, think about this for a moment.

O5-1: You're right, Two, we are just talking about it. For now, all we can do is hope it is a simple communication issue.

The Council falls silent, the faint static from the computer continuing on in the background.

[End Transcript]
