SCP-9349

Special Containment Procedures

SCP-9349 is to be monitored remotely. In the event its anomalous properties remanifest, it is to be captured and held in a Class-4 equine enclosure.

Description

SCP-9349 was a domestic horse (Equus ferus caballus) which seemingly possessed human-level intelligence and was anomalously capable of speech. The entity claimed to have once been a human transformed into its current form by thaumaturgy. As SCP-9349 was never fully within Foundation custody, further information is limited largely to the entity’s own unverified claims.

Discovery

The Foundation learned of the existence of SCP-9349 while monitoring a small conclave belonging to the Order of the Sidereal Stag, an occult religious order devoted to the eponymous Sidereal Stag, a minor deity. This surveillance began when it was discovered that this conclave, located in Southeastern Missouri, had failed to maintain the counter-divinatory wards usually used by members of the Order to avoid detection.1 Hoping to gather information about the secretive group, the Foundation quickly subjected the conclave’s domicile and its surroundings to extensive thaumaturgic surveillance. As a result, the Foundation possesses extensive records of events taking place on and near the property between March 2024 and January 2025.

Prior to SCP-9349’s arrival, three individuals resided on the site.

  • Remy Bulter (28): The conclave’s “Sidereal” (leader), a skilled thaumaturge specializing in “folk magic,” especially magical healing.
  • Earnest Knight (26): The conclave’s “Horned Warden,”2 and an accomplished theurge.3
  • Nathan Sharp (25): The youngest member of the conclave, Sharp is a skilled and studious alchemist known to frequent the Wanderer’s Library.

The Foundation became aware of SCP-9349 on August 18th, 2024, when the entity approached this conclave’s residence while looking for sanctuary after escaping captivity on a nearby farm. Below is a transcription of the earliest Foundation record to feature SCP-9349.

Foreword: SCP-9349’s approach triggered a thaumaturgic alarm placed by Butler, alerting the conclave that someone was approaching. The three emerged from their domicile to investigate.

<Begin Log, August 18, 2024, 03:03>

[Butler, Knight, and Sharp approach the boarder of the conclave where the disturbance was detected. Knight has a bow4 at the ready.]

Earnest Knight: They came from this way?

Remy Butler: Yeah. The alarm went off just a minute ago. They should still be nearby.

Nathan Sharp: How are we supposed to spot anything out here? It’s so dark.

Earnest Knight: Hold on, let me do something about that.

[Knight clasps his hands together.]

Earnest Knight: Father of the Night, bless us with your sterling sight.

[Knight relaxes his pose.]

Earnest Knight: There. We should be able to see, now.

Nathan Sharp: That’s much better. Thanks, honey.

Remy Butler: There’s a horse over there.

[The three look in the direction Butler is indicating, where SCP-9349 stands. SCP-9349 looks back at them.]

Nathan Sharp: Just a horse?

Earnest Knight: Apparently. Do we have any idea where it came from?

Nathan Sharp: Or why it’s staring at us?

Remy Butler: It probably belongs to one of the farmers around here.

Earnest Knight: Then we need to get rid of it as soon as possible. We don’t want anyone to have a reason to visit us. They could see something.

Nathan Sharp: Relax. It’s just a horse. I’m sure we’ll resolve this without any trouble. Rem, can you cast something to help figure out where it came from?

[SCP-9349 begins to approach the group.]

Earnest Knight: And now it’s walking toward us.

Nathan Sharp: I guess we look friendly.

Earnest Knight: I like to think I don’t.

Nathan Sharp: Yes, you do.

Earnest Knight: Well, either way, we need to figure out where it came from.

SCP-9349: Perhaps it would be easier if I simply informed you?

[The three stare silently at SCP-9349.]

Earnest Knight: Did you just talk?

SCP-9349: Yes.

Earnest Knight: Okay. That’s good, then. Not going insane.

SCP-9349: I gather from your conversations that you are spellcasters?

[Knight and Sharp both look toward Butler, who hesitates for a moment before speaking.]

Remy Butler: Yes, we are.

SCP-9349: Excellent. To be clear, I ask only because I would not want to attract the wrong kind of attention by being open about my ability to speak among those unaccustomed to the supernatural.

Remy Butler: I understand. Why are you here?

SCP-9349: I’m looking for shelter. I have recently escaped captivity on a nearby farm. I do not expect my absence will be noticed until morning. However, once it is noted, I believe my owners will begin searching for me. I do not wish to be returned to them. They are not aware of my intellect, and should they reacquire me, I will be forced to once again take on the role of an unspeaking beast of burden.

Nathan Sharp: You poor thing…

Earnest Knight: Can you prove anything you just said?

Nathan Sharp: Earnie!

Earnest Knight: I’m just being diligent.

Nathan Sharp: He needs our help.

Earnest Knight: Possibly.

Nathan Sharp: [Sighs, and turns toward Butler] What do you think?

Remy Butler: [Studies SCP-9349 for a moment.] How long do you need someone to hide you?

SCP-9349: I should hope to impose on you for as little time as possible. I would not approach you at all, except that my owners will rise soon, and I would not like to travel further until they are once again asleep.

Earnest Knight: Who owns you?

SCP-9349: The McCallisters.

Earnest Knight: [Studies SCP-9349 for a moment.] I guess it is your call, Rem. I don’t trust this, but… maybe someone does need our help.

Nathan Sharp: Maybe?

Remy Butler: [Looks up at SCP-9349] You do need our help?

SCP-9349: Gravely. As I said, I should like to impose as little as possible. I wish only to be housed for the day and not turned over to my owners. I shall resume my flight tomorrow at dusk, when they are asleep once again.

Earnest Knight: We don’t have any food for you, and we can’t afford to buy any.

SCP-9349: I am not asking you to. I do not intend to remain here long enough for such a thing to be needed.

Earnest Knight: We don’t have any other facilities for keeping a horse.

Nathan Sharp: He’s sapient, though. We don’t need a stable to keep him in. We can just keep him in the barn.

[Knight doesn’t immediately respond. He looks at Butler. Butler sighs.]

Remy Butler: I’m sorry, honey. I think we have to help him.

Earnest Knight: [Takes a long, deep breath.] Okay.

Remy Butler: [To SCP-9349] We can let you stay in our barn. Our car is in there, and there’s some ritual stuff, too, but there should be enough space for you to hide.

Earnest Knight: Just stay away from our stuff.

SCP-9349: Of course. I wouldn’t dream of damaging your belongings. I appreciate your hospitality immensely. Thank you.

Remy Butler: Alright then. Follow me.

[Butler leads SCP-9349 to the barn. Despite being large, the building seems mostly unused, save a blue sedan parked near the center and a single corner set aside for thaumaturgic and ritual use. Walls near this corner are lined with shelves and crates containing a variety of occult items, including candles, cards, a large astrological calendar, and four antlered masks.]

Remy Butler: You’ll have to stay inside until morning. The door won’t stay closed unless we latch it from the outside. We don’t have a way to make it so you can come and go as you please.

SCP-9349: I understand.

Earnest Knight: We’ll have to cover the windows. We don’t want him to be seen from the outside.

Remy Butler: Good idea.

Earnest Knight: What level of heat do you have on you, exactly? Are people going to think you were stolen? Because if someone finds us hiding you and thinks we did something—

SCP-9349: I understand your trepidation, and I would not blame you at all if you were to turn me away outright due to this concern. However, I have a history of escape with which my owners are well acquainted. I believe it extremely likely that theft will be the McCalisters’ first assumption. As I said, my owners do not know of my intellect. I do not believe they will expect anyone to shelter me.

Nathan Sharp: That’s good.

Remy Butler: It just dawned on me that there isn’t a great place for him to sleep in here.

SCP-9349: It’s quite alright. I can take the floor.

Remy Butler: No, you shouldn’t have to. We don’t have straw, or anything, but… Nate, could you try to find something in the house? Even if it’s just a few blankets, that’s better than nothing.

Nathan Sharp: Alright. I’ll get what I can.

SCP-9349: That’s very kind of you. You once again have my gratitude.

Earnest Knight: I’ll stand guard outside. We need to make sure he wasn’t followed.

[Both Sharp and Knight exit the building, leaving Butler alone with SCP-9349.]

SCP-9349: Once again, I must profusely apologize for any inconvenience I have caused you.

Remy Butler: Don’t worry. Part of our job is protecting people who need protection.

SCP-9349: I see.

Remy Butler: So, I don’t mean to pry, but Earnie will get mad at me if I don’t ask this question for security reasons. How is it you can talk? I’m sorry to phrase it so bluntly—

SCP-9349: It’s quite alright. Your curiosity regarding my nature is understandable. Suffice it to say, I was transformed into my current form as punishment for… a transgression. This happened a long time ago, and the circumstances responsible will not affect you. I will not make you sit here and listen to my entire life story. Suffice it to say, I do not believe there is any chance I will ever be transformed back into a human. I have resigned myself to remaining as I am.

Remy Butler: I’m sorry to hear that. I can’t imagine what it would be like to have to pretend to be an animal and be treated like one.

SCP-9349: Thank you for saying so.

Remy Butler: What should I call you, by the way?

SCP-9349: You may call me Zachariah. That is the name my human parents gave me, long ago.

Remy Butler: Well, Zachariah, I’m Rem. The one with the bow is Earnie, and the other one is Nate. You can rest here for the night. I don’t think there’s a long-term home for you here. We’re… we’ve gone through some stuff recently, and aren’t really recruiting right now, but we can help you while you need us.

SCP-9349: Of course. That’s all I would dream of asking for.

<End Log>

Afterword: Shortly after this exchange concluded, Sharp returned from the conclave’s domicile with several blankets, from which rudimentary bedding was improvised. Shortly thereafter, the four agreed to allow SCP-9349 to stay the night in the barn and further discuss their situation in the morning.


Foreword: Once back in their domicile, the conclave’s members discussed the implications of SCP-9349’s arrival.

<Begin Log, August 18, 2024, 03:28>

Earnest Knight: Listen, Rem, I respect your decision as Sidereal, but remember that we need to be careful.

Remy Butler: I know, but I can’t turn away someone in need, and I don’t think helping him is that dangerous. We know people in Three Portlands. We just need to get someone there to come pick him up. I’ll start making calls in the morning.

Earnest Knight: Okay. Let’s just try to get him where he’s going as fast as we can. You need to remember that you can be a bit too vulnerable to sob stories. We’re still recovering from—

Remy Butler: I know. I promise he won’t stay long.

<End Log>

Addendum 9349-1: Additional Divinatory Logs

Due to the surveillance on the conclave’s residence, the Foundation was immediately alerted to the presence of SCP-9349. However, it was decided that no immediate action would be taken for fear of alerting the conclave to the failure of their anti-surveillance wards.

Foreword: The following morning, Butler, Knight, and Sharp returned to the barn to visit their guest and further discuss their situation.

<Begin Log, August 18, 2024, 10:18>

[The three members of the conclave enter the barn. As soon as the door opens, SCP-9349 bolts out. Knight quickly jumps out of its way. SCP-9349 gallops around the outside of the barn. Knight begins to follow it, but Sharp objects.]

Nathan Sharp: Earnie, give him some privacy.

Earnest Knight: Why?

Nathan Sharp: Because he’s been in the barn for eight hours and probably has to pee!

[Knight stops.]

Earnest Knight: Right. That makes sense.

[Just a moment later, SCP-9349 returns.]

SCP-9349: Apologies, friends. I have been in… relatively dire need for some hours now.

Remy Butler: It’s fine. It should have occurred to us that there’s no place for you to do that inside the barn.

SCP-9349: Oh, not at all. Even if it had occurred to you, there would have been nothing to be done about it.

Remy Butler: I guess.

Earnest Knight: With that taken care of, we should get back in the barn. Every second Zachariah spends outside is an opportunity for someone to see him.

[The four of them go back inside the barn. Knight closes the door behind them.]

Remy Butler: Alright, so, I’ve been making some calls.

SCP-9349: [Tilts head.] Calls?

Remy Butler: Phone calls. I’ve been looking for a place to send you. Somewhere behind the Veil, where you won’t have to pretend to be an animal.

SCP-9349: Oh, no. I cannot ask you to do such a thing. Sheltering me for this one night was already more than I had any right to ask of you. I could not possibly impose—

Remy Butler: No, it’s really okay. It’s a lot easier for us to arrange for you to be transported to Three Portlands than it would be for you to find your way somewhere safe.

SCP-9349: Please, my friends, I cannot possibly ask so much of you.

Nathan Sharp: Can you honestly say you have another choice? It doesn’t seem like you really have anywhere to go.

SCP-9349: I have wandered much in my many years. I can wander again.

Nathan Sharp: Won’t that just get you back in the same situation on a different farm?

[SCP-9349 does not respond.]

Remy Butler: It’s your choice. I won’t make you stay here, but please don’t leave just because you feel guilty about accepting help from us. I’ve already called a friend of ours in Three Portlands. They can come get you in about a month, then you’ll be in a free port where a talking horse can openly exist. I promise, it’s not an imposition. We sincerely want to do that for you.

SCP-9349: I see. You are exceptionally kind. Once my circumstances change, I will do what I can to repay you.

Remy Butler: If you want. We can keep you around for a month.

Earnest Knight: That’s how long we can afford to buy hay.

Remy Butler: But that’s the only part that’s at all difficult.

SCP-9349: [After a moment of silence.] Very well. I cannot reject a gift so heartfully offered. I can only imagine such compassionate souls as you would make wonderful hosts. Please, acquire no more for me than you can afford. I will graze as much as I can without being seen.

Nathan Sharp: The wards we have on this place should help keep people from noticing you. It won’t be foolproof. We’re still going to want to minimize the time you spend outside, but it’ll help.

SCP-9349: Thank you so much, friends. You are kinder than I dared hope.

Remy Butler: Speaking of hay, Nate and I are going to go into town to get some. Is there anything else you’d appreciate?

SCP-9349: My friends, you are already doing so much—

Nathan Sharp: Look, we genuinely don’t know very much about taking care of a horse. There are probably things you need that we won’t think of on our own.

SCP-9349: I suppose that is fair. A hoof pick and brush would be of use.

Nathan Sharp: Sure thing.

Earnest Knight: And you remember what you’re going to say if someone asks why we need them?

Nathan Sharp: No one’s going to ask—
 
Earnest Knight: Do you remember what you’re going to say?

Nathan Sharp: [Sighs] That we’re horse-sitting for a friend. Be vague, but mention the horse being black in order to throw people off.

Earnest Knight: Good boy.

Nathan Sharp: Hey, you’re only supposed to call me that while we’re—

Remy Butler: In any case, we’ll be back with that as soon as we can.

<End Log>

Afterword: Butler and Sharp departed soon afterward and returned later with the indicated supplies. The two of them brought the hay inside for SCP-9349 to consume, and SCP-9349 guided Sharp in the use of the aforementioned tools.


Foreword: The following evening, the conclave returned to the barn intending to use it as a ritual space.

<Begin Log, August 18, 2024, 17:24>

[Knight, Butler, and Sharp approach the barn. They are not yet inside.]

Earnest Knight: So, are we just going to do it even though there’s someone else there?

Remy Butler: I don’t think there’s any harm in him watching. It’s not like we’re going to do anything that’s supposed to be secret.

Earnest Knight: I guess. I just don’t like the idea of revealing exactly who we are.

Nathan Sharp: He’s already seen our masks. If he knows about our order, he already knows we’re a part of it.

Earnest Knight: Has it occurred to you that that could be a bad thing? We don’t know where he comes from. We don’t know how old he is, and he’s dropped a few hints that he might be pretty damn old. He might not be crazy about people like us.

Nathan Sharp: What, pagans? Gay people?

Earnest Knight: Either.

Nathan Sharp: Look, Earnie, I get it. We all know that we can’t just trust everyone. No one here has forgotten… what happened, but we have no reason to doubt this guy’s story. We verified while we were in town that the McCallisters really did lose a horse.

Earnest Knight: You did? How hard are they looking for him?

Nathan Sharp: Not seriously enough that they’re going to break into our barn or watch us like a hawk. Zach was right about them thinking he’d escaped, and they frankly don’t seem too worried about it.

Earnest Knight: That doesn’t mean he can’t screw us over if he has different intentions than he claims.

Nathan Sharp: Screw us over how? Even if Zach has the worst intentions we can imagine, what could he do? Tell someone in Three Portlands where we are?

Earnest Knight: Why not? There’s Horizon Initiative people in Three Portlan—

Nathan Sharp: He already knows we’re casters. He seems to be okay with the occult world.

Earnest Knight: Fine, but we need to be at least a bit careful. Don’t just tell him our whole life story.

[The three arrive at the barn. SCP-9349, which had been lounging on its pile of blankets, arises.]

SCP-9349: Oh, greetings. Can I do anything for you?

Nathan Sharp: No. Actually… we’re here to use the barn the way we normally would. Do some ritual stuff in that corner over there.

SCP-9349: [Glances at indicated corner.] Ah. I see. Do you need me to wait outside?

Remy Butler: No. It won’t hurt anything for you to be here. Just don’t come over and mess with what we’re doing.

SCP-9349: I wouldn’t dream of it.

[The conclave retreats to the opposite side of the barn and begins a Class-2 Theurgic Divination Ritual. At the beginning of the ritual, SCP-9349 isn’t paying the practitioners much mind, but over time, it comes to take more interest in them. About half an hour into the ritual, the three take a break while waiting for an alchemical reaction to conclude. Soon after they start making light conversation, SCP-9349 speaks up.]

SCP-9349: Excuse me, you seem to be taking a break?

Earnest Knight: Yeah. Why?

SCP-9349: My apologies. It’s rude of me to interrupt. You should proceed with your—

Remy Butler: No. It’s fine. Do you need something? Oh, do you need to go to the bathroom again? I can open the barn door.

SCP-9349: Oh, no. That’s not… actually, come to think of it, that might be a good idea, but it wasn’t my original reason for speaking. I am simply a bit curious about what you’re doing.

Remy Butler: Oh? It’s nothing much. Just a bit of theurgic divination.

SCP-9349: I see.

Nathan Sharp: Do you know what that means?

SCP-9349: Oh, well… perhaps I could benefit from an explanation, if you please.

Earnest Knight: Nate…

Nathan Sharp: Divination is magic you use to try to learn something. Where something is, what’s going to happen in the future, that kind of thing.

SCP-9349: Like… like a fortune teller?

Nathan Sharp: That’s not totally off the mark. Right now, we’re trying to help a friend escape a difficult living situation. To do that, he needs to know when an opportunity to run away is going to arise. This is information that needs to be more specific and actionable than you can get from astrology or a tarot reading. We need the exact time and date something is going to happen.

SCP-9349: I see. You mention your… order? What manner of sorcerers have I found myself the guest of, if it’s alright for me to ask?

Nathan Sharp: The Order of the Sidereal Stag.

Earnest Knight: Nate!

Nathan Sharp: He’s just curious.

SCP-9349: I apologize if I am prying—

Nathan Sharp: No. Not at all.

Remy Butler: It’s understandable that you’d be curious about the people you’re living with.

SCP-9349: I will confess, I do not believe I recognize the name you have given.

Nathan Sharp: It’s sort of a religious order crossed with a coven. The group is… this isn’t quite right, but for simplicity’s sake, you could call it “all-male.” We aspire to healthy masculinity, so there has to be some masculine component to your gender for you to be in it. It’s a social club where men and the man-adjacent with occult interests gather to protect and help each other.

SCP-9349: I see. You said it was religious as well?

Remy Butler: Somewhat. We have a patron god. Still, it’s not all about worshiping him. Think of it as a notch down from Ortothans, whose whole thing is the worship of a specific deity, but a notch up from people who just practice theurgic magic and deal with gods purely as a means to an end.

Earnest Knight: Though we do that, too.

SCP-9349: What sort of god do you serve?

Nathan Sharp: The Sidereal Stag. Like Rem said, he’s a representation of masculinity. Idealized, but healthy.

SCP-9349: Healthy?

Remy Butler: You know, non-toxic? Instead of trying to see manhood as a bunch of rules you have to follow in order to be a real man, we try to see strength, bravery, and protecting and providing for each other as virtues to strive toward.

SCP-9349: I see. I fear for my ability to please such a being.

Remy Butler: What makes you say that?

SCP-9349: I… I am not a brave man.

Nathan Sharp: Not everyone has to have the same kind of bravery. We’re not about the idea that all men have to be the same. People can accept each other’s differences while still aspiring toward the same virtues.

SCP-9349: I suppose.

Earnest Knight: Also we’re mostly gay.

[Both other members of the conclave look over at Knight.]

Earnest Knight: What? I mean, since we’re apparently giving him a crash course on the Order of the Sidereal Stag, we might as well mention that bit. [Stares directly at SCP-9349] Is it a problem?

SCP-9349: What? No. Not at all! My deepest apologies if something in my continence made it seem like I disapprove. I swear I had no such intention.

Nathan Sharp: Don’t worry. You haven’t done anything wrong. Earnie’s just being a jerk.

[Knight scoffs.]

SCP-9349: I… confess I come from a time and place that was less enlightened regarding such things, but I retain no allegiance to the faith in which I was raised, and that leaves me with no reason to disapprove.

Earnest Knight: Good.

SCP-9349: Would it be alright if I asked… which two of you—

Earnest Knight: We’re a polycule.

[SCP-9349 does not respond.]

Earnest Knight: You don’t know what that is either, do you?

SCP-9349: I’m afraid not.

Nathan Sharp: We’re all each others’ partners.

SCP-9349: [Takes a moment to reply.] I see.

Earnest Knight: Do you?

SCP-9349: I believe I do, yes. Forgive me, I seem to have done something which has led you to distrust me. I can only assure you that that was never my intention. You are the kindest souls with whom I have had the pleasure of speaking in many years. I feel only gratitude toward you.

Nathan Sharp: It’s okay. We…

Remy Butler: …We should get back to our ritual. The solvent is probably done.

SCP-9349: Very well. I shall bother you no further.

<End Log>

Afterword: Butler, Knight, and Sharp finished their ritual, wherein they called upon the Sidereal Stag to inform them when an associate’s father would next be away from home for an extended period of time. Their precise intentions with this information are unknown but not believed to be of any urgent significance.


Foreword: A few days later, Butler and Knight returned from an outing. After parking their car inside the barn, they exited the vehicle. after which they returned to the barn to speak with SCP-9349.

<Begin Log, August 20, 2024, 19:34>

[As Butler and Knight exit their vehicle, SCP-9349 is in the far corner, examining some of the ritual objects stored in the barn. Sharp is nearby, shelving jars of incense. Both Sharp and SCP-9349 turn to face the others.]

Earnest Knight: I see you’re talking an interest in our stuff?

SCP-9349: Oh, yes. Nathan was showing me what some of these artifacts were. I hope that’s alright. I find myself curious about them.

Remy Butler: It’s fine. In a minute, I might join Nate in answering your questions. First, though, I have another piece of news.

SCP-9349: Oh?

Earnest Knight: It doesn’t seem like the McCalisters are looking for you.

Nathan Sharp: What?

Remy Butler: We ran into Mrs. McCallister while we were getting your hay. Earnie… inquired as to the status of their lost horse, and she admitted she didn’t really care about finding you.

Earnest Knight: Apparently, you have ‘behavioral issues.’

SCP-9349: I see.

Earnest Knight: Did you really break a dude’s wrist when he tried to whip you?

SCP-9349: Oh. Um, that… I cannot truthfully deny that that incident occurred. I did not intend to harm him so gravely. I was simply… emotionally overwhelmed. They had not used such means to prod me prior to that point, and I feared this first use of that instrument portended a lasting decline in their treatment of me. I should not have reacted so harshly—

Earnest Knight: [Shrugs] It’s what I would have done.

SCP-9349: It is?

Earnest Knight: Of course. Anyone who tried to treat a member of my family like that, whether it was me or someone else, would be lucky if all they had to deal with was a broken wrist.

SCP-9349: I see.

Earnest Knight: Anyways, they’re not looking for you. They think you’re more trouble than you’re worth, and they don’t think they can sell you to anyone. It also helps that they think you’re near the end of your life. Apparently, you’ve been with them for longer than a horse would normally live.

SCP-9349: Ah, yes. I suppose they would be under the impression that I’m likely to die soon.

Earnest Knight: How old are you, exactly? Because you’ve alluded to being pretty damn old. Your ‘many years.’ Coming from a time and place that wasn’t okay with gay people. What’s up with all of that?

[SCP-9349 hesitates for a moment.]

Nathan Sharp: Hey, he doesn’t have to say if he doesn’t want to.

SCP-9349: Thank you, Nathan, but Earnest is right. I have been secretive about this matter. I often find that it is easier not to explain my origins. However, you three have given me reason after reason to trust you, so I shall confess, I am about six-hundred years old.

Earnest Knight: What?

Remy Butler: Six-hundred?!

SCP-9349: Yes. I was born in 1419.

Nathan Sharp: Holy shit.

SCP-9349: I do not like to mention it when I do not need to. I did less than nothing to earn my immortality. I would shed it if I could, but I cannot, just as I cannot reclaim my human form.

Nathan Sharp: Still…you’re older than the country we’re in. You’re from before Europeans even came here. Before lightbulbs. Before cameras. Before Napoleon. Before Shakespeare. Before Guns… Do you… did you know anyone I’d recognize from history?

SCP-9349: No. I never had any interactions with anyone whose name you’d recognize. I was only twenty when I gained my present form, and, as a horse, I have had no occasion to interact with the famous or notable.

Remy Butler: Still…

SCP-9349: I understand. It is a remarkable thing. I will do my best to answer any questions you have, so long as they do not concern my personal life. Just be aware of my limited perspective on worldly events. I have lived nearly my whole life in this form and spent most of that time pretending I could not speak.

Nathan Sharp: Of course. I won’t bombard you with questions, and I won’t let either of them do it, either. You don’t owe us your life story.

SCP-9349: Thank you for understanding.

<End Log>

Afterword: After this interaction, SCP-9349 began to spend more time outside of the barn due to decreased concern regarding it being seen.

Nathan Sharp: He’s a white horse. Hopefully that’ll make him easy to spot in the dark.

Earnest Knight: But not so easy that the Foundation finds him first.

The man outside the stable is grinning.

“You’re being a good boy right now, aren’t you, Dasher?” the man says. “Still remembering what happened the last time you ran?”

Screams echo in the distance, along with rhythmic incantations and the sick, wet sounds of warping flesh.

But a moment later, another voice echoes in the distance.

“Zach!”

Zachariah’s ears perk up. He knows the voice. It’s Nathan. What’s he doing here? Is he somehow trapped in this place, too? That thought horrifies Zach more than any of the visions he’s seen.

The man sneers and glances toward the door out of the stable.

“Zach!” the voice echoes again, “where are you?”

“They must be worried you’ll expose them when you’re captured,” the man says. “Hopefully they don’t keep looking for you too long. It would be a shame if they got caught looking for you, even after the sacrifice you made.”

“Zach, please, where are you?”

“I’m sure they’ll give up on you soon enough. They’ll be safe, if you stay in here.”

“Zach!”

“You’re safe, too, after a fashion. You can’t ruin anyone’s life from in here. No more wives will weep for you. No more sons will live and die with only the story of a coward to call ‘father.’”

“Zach, please, I need you to hear me. I… I don’t want to lose you! And… even if you don’t want to stay with us, I need to at least know you’re okay. Please, tell me you’re okay!”

“Nate…” came the horse’s voice, weakly.

“Zach? Zach, was that you?”

“I wouldn’t answer if I were you. It’d be cruel, to taunt him with your voice.”

“But he can hear me.”

“Yes! Yes, I can hear you! Where are you?”

Every movement makes Zachariah’s muscles explode with pain. By all rights, that should keep him pinned to the ground, but, somehow, he plants his hoof in front of him and begins struggling to his feet.

The boy in the cage is still screaming outside, his pained cries warping into neighs as his body is twisted and changed. Zachariah does not allow himself to listen to those screams. He forces himself to listen to Nate’s voice as he pushes himself upright.

“Zach, please? I love you. I want to see you again. Please, wherever you are, hear me.”

Zachariah pushes himself to his feet. He stumbles but does not fall.

“He doesn’t know what’s good for him. He’s even stupider than you are.”

Zachariah sticks his head through the gap over the stable’s door and looks toward the voices. He doesn’t see Nate, only the mages, the cage, and the boy inside it, half-way morphed into a horse.

“See that?” the man says. “That’s what happened last time you ran away.”

“Nate…”

Then, another voice. Rem. “Zach, we hear you too.”

And a third. Earnie. Their warden. “I’m sorry,” he cries. “I’m sorry if I ever made it seem like I wanted you to leave. I don’t. I promise I never did. I was afraid, but I want you to be with us, always.”

“You’ll destroy them.”

As if the man had not spoken, Zachariah rears back and slams his hooves into the stall door. He runs out into the stable’s main hall. The cage, and the morphing boy inside it, are still there, but so is the cool, gentle forest. Above the blood red moon, three bright stars twinkle. The man is also outside, dressed as a mage. The boy in the cage starts screaming again, and Zachariah’s eyes find him. He takes a single step back.

“That’s right, you know better than to run.”

“Zach, where are you?”

The horse looks at the man. “They want me…”

“So, what if they do?”

Zachariah takes another step back. The Entity raises his hand, which begins to glow with crackling, red power. Zachariah’s gaze lingers on him. He closes his eyes.

“I’m sorry, Lenore. I wish I could have come back to you, too. I hope… I hope you understood that I tried.”

Zachariah charges forward, directly toward the cage and the voices beyond it. The man utters an incantation and shoots an enormous jet of fire out of his hand. The heat engulfs Zachariah. His flesh sizzles with burning pain. Every cell in his body is screaming at him to turn back, to run, to hide, but he charges through the fire until he reaches the threshold of the building.

He doesn’t even see the man until he’s already run into him, his weight and speed knocking him over. He charges into the wooded night, passing through the mages, the cage, and the boy inside, as if they weren’t there.

Every speck of hair on him has been scorched away. Every inch of his skin is burning, but he still runs into the cool night.

Just a moment later, he trips. He falls forward, and begins to roll across the ground, spinning and tumbling, but not hurting, as if the cool night grass were somehow soothing his burning skin.

[Sharp, Knight, and Butler are whispering to one another.]
