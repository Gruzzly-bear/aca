I circled back to the conductor today. Asked him flat-out: “Do you remember the name Shauna Cross?” He paused. Brow furrowed like I just asked him a question about calculus. I stood around thinking something would click for him. But no. He just gave me this dull shrug and said it wasn’t ringing a bell.

I tracked down that Clark guy who should’ve been on the same car as her. Same question. Face deep in thought. Nothing. I asked him about the seat and whether he ever saw a woman with a black tee and a red duffel bag. Another stupid look. Then: “Oh yeah, 1G? Naw, it was empty.”

Of course it was. Of fucking course it was.

Empty. He said it like it was obvious. Like I was the crazy one.

I could scream.

I miss when I used to get excited about things like this. Before it felt like I was the only one still giving a fuck about anything. Am I the only one who actually cares when someone disappears?

But it's fine. When nobody has the answer, I'll just do what I always do. Go where no one bothers to check.

+ I have to figure this out..
- Or so I wish

I visited a couple Cross residences today, but only one of them had a Charlie. It was a quiet little detached house out in the suburbs. A woman with a tired face answered the door. It was just her and her son, Charlie. Fifteen years old, plays soccer. When I got there, he was sitting in the living room playing something on the TV. Came off as a little stuck-up when I tried to talk to him.

There was a photo on the wall of the two of them. It looked so obvious that there was supposed to be a third person in the frame, but there wasn't.

I asked the woman about Shauna. She scrunched her face up and said that maybe it was her great-great-grandma's name or something. They keep doing that. Getting a weird look on their face like they have the faintest memory, then snarkily saying that they don't know what you're talking about.

I asked her again.

“Are you sure you’ve never had a daughter? Maybe from a different father?”

"What kind of question is that? I know how many kids I have."

She looked really disgusted with my question so I just left. Sat in my car for a long time. Read the notebook again. Read that line about Charlie going to the store. Same name. Looked further back in the notebook. Same age. His birthday was in April. But no memory. No record.

I checked the nearby hospitals. No birth records for a Shauna Cross in the province. Not the next one over either. No childhood immunizations. No dental forms. I called her college board. Nothing. It's like I’m reverse engineering a ghost. But she’s not even supposed to be an unsolved case. She's supposed to be nothing.

I’ve been pulling long nights. Trying to backtrack her entire existence. Every person I talk to, every place I call is just another dead end. I talked to a bunch of hospitals multiple times, but once, one of them said I had never talked to them before when I mentioned it. Am I starting to lose track? I looked at my call log, and some of the calls I knew I took I KNEW THAT I FUCKING TOOK I TALKED TO THESE PEOPLE, they weren't there. I don't know what's going on.

..

Reality is editing itself faster than I can document it. Reorganizing itself to close the hole she left behind. But I still have the notebook.

I haven’t slept in two days. I can't let this notebook out of my sight.
