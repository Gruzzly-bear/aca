This article is maintained and operated by the Foundation Academic Service for use exclusively for training and Academic purposes.

This article is strictly hypothetical, and may be used by the following departments for training means:

  • Department of Containment
  • Explanation and Research Department
  • Foundation Academic Service
  • Recordkeeping and Information Security Administration
  • Recruitment Division, Department for Human Resources

If you are reading this as part of your examination, please write down answers to any questions with pen and paper, which will be provided.

Containment Class: PENDING1
Disruption Class: PENDING
Risk Class: PENDING

SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES


SCP-8287 is to remain unknown by staff which hold a clearance level lower than Clearance Level 3 “SECRET”.

Strange to use a secret one for this.
Immersion ruined…


It is to remain within the pre-approved Foundation Academic Service Computer system stored at University-12's Information Technology Laboratories in Geneva, Switzerland.

Staff being transferred departments or trained in understanding articles are to believe that the anomaly is solely part of the SCP-Foundation’s training program, as an example of an SCP Article in the database. The article must be used for training, as removal from the program results in a shutdown of the Foundation's article documenting system.2

Childish to shut down the system…

The article is not to be edited, and the anomaly is not to be communicated without permission from Foundation Academic Service management and Site Manager Jacobs.

In the event that the subject has gained awareness of their own existence, the device housing SCP-8287 at the time is to be shut down.

DESCRIPTION


SCP-8287 is an anomalous series of text boxes present within the article labelled with the same name, originally used for testing purposes. The anomaly responds to statements and questions made throughout the training article, with regular appearances utilising text boxes, often in bright yellow.

The anomaly has sporadically appeared alphabetically on Foundation Academic Service computers numbered AB-12-44 through to XS-36-01.

So the anomaly believes it's in
training..?
How does that work?
Shoddy writing from the training
department in my opinion.

The anomaly does not seem to be actively aware that it is within an article, and the memory of the anomaly is wiped upon the shutdown of the computer it is active on at the time.

SCP-8287 has been described as "tempting to respond to", due to its highly sarcastic nature according to first-hand accounts of researchers, examiners and trainees alike.

INITIAL DISCOVERY REPORT

On 04/12/1991, SCP-8287 was first observed on the Foundation's training computers, on a mock article of the same name. The article would be about a conscious article, however it did not initially contain any anomaly.

Guiying Cai, a recruit from Peking University, would be the first to notice the anomaly and bring it to site authorities attention, namely Simon Plunkett, the invigilator for his examination. It is, however, expected the anomaly may have occurred earlier, albeit unreported.

ADDENDUM-1


As part of a refurbishment of the SCP Training program, SCP-8287 would be shortly removed from the system, and moved to property of the Recordkeeping and Information Security Administration (RAISA).
At 04:45 on ██/██/1996, the file containing SCP-8287 would be formally removed from the program. At 05:00, all RAISA files would become unreadable, with the exception of SCP-8287.

I AM NOT REAL
HOW DO YOU KNOW YOU ARE


RAISA would quickly revert SCP-8287's file back to the Foundation Academic Service, and all damage would be reverted immediately. SCP-8287 is to remain within the training program in future.
