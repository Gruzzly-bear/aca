SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES:

Foundation webcrawlers are to monitor the internet for instances of SCP-9095. Located instances are to be taken down once data from applications submitted to the "Victims of VKTM Memorial Fund" has been extracted. The SCP Foundation's Division of Accounting is to be advised of each these instances and has been instructed to flag and report any suspicious or unplanned account withdrawals.

DESCRIPTION:

SCP-9095 is an anomalous website, titled "Victims of VKTM Memorial Foundation", presumably originating from and maintained by Vikander-Kneed Technical Media (GOI-5889). SCP-9095 can be taken offline with no unusual difficulty, but, since its discovery, a new iteration has reliably been identified within 5-10 days of removal. No infohazardous properties have been observed, but there is evidence that accessing the site may cause an individual to become more open to suggestion, more trusting of the site's legitimacy2, and more likely to share it with others; therefore suggesting a mild cognitohazardous or memetic effect.

The site purports to act as a memorial for all individuals who have been, quote, "touched in unwanted and negative ways" by VKTM (referred to as a "Victim of VKTM" within SCP-9095 instances). However, the criteria used to define an aforementioned "Victim of VKTM" is notably vague, and its breadth unnecessarily wide; to the point at which it encapsulates approximately 92.91% of the human population. A list of these individuals is apparently included, yet allegedly cannot be displayed due to "device limitation issues".

In addition, a "Victims of VKTM Memorial Fund" is also advertised on the site, stating that all individuals meeting the criteria to be considered a "Victim of VKTM" are entitled to compensation. This compensation is known to range anywhere between $1.25 and $1,100,100.50, with the amount received having no correlation with the severity of the claim. Despite the illegitimate appearance of both the SCP-9095 website and its online advertisements, testing has determined that those who fill out the included application form do, in fact, receive compensation within the 1-9 business day waiting period. Notably, recipients (designated SCP-9095-1) will still receive compensation, despite the application containing no field for the input of an address, nor one for the input of payment information.

In all known instances, compensation received by SCP-9095-1 instances had been directly transferred from at least one SCP Foundation bank account.
