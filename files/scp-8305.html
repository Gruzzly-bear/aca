SCP-8305.

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8305 is to be contained in a safe storage locker at Site-19. Said locker should only be accessible to Level 3 personnel for testing purposes. Any researcher found trying to vacate SCP-8305 off-site will have their Foundation employment terminated and will be investigated for leads towards GoI-012.1

Description: SCP-8305 is an antique artisanal silver fork. A message saying "from P to I" is inscribed beneath the beetle at the bottom of the handle. SCP-8305 shows no anomalous characteristics outside of use. Once SCP-8305 has been stabbed into an organic subject and is twisted at least 180 degrees, all of the flesh inside the subject will liquefy. An outer husk of 2.5 cm is spared from this anomalous effect and appears to be unaffected. This husk creates a barrier in which the residual matter can be kept within the subject. Subjects who ingest the liquefied subject experience euphoria followed by intoxication, similar to that of alcohol. These effects last longer depending on how much matter the subject consumed.

Discovery: SCP-8305 was purchased on 20██/12/01 by Mobile Task Force Mu-3 ("Highest Bidders") on an anomalous marketplace supported by GoI-012. Attempts by the Foundation to contact and track the seller of SCP-8305 remains unsuccessful to this day.

Addendum 8305 - B: Operation R.S.R.B

The following addendum is in relation to Operation R.S.R.B3 where Mu-3 worked to capture and contain PoI-11098.

GoI-012 gave the location of PoI-11098 soon after they struck an accord with the Foundation. Upon receiving GoI-012's information, Mu-3 began to mobilize towards PoI-11098's location in Chickasaw County, Mississippi, USA. The task force was ordered to capture PoI-11098 alive for interrogation.

Unfortunately, PoI-11098 was aware of the Foundation's attempt to capture them. This resulted in the deaths of 4 task force members as well as PoI-11098, who was terminated during the altercation through the use of a SRA.4 PoI-11098 was later identified as ██████ ████, a lawyer currently residing in the region on business. Investigation yielded fruitful results, which will be placed in Addendum C. Standard disinformation protocols took place thereon afterwards.

Due to PoI-11098's termination, the Foundation refused to hand over SCP-8305 back into GoI-012's possession. Ever since then, GoI-012 has been attempting to bribe Foundation staff in exchange to receive SCP-8305.

Addendum 8305 - C: Investigation Results

The following addendum is in relation to the investigation that took place in PoI-11098's apartment, as previously mentioned.

Initial forensic analysis of unidentified prints and hairs yielded no results. The following list of items were confiscated for investigation and have been thoroughly examined.

Object Description Results
Laptop A modern laptop. The inside of the keyboard was filthy. Revealed information regarding the existence of several anomalous markets alongside connections to other individuals behind the veil.
Phone A small phone. The protective screen is cracked along the edges and there is no SIM card. Contains conversations with several clients who PoI-11098 worked for. One anonymous number deemed to be connected to GoI-012 directly.5
Stack of Papers A stack of printed documents. Some are stained with coffee and written on with ink pen. Documents relate to large transfers of cash in banks in the following states: New York, New Jersey, Kentucky, Nevada, █████████, Florida and Los Angeles. Writing at the top of the pages are inscribed with I, C or A.
Coffee Mug An anomalous coffee mug. Contains endless hot coffee. Stored in a security locker at Site-███.
