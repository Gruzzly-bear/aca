Special Containment Procedures:

The Foundation has been cooperating with the Manna Charitable Foundation (MCF) to manage and mitigate the effects of SCP-9075 in Postojna, Slovenia.

Foundation agents embedded in Postojna have taken on cover identities as MCF employees. Personnel are to monitor residents for any indications of SCP-9075-A sightings and respond accordingly.1 Postojna Cave has been permanently closed to the public with guards stationed at the current entrance to prevent any unauthorized access. Class-V full-body hazmat gear is required for any personnel entering the cave.

A pediatric clinic jointly run by the Foundation and the MCF has been established in the city to identify instances of SCP-9075-B. Any discovered instances, along with their families, are to be taken immediately into Foundation care at Facility L27, with cover stories disseminated to the community to explain their disappearance.

SCP-9075-A instances are to be left alone unless found outside Postojna Cave. Instances are to be captured and relocated inside the cave; neutralization of instances is only permitted if such relocation is not possible. Agents are to notify researchers immediately if a new variety of SCP-9075-A is discovered.

The Department of Herpetology’s batrachology division is to monitor olm populations outside the nation of Slovenia for any anomalous changes in physiology. Any such changes must be reported to the SCP-9075 research team immediately.

MTF-Iota-90 (“Spelunk Hazard”) has been assigned to explore the entirety of Postojna Cave. Such exploration is to continue until the source of SCP-9075 is identified.

Description:

SCP-9075 is the collective designation for the various ongoing phenomena affecting Postojna, Slovenia and its residents in the years since the 2077 Kresnik Chemical Plant Disaster. These phenomena center around the potential contamination of the town’s water supply and are believed to originate from one of the locations most severely impacted by the chemical plant’s total destruction — the depths of Postojna Cave.

Background Dossier:

[Note: the following dossier was prepared by Dr. Mirjam van Rijn to provide important context and background regarding SCP-9075. Personnel newly assigned to the anomaly must familiarize themselves with the information presented below before commencing their work.]

—The Kresnik Chemical Plant

The Kresnik Chemical Plant was constructed in 2072 by the BadenForm Corporation.2 Located near the Pivka River on the outskirts of Postojna, the plant was designed to produce industrial chemicals used by a number of corporate clients — their largest known client being oil and gas conglomerate ExxArc.3

In particular, one of the plant’s primary functions was the synthesis of various coolant and insulation fluids containing high amounts of polychlorinated biphenyls (PCB). These chemicals are known to be highly toxic and possess carcinogenic properties. Additionally, they easily bind to sediments, soils, and organic tissue, allowing them to persist for long periods in environments to which they are exposed.

An engineer affixing a warning label to a transformer indicating the presence of PCB. Application of such warnings has since fallen out of standard industry practice.

Despite the risks and dangers, the use of PCB in the industry had become commonplace following the repeal of the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants in 2058. Their legality was not enough to assuage the concerns of the residents of Postojna, however, and the Kresnik Plant was met with considerable opposition due to its proposed location that would place it not only along the Pivka River, but above Postojna Cave.

Postojna Cave is the second longest cave system in Slovenia, with a total length of approximately 24 km. The cave was formed by the Pivka River, which flows into the cave system from the north before fully disappearing underground. Notably, the last four kilometers at the deepest section of the cave are entirely underwater.4 The cave is especially important to the Postojna community, as the groundwater serves as a primary water source for the area. Additionally, the cave is a notable habitat for Slovenia’s sole remaining population of olms.

—The Olm

The olm (Proteus anguinus) is an aquatic salamander that has adapted to thrive in subterranean environments. Members of the species possess long serpentine bodies with short limbs. Their thin skin largely lacks any pigmentation, giving their bodies a pale, pink-white color. Additionally, as a result of the perpetual darkness of their subterranean habitat, olms are entirely blind with undeveloped eyes that are covered by a layer of skin. To make up for their lack of sight, they are equipped with other sensory organs that allow them to detect alternative stimuli such as vibrations, electrical charges, and the presence of organic chemicals.

Photograph of a Postojna Cave olm taken prior to the 2077 Kresnik Plant Disaster.

Similar to certain other species of salamanders such as the axolotl5 and the common mudpuppy,6 olms exhibit neoteny; that is, they do not undergo metamorphosis like most other amphibians and retain their larval traits even into adulthood. As such, olms remain entirely aquatic with external gills that protrude out from the back of the head.

Historically native to the region bordering the northern and eastern Adriatic Sea, by 2054 the olm had become critically endangered due to increasing groundwater pollution across Europe with Postojna Cave serving as the only remaining habitat within Slovenia. The decision to construct the chemical plant above this habitat was met with considerable resistance, though this ultimately failed to stop BadenForm from proceeding with its plans. The plant was eventually completed in 2072.

—Period of Operation (2072-2077)

Little is known about the plant’s operations during the period it was active. The plant’s manager, Victor Cho, enforced a policy of strict secrecy among his employees and aggressively opposed any attempted inquiries regarding the plant from outside entities. Cho additionally took on an adversarial tone with Postojna’s residents, and complaints about the plant consistently went unanswered. The relationship with the town was tense in general, as, rather than hiring residents from Postojna, the company staffed the plant purely with pre-existing employees who lived on-site.7

Logo for BadenForm Corporation, which was displayed prominently around the plant and on all employee uniforms.

Additionally, as the plant’s period of operation continued, residents began to notice deceased olms washing up from the cave with gradually increasing frequency. Groundwater pollution by the plant was attributed as the likely cause, though Cho and BadenForm vigorously denied these claims. Indeed, testing of the water in the cave failed to reveal the presence of any harmful chemicals, leaving the cause of death for the olms unexplained. Residents were largely unconvinced of BadenForm’s innocence, however, and the plant endured mounting opposition from the community up until the day it was entirely destroyed.

—The Kresnik Plant Disaster

At 06:02 on 2077/02/09, Postojna residents were met with the sound of a loud explosion coming from the Kresnik Plant’s location. First responders arriving at the scene discovered the area covered in flames with no trace of the plant itself. The explosion was apparently large enough to break apart the ground beneath the building, causing the entirety of the plant to collapse into the cave below. The immediate consequence of the cave-in was the mass seepage of the various chemicals produced by BadenForm — including the PCB mixtures — into the cave’s water system — contaminating the town’s primary water supply.

Due to the immediate risk of toxic fumes from the explosion migrating to Postojna proper, the Slovenian government promptly declared an emergency and ordered the town to evacuate. An environmental assessment quickly commenced that found the water systems had become heavily contaminated with PCB.

The effects of the spill became rapidly apparent, with the wildlife in and along the Pivka River dying in droves. The most notable casualty of the disaster was the population of olms, which was effectively wiped out, their corpses washing up from the cave en masse for several days following the spill.

The species would be formally declared extinct in Slovenia two weeks after the disaster.

—Response

The disaster received moderate international attention, with various non-governmental organizations — the Manna Charitable Foundation8 chief among them — arriving to provide aid to those displaced by the disaster. An assessment of the cave discovered additional collapses — one notably located near the main entrance — rendering much of the cave inaccessible.

With the entirety of the plant having disappeared into the depths of the cave, all employees present at the time of the disaster, including Victor Cho, were declared missing, presumed deceased. In the face of widespread criticism, BadenForm pledged to remediate all damages caused by the spill. The corporation was soon joined by ExxArc with CEO DeForest Morgan personally vowing to use his resources to expedite all clean-up efforts and provide compensation to all residents of Postojna. The move was widely celebrated, and after only six months of clean-up efforts, tests of the water supply consistently showed a lack of harmful chemicals in the water. Residents were cleared to move back to the town shortly after.

BadenForm suffered a severe drop in stock price immediately following the disaster. Notably, during this clean-up period, ExxArc was able to successfully negotiate the total acquisition of BadenForm. It is now formally a subsidiary of ExxArc.

Discovery:

Because the circumstances surrounding the Kresnik Plant Disaster did not indicate the existence of any anomalous elements, the Foundation concluded the matter to be beyond its purview. On 2080/07/09, however, representatives from the Manna Charitable Foundation contacted Foundation liaisons regarding supposed recent anomalous developments in Postojna. Dr. Mirjam van Rijn, a Foundation biochemist, was instructed to travel to Postojna and meet with MCF representative Dr. Tabitha Nkosi. Dr. Nkosi is a medical practitioner and one of a handful of MCF employees remaining in Postojna to help provide continued additional aid to those affected by the plant disaster.

Meeting Log — 2080/07/10

<Begin Log>

Dr. Nkosi: Thank you for coming in, Dr. van Rijn. We’re so happy you’re here to help.

Dr. van Rijn: Well, it would depend on what exactly you need our help with.

Dr. Nkosi: Right, of course. Sorry about that.

Dr. van Rijn: It’s fine, just start from the beginning. You believe something anomalous is happening here in Postojna?

Dr. Nkosi: Oh, without a doubt. Putting it simply, we believe there’s something wrong with the water.

Dr. van Rijn: The water?

Dr. Nkosi: The groundwater, specifically. From the cave. We think it might still be contaminated.

Dr. van Rijn: You mean from the chemical spill? I thought that all got cleaned up.

[Dr. Nkosi nods.]

Dr. Nkosi: So did we. And all our tests say so too. Every sample we’ve taken comes back clear as crystal. Not a trace of contaminants.

Dr. van Rijn: I don’t see the problem; isn’t that a good thing?

Dr. Nkosi: I mean, it would be, but… it seems like people are getting sick anyway.

Dr. van Rijn: Could you please elaborate?

Dr. Nkosi: Well, we’ve got people coming into our clinics with the exact kinds of symptoms you would expect from PCB poisoning. The sores and the lesions, of course. But we’re also seeing some really bad liver damage and messed up hormone levels. Obviously this shouldn’t be possible, since the water’s coming back clean. It’s all gotten us thinking there may be something unnatural, maybe even deceptive, going on.

[Dr. van Rijn nods.]

Dr. van Rijn: Okay, I think I get it now. You’re unsure if ExxArc really did clean up the spill like they say they did.

Dr. Nkosi: Do you think it’s possible? That they might’ve done something to mask the presence of the chemicals? Like, they’re still in the water, but some magic or something is keeping us from detecting them?

Dr. van Rijn: I wouldn’t put it past them, I’ll put it that way.

[She leans forward.]

Dr. van Rijn: How about this? We’ll have some of our people take a look at the water as well as your patients. We’ll see if our own tests can find anything useful. We can also take a look at our intel on ExxArc; see if there’s anything there that might be of help.

Dr. Nkosi: That would be great! We’ve tried reaching out to ExxArc ourselves — multiple times, actually — but they just keep giving us the cold shoulder.

Dr. van Rijn: Not surprising; that’s just how they are, apparently. But I think we have enough to go on for now, as far as next steps go. Unless there was anything else you wanted to talk about?

[Pause.]

Dr. Nkosi: Well, I guess there is one more thing…

[She shifts her weight to one side.]

Dr. Nkosi: I haven’t been able to verify it for myself yet, but I’ve gotten a few reports of olm sightings by Postojna residents over the past two weeks or so. Dead olms, to be clear. Supposedly they’ve been washing up from the cave.

Dr. van Rijn: Olms? Didn’t the spill kill them all off?

Dr. Nkosi: As far as we know, yes. We haven’t seen any of these corpses for ourselves. And our ecologists have thoroughly explored the cave several times — at least the portion anyone can get to. No one saw even a hint of wildlife inside.

Dr. van Rijn: So where could the corpses have come from? Did any of these witnesses take a sample?

Dr. Nkosi: Well, I asked, and it seems one of them tried. But he says something strange stopped him from doing so.

Dr. van Rijn: What?

Dr. Nkosi: Before he could grab it, it was like the corpse dissolved into the water.

<End Log>

Addendum 9075-01:

Foundation chemists collected samples of groundwater from the vicinity of Postojna (including from within Postojna Cave). Similar to the MCF, their tests were unable to detect the presence of any toxic contaminants. Still, medical evaluations of affected residents found their symptoms to be consistent with those resulting from exposure to PCB. The Foundation has since commenced cooperation with the MCF to provide medical treatment for these symptoms.

Foundation representatives reached out to ExxArc regarding this discrepancy and received the following message:

To our friends at the SCP Foundation:

We are saddened to learn of the misfortune that has befallen the wonderful people of Postojna, and we offer our deepest sympathies. However, we would like to stress that ExxArc pledged to remediate all damages resulting from the 2077 chemical spill and by all conceivable metrics, we have thoroughly exceeded these obligations. ExxArc is fully committed to maintaining healthy and robust standards of living for all life on our beautiful planet.

Any statements indicating otherwise we shall consider to be defamatory in nature. And we will respond accordingly.

We send our thoughts and prayers to the citizens of Postojna, which we believe should be sufficient on our part to address your concerns.

Kindest regards,
Cecilia Wollensky-Huesca
Assistant to DeForest Morgan, CEO
ExxArc

Following conferral with the Department of External Affairs, the decision was reached to abstain from further contact with ExxArc in regards to SCP-9075 for the foreseeable future.9

After a period of negotiations, the Foundation agreed to assist the MCF in implementing a water-filtration system for the town’s water supply that made use of various anomalous Foundation technologies. Due to the inability to detect PCB levels, the efficacy of the system could not be measured with precision, though Postojna did see a significant reduction in PCB exposure symptoms over the following weeks.

Interview Log — 2080/07/15

Interviewer: Dr. Mirjam van Rijn
Subject: Jakob Avsenik, 49
Notes: Subject is a Postojna resident who claims to have discovered and attempted to grab an olm corpse outside Postojna Cave the week prior. Interview is conducted in English.10

<Begin Log>

Dr. van Rijn: Good evening, Mr. Avsenik. How are you feeling today?

[Avsenik shrugs.]

Avsenik: Eh, could be worse. The pills you doctors11 prescribed have got me feeling a little less like shit. Just a little, mind you. Still feel like shit.

Dr. van Rijn: Well, I’m glad to hear you’re feeling at least somewhat better.

Avsenik: Why did you really call me here, doctor? I didn’t think I was due for another check-up this soon.

Dr. van Rijn: Oh, well… I was actually hoping you could tell me a little about what you saw last week.

[Avsenik sighs.]

Avsenik: I see, you want to know about that damned salamander.

Dr. van Rijn: If you don’t mind.

[Pause.]

Avsenik: Fine, I guess I’ll bite. It was last Tuesday night. I was walking home from the pub after a miserable night watching Olimpija fumble every shot on goal like a bunch of fuckwits. It had been raining that evening, so I thought I’d take the short way home, the one that takes me past the front of that godforsaken cave.

Former entrance to Postojna Cave.

Dr. van Rijn: You don’t sound particularly fond of the cave.

[Avsenik shrugs.]

Avsenik: Never really cared for it even before it swallowed that factory and fucked up our water. Still didn’t care for it much that night either. I had my eyes on the ground trying to get past as quick as I could, when I saw it lying on the ground.

Dr. van Rijn: An olm?

Avsenik: Right away I could tell it was dead. I remember seeing loads of corpses washing up the day of the spill. It was just like that.

Avsenik: Well, almost just like that.

Dr. van Rijn: What do you mean?

Avsenik: I remember always being so put off by how pale those little fuckers were. Creeped me out like nothing else. But the one I saw that night had this… nasty reddish color to it. I took a few steps towards it and saw that the color was coming from these deep-red veins running all across its body. I know those things were always thin-skinned — practically translucent — but this was something else. Those veins were bulging. Like they were ready to burst any second.

Dr. van Rijn: What do you think happened to the corpse?

Avsenik: Been trying not to think about it. Especially not after I tried to pick it up.

Dr. van Rijn: So you did try to grab it.

[Avsenik sighs.]

Avsenik: I have no idea what the fuck I was thinking. That thing looked so unnatural, I guess I wanted to make sure it was actually there — that I wasn’t hallucinating or something. I mean, these salamanders are supposed to be long dead, right?

Dr. van Rijn: What happened when you tried to grab it?

[Avsenik shrugs.]

Avsenik: The moment my skin made contact, the corpse just… dissolved. Right into the water. Like it was never there.

Dr. van Rijn: It dissolved?

Avsenik: Look, I know it sounds crazy, but I know what I saw. I have no reason to lie about seeing one of them. I’ve always hated those things.

Dr. van Rijn: Yes, I’ve kind of been getting that impression. If you don’t mind, why exactly do you feel that way?

Avsenik: I don’t know, I… I guess it’s the way they look. Before the cave closed down, there used to be a vivarium in there that had a bunch of those salamanders. My parents would take me to see them when I was a kid, and… looking at them just made my skin crawl. I mean, they’re like these long slimy worms, with this nasty fleshy color to them. Like human skin. Pale human skin.

[Avsenik laughs.]

Avsenik: You know what we call them, right? The Slovene term for the little things? “Človeška ribica.” You know what that directly translates to in English?

[Dr. van Rijn nods.]

Dr. van Rijn: “Human fish.”

[Avsenik laughs again.]

Avsenik: I think that’s what I always hated most about them. That we would have anything in common with such a loathsome creature. I used to get these awful nightmares, where our town would suddenly fall into the cave and trap us there, and after years of living in the darkness, we’d start to lose our eyes too. Become all disgusting and slimy like them.

[Avsenik shrugs.]

Avsenik: Hell, thinking about it almost makes me feel bad for those bastards at the chemical plant.

<End Log>

Addendum 9075-04:

Incident Report — 2080/07/30: At 14:57, Postojna Hospital admitted Anthony Haverford to its intensive care unit. Haverford, an English college student who was backpacking through the region with friends, was suffering from acute liver failure and muscle spasms, and showed numerous lesions on his hands and arms. He was pronounced dead at 15:49.

Toxicology reports indicated high concentrations of various potent neurotoxins and dioxins in Haverford’s bloodstream.

Haverford’s companions insisted he was feeling fine until he collapsed around 14:32 outside a convenience store in Postojna. Foundation personnel at the hospital questioned the group about their activities that day; of note was that shortly before the group arrived in the town, Haverford reportedly found what he described as a “funny little lizard” crawling through some mud. Haverford supposedly picked up the animal and told a friend to take a photo of it with their phone.

Image recovered from Anthony Haverford’s phone.

A task force was quickly dispatched to the location where the photo was taken. Investigation of the area eventually led to the discovery of a small, previously unknown entrance to Postojna Cave obscured by the overgrowth. The task force was able to enter the opening and found themselves in the larger section of the cave that had been rendered inaccessible as a result of the Kresnik Plant Disaster.

Cursory exploration of the cave promptly led to the discovery of SCP-9075-A.





“Far from feeling surprise that some of the cave-animals should be very anomalous…as is the case with blind Proteus…I am only surprised that more wrecks of ancient life have not been preserved, owing to the less severe competition to which the scanty inhabitants of these dark abodes will have been exposed.”

-Charles Darwin, On the Origin of Species





SCP-9075-A are various organisms living within the previously inaccessible portion of Postojna Cave that all bear a general resemblance to the olm (Proteus anguinus). This section of the cave has become highly saturated with toxic substances in the time since the chemical spill. As such, researchers have theorized these organisms comprise different anomalous subspecies of olm that have drastically adapted in different ways to the current conditions in the cave.

MTF-Iota-90 has been tasked with exploring the cave and logging observed subspecies. A description of the subspecies identified so far is included below.

Subspecies Designation Description
SCP-9075-A1 Mostly resembles a non-anomalous olm, but with the ability to secrete large quantities of a highly toxic mucus.12
SCP-9075-A2 Maintains similar body width to non-anomalous olms, but possesses far greater length in excess of 5 m.13
SCP-9075-A3 Veins and arteries are considerably larger in diameter than other subspecies; appear to bulge out from underneath skin. Additionally demonstrates the ability to “melt” into a liquid state through a currently unknown mechanism.14
SCP-9075-A4 Possesses 16 additional pairs of limbs all spaced equidistantly between the forelimbs and hind limbs.
SCP-9075-A5 Does not appear to have a head nor gills. Means of satiation and respiration are unknown.

[Dr. van Rijn nods. She continues moving.]

[She approaches a pair of large stalagmites that give the appearance of pillars. As she passes between them, a faint noise becomes audible off to her left. She turns and sees what appears to be a writhing mass. A closer look reveals a mound of pulsating human bodies, seemingly fused together in a chaotic manner. Pained moans emanate from open mouths which emit continuous torrents of viscous mucus.]

[The faces have no eyes, nor skin covering the sockets. There are only empty black cavities. Nothing visible inside them.]

[Dr. van Rijn slowly circles the mound. Some of the bodies are wearing uniforms bearing the BadenForm Logo. After a moment, she stops in front of one body. It is former plant manager Victor Cho. His face is low to the ground, someone else’s foot fused to his temple. Whether Cho can sense Dr. van Rijn’s presence is uncertain; even so, Cho’s moans seem to become sounds of weeping just moments after she stops.]

[Dr. van Rijn stands still facing Cho for roughly thirty seconds. Then, without a word, she returns to walking, leaving the mound behind.]

[Her path becomes a gentle incline, which gradually increases in steepness as she continues. The walk eventually turns into a climb, but Dr. van Rijn does not slow her pace. Numerous SCP-9075-A instances follow alongside her on both sides. Soon, a crest in the hill becomes visible.]

[She continues her climb.]

[She reaches the top.]

[In front of her is a massive pit.]

[She looks down. It is dark inside. But there is something moving within. A deep, slow breathing is audible.]

Dr. van Rijn: Father.

Dr. van Rijn: Mother.

Dr. van Rijn: Whatever you are.

Dr. van Rijn: I see you.

Dr. van Rijn: I’ve grown up.

Dr. van Rijn: And I think we should talk.

[A pair of shining red orbs appear in the dark below. Its eyes are wide open.]

[The pale dragon of flesh raises its head, its gaze fixed on Dr. van Rijn.]

[The dragon roars.]

[Dr. van Rijn laughs softly.]

[The video suddenly cuts out.]

<End Log>

A thorough search of Postojna Cave commenced shortly after receipt of the file. Exploration teams were unable to locate the chamber depicted in the video or the entities within. Their whereabouts remain unknown.









Addendum 9075-17:

On 2081/09/19, the Foundation was alerted to an explosion at the BadenForm Corporation’s global headquarters in Munich, Germany. Reports indicate that the explosion occurred at 03:42 local time and originated from within the center of the building. The explosion was promptly followed by the sudden formation of a massive sinkhole directly beneath the structure, which pulled the entirety of the building into the earth.

One witness present near the scene described the explosion as “a gigantic blood-red flame.”

Two days after the initial explosion, the Foundation was able to acquire a video recording from another witness to the event. The video depicts a humanoid figure at the scene of the incident immediately following the explosion. This figure — designated Person of Interest 989 — appears to be female, with 2 chiropterine wings protruding from her back and 2 large horns growing from her head.

The subject is seen standing amid the flames as walls of rubble cascade into the earth around her.

She is observed to be smiling.
