Special Containment Procedures: All attempts to trap, destroy, or alter this anomaly have failed, either proven to be entirely inconsequential or have been consumed by the anomaly itself. It is impossible to transport the SCP to another location, and every attempt to slow progression has stalled.

Thus, the Foundation is still actively exploring other avenues to contain this anomaly and prevent a possible ZK-Class Reality Failure Scenario.Edited

Originally: "Thus, it is recommended the Foundation ceases investigation into this anomaly to preserve funds and manpower."

If, for any reason, a Foundation agent is dispatched to the anomaly's location, they are under strict instructions not to enter the distorted area. Agents are advised to be cautious of the anomaly's exact entrance and to maintain a minimum of a 5m distance from their perception of the "beginning" of the SCP at all times. Expeditions into SCP-8175 are restricted to D-Class Personnel only.

Description: SCP-8175 is an area near Kayenta, Arizona that is currently expandingEdited, and appears to distort space and time within itself, creating an apparent "Vanishing Point" from all visible angles.

Originally stated to be "of ever-expanding size".

To the human eye, the area appears as if it extends forever, but increased study has led Foundation staff to believe this distortion occurs around a single point, which has steadily grown to encompass more space. As more of the town is enveloped, the buildings fold inwards to create the appearance of a tunnel and to faciliate the "vanishing" effect.

A photo of the anomaly taken from the Northern side by Agent Báez on May 5th, 2011.

As one moves into the distorted space, the area seems to "fold" around them from their point of view, creating the illusion that they walk toward the horizon. However, the time they spend and distance increases exponentially the further they walk, though appearing normally to the person inside the anomaly. Walking for a few minutes will result in a several hour trek to return to the beginning. While able to move to their relative left and right, those who have entered the anomaly consistently report an endless, empty desert. They are able to walk in these directions, but they never seem to make any progress, remaining directly in front of the Vanishing Point.

Through extensive testing and experimentation, researchers have determined that the ratio of perceived seconds outside the anomaly to seconds inside steadily increases the further one moves toward the horizon. If one is motionless or moving back toward the entrance, the dilation is removed entirely, as communication is possible through radio technology only while subjects are stationary or actively returning to the beginning of the anomaly.

An emotional sensation has also been described upon entering the anomaly, subtly compelling those who have entered to continue forward until reaching the Vanishing Point. This sensation appears to grow stronger the further one ventures, but does not diminish upon returning from the anomaly itself.

Descriptions of the exact feeling, voice, or sensation tend to differ greatly upon separate accounts, but ultimately, all who enter the anomaly eventually succumb to the need to reach the Vanishing Point.Edited

Sentence has been cut to end after "accounts".

Outside the space, the figure grows smaller and smaller until they eventually disappear at the perceived point in space. No one has ever returned after reaching this point, seemingly becoming lost within the anomaly. Video and audio surveillance technology remains fully operational, but the distortion of time makes communication difficult. Additionally, the Foundation is unsure how radio technology is able to operate through the warped space, and transmit sound waves without issue.

Discovery: In early 1931, two Foundation agents were dispatched to a neighborhood outside Kayenta, Arizona, after reports of a "space hole" to local authorities were intercepted, detailing a distorted area in space that showed the horizon far away from them. Both agents verified the existence of the hole, requesting further aid from the Foundation to contain its existence and the anomaly itself.

The first photo taken of the anomaly by Dr. Ethel Hollis on December 3rd, 1931.

At the time, the hole was no more than 1 centimeter in diameter, and the space distorted inside it was barely visible. Even so, the neighborhood was swiftly evacuated, and every citizen was administered Class-G amnestics to ensure there would be no stray memory of this anomaly.

Site-111 was established several thousand meters away from SCP-8175, utilizing the foundation of several existing buildings in the town. Its primary purpose was to monitor and contain SCP-8175, but was known to house several other smaller-scale SCPs, all of which are now "trapped" within SCP-8175.

On specially-approved expeditions to recover important documents from within the site, these anomalies' continued existence was confirmed, as well their relative containment methods to continue to be a success. However, after consulting the O5 Council, it was determined that these anomalies be left inside Site-111, which is estimated to pass the Vanishing Point in early 2021.

Update- 4/18/2022: Site-111 has passed the Vanishing Point. All anomalies previously observed in the facility are to be marked "neutralized".

Expedition Log 8175.32.1 from Dr. Ethel Hollis on August 27th, 1932

Expedition Form

Reporting Agent: Ethel Hollis

Preparation: SCP-8175 has expanded to the size of an average doorway (approximately 2m in height), and has thus been deemed prepared for expeditions utilizing D-Class Personnel by the O5 Council.

D-Class Personnel #2230 was equipped with a backpack containing: 1 mechanically powered flashlight, approximately 2 day's worth rations, and a camera to take photos of the inside of the anomaly. Level 5 Researcher Dr. Ethel Hollis monitored the expedition from the safety of Site-111's observatory.

Execution:

The following is a transcript of the audio captured from D-Class Personnel #2230 and Dr. Ethel Hollis as they entered the anomaly for the first time.

<Begin Log>

Dr. Hollis: Hello, #2230: can you hear me? Is the earpiece working?

#2230: #2230? What does that…number mean?

Dr. Hollis: Oh, right. My apologies- you said you preferred to be called…"Marvin", was it?

#2230: Marvin. Yeah. Definitely preferable.

Dr. Hollis: Very well. Have you arrived at the, uh- gate yet?

#2230: You said it was just past the diner, right?

Dr. Hollis: That is correct, yes.

(A bit of shuffling can be heard on the tape, as #2230 approaches the entrance to SCP-8175. Suddenly, they come to a stop.)

#2230: Holy h- What in the- What is that?!

Dr. Hollis: #22- Marvin. We explained all of this to you before you were dispatched-

#2230: Yeah, yeah, you said it was an "area that distorts the space inside". I don't know if that was- I mean, it's accurate, but-

Dr. Hollis: Please just walk forward into the gateway.

#2230: I'm…not sure that I-

Dr. Hollis: -Marvin. I'm telling you this for your own good: walking through this is better than the alternative.

(A long pause. #2230 sighs.)

#2230: Fine, yeah. I get the deal.

(The sound of boots crunching against the sand relays for a few seconds, then comes to an sudden stop.)

#2230: Okay, I'm-

(#2230 abruptly pauses.)

#2230: Oh- Sorry, I, uh…I'm inside.

Dr. Hollis: Are you experiencing any physical pain?

#2230: Uh, no.

Dr. Hollis: Do you remember my name?

#2230: Ethel Hollis.

Dr. Hollis: And the date?

#2230: It's uh…August 27th, right?

(Dr. Hollis sighs in relief.)

Dr. Hollis: Yes. It is. Marvin, is there anything you can report? What do you see around you? Is it the same?

#2230: I mean, it- It looks mostly the same, yeah. But when I look past the diner on my right…there's nothing there.

Dr. Hollis: What do you mean "nothing"?

#2230: Well, the diner's still there. But there was a road behind it, and houses, buildings in the distance. Now, it just- It's just a long, empty desert with this one road down the middle. It looks…fake.

(The sound of a pencil striking paper can be heard, as Dr. Hollis records their conversation.)

Dr. Hollis: Hm…Interesting. Anything else of note?

#2230: Uh…y-yeah. Is there- Did you send other people in here?

Dr. Hollis: You are the first person to ever enter this anomaly. I can assure you of that.

#2230: But there's…some sort of voice. Or there's- Yeah, there's voices. They're faint, but I can definitely hear them.

Dr. Hollis: What are they saying?

#2230: It's not close enough to hear. I can barely even tell they're there. Should I…

(#2230 trails off. A short pause.)

#2230: Sorry. Do I have to keep moving forward?

Dr. Hollis: Yes. Please continue walking forward, and report anything notable you see along the way. I'll be standing by.

#2230: And…when can I stop?

Dr. Hollis: (Sigh) If nothing happens at all, you can return after 30 minutes. You understand?

#2230: I guess so.

(As #2230's boot can be heard striking the dirt, a slight scratching sound cuts through the tape.)

Dr. Hollis: Wh- Marvin? Hello? Are you- Are you still there?

(Silence.)

Dr. Hollis: (Sigh) Ah, shit.

(Dr. Hollis tries in vain to "fix" the radio she speaks into for 3 hours, but eventually gives up and takes a short rest on the nearby seating in the observatory. 4 more hours later, the radio crackles to life.)

#2230: -ell is going on with you, Hollis? …HOLLIS!

(A loud thud can be heard, followed by rapid footsteps, as Dr. Hollis rushes back to the microphone.)

Dr. Hollis: Hello- Hello? Marvin? Is that you?

#2230: YES! Where the hell have you been?!

Dr. Hollis: Oh, thank god. I've been waiting for you to respond for seven hours!

#2230: Se- That's…not possible. I've only been walking for about 20 minutes. And I've been trying to talk to you the whole time! You weren't answering me! It's the least you could do-

Dr. Hollis: -I'm sorry, I didn't hear you, or…anything. I thought-

(Dr. Hollis takes a deep breath.)

Dr. Hollis: How are you feeling?

#2230: I'm, uh- I want to get the hell out of here. That's how I'm feeling. This is all…too strange.

Dr. Hollis: What exactly is "strange"?

#2230: Those voices I told you about earlier! They're- They're louder now, too.

Dr. Hollis: Can you make out what they're saying?

#2230: It's- I don't really… (Clears throat) Listen, Hollis: I need to get out of here-

Dr. Hollis: -What are they saying, Marvin?

#2230: Well, I think they're telling me to keep moving forward. They're…begging, almost. It's still really hard to make any of it out, but I- I don't know if-

Dr. Hollis: -Who is "they"? Can you make out their approximate age? Sex?

#2230: It kinda sounds- I- I…don't know. But, I can't- I can't stay in here any longer, can I please come back now? It's been c-close enough to 30 minutes, right?

Dr. Hollis: For you, I suppose. Yes, you can leave. Please return to the base and I will examine you before you return to your containment area.

#2230: Oh, thank the lord. I can't bear to hear these-

(#2230 abruptly stops. A short pause.)

Dr. Hollis: Marvin? Are you alright?

#2230: Hollis…it's going to take me a long time to get back from all the way out here.

<End Log>

Conclusion: SCP-8175 appears to create a "false" reality inside itself, replicating what is near it to the best of its ability, but lacks authenticity. Consistently, it creates a single "road" or "path" that runs down the center of the anomaly until the Vanishing Point, presumably. It also seems to attempt to draw those inside toward the center, calling out in a supposed chorus of voices, though the reason is still currently unknown.

Additional Notes: D-Class Personnel #2230 is to be placed back into confinement. I've confirmed that they are not a danger to any further Foundation activities. Please provide them with a special designation so that they may be re-used for further expedition, and put in a request for extended containment. -Hollis

Expedition Log 8175.43.19 from Dr. Ethel Hollis on June 9th, 1943

Expedition Form

Reporting Agent: Ethel Hollis

Preparation: SCP-8175 has continued encompassing more of the town, completely enveloping the diner it appeared next to. Its approximate size is 15m in diameter, and appears to expand in every direction.

While other D-Class were used, D-Class Personnel #2230, due to special designation, has journeyed into SCP-8175 22 separate times, but has never come close to reaching the Vanishing Point. The hold that this anomaly has over them has grown significantly, as the subject describes the resultant voices as "constantly present" and, oftentimes, "overwhelming".

This expedition, they were outfitted with 12 months worth of rations (doubling the quantity from the previous trial), as well as a mechanical flashlight, tent for shelter, and camera.

Agent Takumi Nakano was assigned to accompany #2230 into the anomaly to ensure total collaboration, after #2230 exhibited extreme trepidation toward entering the anomaly again. He was oufitted with a ballistic vest and helmet, as well as a Colt Official Police service revolver for defense in case of any aggression.

Execution: As Dr. Hollis sets up a camera at the front of SCP-8175 to consistently film the expedition, Agent Nakano drags D-Class Personnel #2230 to the base of the anomaly, despite a great deal of resistance. Junior Researcher Dr. Anthony DeMartin was assigned to observe and study proper protocol for handling D-Class Personnel, and for approaching 8175.

The following is an (abridged) transcript of the entire expedition.

<Begin Log>

D-Class #2230: NO! You can't! You can't make me go in there! Send me back!!

Agent Takumi Nakano: (Struggling with #2230) Ma'am, h- they're presenting a bit of a problem-

Dr. Ethel Hollis: -They have for the past few expeditions. It's…something we're going to have to deal with. We've deemd them necessary for further investigation.

Agent Nakano: R-Right, but-

Dr. Hollis: -I understand how this must feel, but…please. Just follow our lead, alright?.

(A short pause.)

Agent Nakano: I will, ma'am.

Dr. Hollis: Thank you. Now, please escort #2230 into the anomaly.

#2230: WAIT! W-wait. Ethel, please-

Dr. Hollis: -Don't- …Please, don't call me that. It's "Dr. Hollis."

#2230: S-sorry. Dr. Hollis, I- I can't go in there again. Please…Please. I- I've spent years in there at this point, and it's- Those voices, they're- they're killing me. Every time I go in there, they get louder and louder, and- I can't- I can't do it anymore. I just- Just send someone in my place, I'm begging you.

(Dr. Hollis turns away from #2230.)

Dr. Hollis: I'm- (Deep breath) I'm afraid your experience is too valuable. Providing more information about the compulsion you're feeling might aid us in understanding this anomaly further. I'd urge you to cooperate with Agent Nakano for this expedition. I really don't want to have to- …Just, trust me, alright?

#2230: -I DON'T CARE! I- I need to go. I need to go to…No. Nonono. Not that, I can't- I can't face this anymore. They're…they're going to yell at me, and then I won't- I won't be able to-

(She turns back to face them.)

Dr. Hollis: -There's no one in there. We've told you this many times. What you're experiencing is unique to you. No one else has reported anything remotely like what you've heard, so it is not possible that any of these…sensations are remotely real. Besides, you don't have a choice. Maybe it would help to think of the…service you're providing hum-

#2230: -No, they said that there isn't- There's nothing that we can do! I've heard them talk around my- my cell! Don't you underst- Don't you get that? Please, I- This won't help. I can't help you, anymore, and you need to just- They- They're just going to get louder, and I can't…focus on anything. I can't keep living like this! Already, it's-

Dr. Hollis: -Can you tell me who they are yet? Can you identify the sex of these voices? Can you tell me why they're calling you forward? Anything at all about them?

(A long pause. #2230 looks down at the ground, avoiding Dr. Hollis's gaze.)

#2230: I- I don't- It isn't that simple. I can't just- I can't show you how it feels. You'd need to go in, and you won't-

Dr. Hollis: -Well, then continued investigation is necessary. We've never reached the finish line, here. There's so much we still don't know, and…I apologize, but I cannot accept failure in this case.

(Dr. Hollis leans in closer.)

Dr. Hollis: And I must say, all this time in the anomaly has alleviated the aging process. Anthony, make a note: #2230 and I are of the same relative age, yet they appear approximately 5-10 years younger. It isn't just a perception of time that is altered inside 8175, it's time itself.

Dr. DeMartin: (Taking notes) Fascinating.

#2230: Listen, I- I know what you're trying to do here is…important. And I know- I know that you want to solve this or- or fix it, but you can't! You can't, and- and sending me back in there over and over, it won't help! What could you even learn that would help get rid of this thing?! Tell me! You- You said yourself you haven't learned anything at all in the last few tries!

(A short pause.)

Dr. Hollis: I already told you: we are further examining this sensation that you've described. We're not sure, exactly, what it is we're looking for in…all of this. However, we understand that scientific experimentation is often about pursuing your goals, even though the means seem to be out of your reach.

#2230: But I'm- I'm just…I'm so tired. I'm not fit for all this.

Dr. Hollis: Of course you're tired. You don't think I am? I'm exhausted. I'm sure everyone here is. But it doesn't mean we aren't fit for this. This is…It's epoch-making, what we're doing. And we need you for any of it to work.

(Dr. Hollis nods to Agent Nakano, signalling them to begin the expedition. #2230 whimpers softly, as the agent escorts them into SCP-8175. #2230 begins muttering immediately upon entry.)

#2230: (Whispering) No. I didn't- I didn't want it to end like that…I'm so sorry. I'm so so sorry. Please just stop.

(Agent Nakano takes in his surroundings.)

Agent Nakano: Hmph.

Dr. Hollis: Make sure they don't present any problems.

Agent Nakano: Roger that.

Dr. Hollis: And try to remain focused on the task at hand. Is there anything distracting you? Perhaps calling to you?

Agent Nakano: There's- I think I can hear someone breathing in the distance? Or maybe they're…wheezing? I'm not sure. It's hard to make it out.

Dr. Hollis: How can you hear breathing "in the distance"?

Agent Nakano: I don't know, but it- It just seems like I'd need to keep moving forward to make…whatever it is sound clear.

Dr. Hollis: Well, lucky for you, that's your job, here. Just keep the ultimate goal in mind, alright? We need you both to return from this expedition. It's pertinent. Please check in every 6 hours, as we discussed. I might not always be here, but Dr. DeMartin will man the radio when I need to rest. You'll always get a response.

Agent Nakano: Understood, ma'am. I won't let you down.

Dr. Hollis: I appreciate it.

(A click can be heard, signaling the end of the radio call. Agent Nakano and D-Class #2230 walk forward in the anomaly for 8 months, 3 weeks, 6 days, and 14 hours before reaching the believed threshold for the Vanishing Point. Times between correspondences steadily increased, as predicted due to the dilation caused by 8175.)

Agent Nakano: Hollis? You there?

Dr. Hollis: Still here. Anything new to report?

Agent Nakano: Well, I- I think we've-

(D-Class Personnel #2230 falls to their knees, clutching the sides of their head, with tears streaming down their cheeks.)

#2230: NO! NO! Please, I- I thought we all could be together peacefully, I- I didn't- I didn't mean for- …Please, please stop, STOP saying that! Please!!

Agent Nakano: Sorry- I think we've reached the end, here.

Dr. Hollis: How can you tell?

Agent Nakano: D-…Did you not just hear them?

Dr. Hollis: Of course, but how do you know? This behavior is not uncommon for #2230.

Agent Nakano: Well, you know that breathing I've been describing? It's uh…crying. Yeah that's- it's someone crying, and it sounds like they're right in front of me. Like if I take one more step, I'll bump right into them.

Dr. Hollis: Can you see who it is? Is there someone there?

Agent Nakano: No, everything looks…the same. The dirt road is in front of me, and we could both keep walking, but-

#2230: -I- I thought it was over! I thought everything we had was over! They'd- They had taken everything from me! I couldn't- I couldn't just leave without you!

Agent Nakano: They just keep yelling. I've tried to calm them down, but it's been like this for the past day and a half.

Dr. Hollis: #2230? Can you hear me? It's Dr. Hollis.

#2230: Shhhh….Shhh, it's okay, hon'. I'm going to see you soon. Real soon, I promise, okay? Just please be quiet for now. I need quiet!

Dr. Hollis: #2230, who are you talking to?

#2230: I- I told you- I told you not to let me back in here! Now, they're…they're yelling at me, and I- I'm not- I can't do any of this anymore. It's- It's over for me. They know it's over for me…

(#2230 begins to stand up, attempting to take a step forward, but Agent Nakano catches them, tackling them back to the ground before they can make a move, pinning their arms behind their back.)

#2230: NO! NO!! I need to be with them! Please! PLEASE!! I can't- I can't be away from them anymore! NO!!! This is the only way they'll forgive me, PLEASE! Don't listen to her! She doesn't understand!

Dr. Hollis: What's going on in there?

Agent Nakano: (Struggling with #2230) Not to- ugh- worry. I've contained them, ma'am. Should I administer an amnestic?

Dr. Hollis: (Sigh) Give it a try. It doesn't usually work. And bring them back as soon as you can.

#2230: NO! It's not going to- Please! Please, just let me in there! You understand, you're in here with me! You have to, please!

(A long pause.)

Agent Nakano: …Y-Yeah. Will do.

Dr. Hollis: You're sure that won't be a problem?

Agent Nakano: I'll be fine. It's just that they- That person…They sound like they need help, you know? What if that's someone you've already sent in here? And- And- And they're stuck there, forever? Maybe it could benefit all of us if I went and retrieved them?

Dr. Hollis: No one has ever been to this location before. That crying isn't real. It's just as we told #2230. Focus on returning to us, your duty to the Foundation, the urgency of this investigation. I'm sure you can put those…sounds behind you. It's just noise, after all.

Agent Nakano: …Right. It'll- It won't affect me going forward.

…Now, how long will it take us to get back?

<End Log>

Conclusion: Agent Nakano returned to the base of the anomaly with D-Class Personnel #2230, after another ≈9 months of travel. The hold that SCP-8175 has over #2230 has only increased, further decreasing their mental capacity and ability to obey basic orders. It appears that this sensation grows stronger the further one journeys into the anomaly, and only compounds on each subsequent expedition. There seems to be a personal connection between #2230 and the "voices", as they've described, but they remain silent on any detail about the identities of those who call to them. It is not known how the anomaly assesses what will successfully draw those inside further forward, but is uniquely capable of acquiring the necessary information the second one enters.

Though Agent Nakano administered a Class B amnestic to the subject to attempt to erase the final few days of the journey, the effect of the anomaly did not seem to diminish. #2230 continued screaming and pleading for part of the journey back, only willingly traveling forward after Nakano administered Class I amnestics. While #2230 still reported hearing voices pleading to them, they felt no emotional connection or drive to follow them while the amnestic lasted, though effects deteriorated quicker than anticipated. Mutliple doses were required, decreasing in efficiency each time they were administered. The Site Amnestics Division advised that this method of coercion was unsustainable going forward, and #2230 should not be given any further amnestics, as they pose a severe threat to the health of the subject's brain.

Additional Notes: Agent Takumi Nakoto, after pyschological evaluation, was deemed unfit for future service due to residual effects of SCP-8175, and was permanently dismissed from the Foundation.

#2230 was placed back into confinement and prescribed 75mg of fluoxetine and 7mg of haloperidol per day, to attempt to counteract the effects of 8175. Dr. Ethel Hollis will continue regular psychological evaluation before further expeditions. Alternate D-Class Personnel should be used in the meantime.

I'm not sure how much longer they'll be able to keep this up. They can't even sit still anymore. So many expeditions, and still…not much new information to show for all of our efforts. And all of this, it's becoming awfully taxing.

This thing, whatever it is, is creeping closer to the Site every day. This is everything. Not just to me, but to every one of us, here. It must be contained before it envelops this Site, this state…

I will request further funding from the O5 Council, and we will increase the number of weekly expeditions. I'm sure they'll be amenable; they've always understood. And if we devote enough effort, we can make sense of this, I'm sure of it. We just need more time. And we will not give in. -Hollis

Expedition Log 8175.60.09 from Dr. Ethel Hollis on March 3rd, 1960Edited

This log is not included in the generally-accessible document.

Expedition Form

Reporting Agent: Ethel Hollis

Preparation: SCP-8175 has grown to approximately 2000 meters, though precise measurement now proves difficult.

D-Class Personnel #2230 was prevented from entering SCP-8175 for the previous 5 years, after concerns grew from their decreasing mental capacity. However, after prolonged medication and extensive psychological counseling by Dr. Ethel Hollis, D-Class Personnel #2230 was deemed fit to be sent into SCP-8175 once again, though other medical personnel advised against the use of any further amnestics.

They were outfitted with the same gear as previous successful expeditions to the Vanishing Point, as well as the newly designed "Signal Protocol Device" (SPD) to attempt to slow the growth or mitigate the effects of 8175. These devices were designed to be hammered into the ground, their small spike affixing them in place, to continually monitor conditions inside the anomaly without disturbance.

Execution: As customary, a camera was positioned at the base of the anomaly by Level 5 Researcher Dr. Anthony DeMartin. #2230 willing walks to the anomaly, escorted by Agent Amelia Castrello, in case of any resistance, who has additionally undergone extensive preventive Cognitohazard training to prepare for SCP-8175.

The following is an (abridged) transcript of the entire expedition.

<Begin Log>

Dr. Anthony DeMartin: Hello, #2230! How are we feeling today?

(#2230 stares down at the ground, unresponsive.)

Dr. Ethel Hollis: (Sigh) They're not going to answer you, Anthony. Give it up; it's pointless. This is the best we can get of them. Any of them, really.

Dr. DeMartin: But I wanted to-

Dr. Hollis: -Anthony.

(A short pause.)

Dr. DeMartin: Sorry…

Dr. Hollis: (To Agent Amelia Castrello) Please escort the D-Class to the Vanishing Point of the anomaly. They shouldn't present you any problems.

Agent Castrello: Understood, ma'am. Anything else I should expect?

(Dr. Hollis breathes in, but cannot find the words she's looking for.)

Dr. DeMartin: Uh, it- It should try and convince you to walk past the Vanishing Point. (Clears throat) The feeling is different for everyone, but it's important you follow our instructions to the letter.

(A short pause. Dr. DeMartin looks nervously between Dr. Hollis and Agent Castrello.)

Agent Castrello: Of course, sir.

(Agent Castrello escorts #2230 past the threshold into SCP-8175. #2230 breathes heavily, but does not utter a word.)

Agent Castrello: Oh, I- I…see what you meant. There's- I can smell something-

Dr. Hollis: -We're not interested in what you experience. Just guide #2230 to the Vanishing Point and back. That's all we need from you.

Agent Castrello: Right. Apologies, ma'am.

Dr. DeMartin: Uh- Out of curiosity: what is it that you smell?

(Dr. Hollis audibly sighs.)

Agent Castrello: It uh- It's really faint, but it- it smells good, I can tell you that much-

Dr. Hollis: -Enough. Please. Just…forward. I can't do these all day anymore.

Agent Castrello: Can do, ma'am.

(The pair begin traveling forward, continuing as scheduled for 9 months, 2 days, and 7 hours, placing the SPDs along the way at equidistant points. #2230 does not speak for the entirety of the expedition, also not participating in planting the devices, despite attempted coaxing from Agent Castrello. However, upon reaching the final threshold before the Vanishing Point, they voluntarily take the final SPD out of their pack.)

#2230: Hey. Ethel?

Dr. Hollis: -#2230? Wh- I told you never to call me that again. But thanks for checking in…finally.

(#2230 begins snickering.)

#2230: Wow, I can- I can barely even he-hear you at all anymore. Heh…That's- It's not even my fault…

Dr. Hollis: Because of 8175? How long have- Why are you even speaking now? You haven't said so much as a word to me or anyone in months.

#2230: Well, I- I don't really- Ugh- God, it's, uh- it's really hard for me to- to keep my thoughts straight. But I've- I've known you for a long…long, long time now. And you…you're just like me, aren't you?

(A short pause.)
