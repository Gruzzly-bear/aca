Item #: SCP-9955

Object Class: Uncontained (Neutralized)

Special Containment Procedures: Radio frequencies from the Large Sagittarius Star Cloud are to be monitored for resurgences of SCP-9955 after the current hosts' expiration. The Foundation is to continue working its investigations in tandem with the Theurgic Company of the Silver Twilight (see below).

Personnel interacting with SCP-9955 must have a cognitive rating of 75 or above, and be trained in the construction of a psionic dæmon.1

Description: SCP-9955 was a previously unknown retrocausal Fifthist Sect known as the Fifth Congregational Church. The five cult leaders of SCP-9955 manipulated members of the inner circle in order to transfer Elan-Vital Energy (EVE) from one host of SCP-9955 to one healthy human subject knowledgeable in occultism and anomalous technology. When a non-cultist subject is within an estimated radius of 500 meters from SCP-9955, they will experience a psionic event within the next 24 hours. The subject will hallucinate being stalked by a cult leader and a hand with a relevant sin burned on its back.

Group of Interest Summary

Name/Code: The Theurgic Company of the Silver Twilight/GoI-1565

Danger Level: Caution

Summary of History: Founded in 1875, GoI-1565 preceded Foundation presence in the region by thirty years. For those thirty years the GoI had been the primary group in maintaining normalcy and the Veil. After Provisional Outpost-3125 was established, GoI-1565 reached out to the Foundation to cooperate regarding discovered anomalous phenomena. Notable cooperations between the Foundation and GoI-1565 include: destruction of an anomalous reef off the coast of Massachusetts, a Martian invasion in the Berkshires, an infinitely looping tunnel (also in the Berkshires), and Mitt Romney.

Known Active Key Members: Head Magus Xavier D'Arta.

Discovery: The Fifth Congregational Church first made itself known on 05/05/2025, when GoI-1565 discovered two of their occultists living in Billington's Wood had disappeared. GoI-1565 would later reach out to Site-3125 to report and seek assistance. This disappearance had gone unnoticed by local authorities, local residents, and Site-3125's HUME2 and EVE scans of the Miskatonic River Valley. Persons who had relatives or friends in Billington's Wood deny knowing said missing persons, or the existence of the village.

Interview Log

Interviewer: Field Researcher Mordecai Ryder
Interviewee: Head Magus Xavier D'Arta
Date: 05/10/2025
Time: 11:23 EST

[Ryder and D'Arta sit on a bench overlooking an open field of grass with little tree cover. College students are playing Frisbee, an old woman is walking her dog, and a group of people wait for the bus. It is a clear, sunny day.]

Ryder: (shakes D'Arta's hand) Thank you for meeting with me.

D'Arta: Likewise.

Ryder: First, I need to know why you picked an unsecured location? This is a public park.

D'Arta: You must be new here.

Ryder: About one and a half years…

D'Arta: Enough weirdness happens in this area that locals are unfazed. Anyone overhearing our conversation will more than likely think we're discussing a LARP.

Ryder: Okay, sure. What do y'all know so far about the Fifth Congregational Church?

D'Arta: "Y'all" (laughs) You are new here. Our thaumaturgic sessions suggest they've operated in the area since the mid-1600s and had been under the radar for nearly 300 years.

Ryder: They've also been under Site-3125's radar. How do you think the congregation remained unknown?

D'Arta: One of my closest occultists believes that this congregation isn't continuous.

Ryder: Continuous? What does this mean?

D'Arta: They might have been in operation since the 1600s, but which 1600s? Whose 1600s? (shakes head) Since neither the Company or the Foundation had any knowledge of this specific Fifthist sect, my fellow occultist and I think they aren't from our 1600s.

Ryder: They're time-travelers?

D'Arta: Sort of? More like multiversal travelers. I feel it would be beneficial if we investigated Billington's Wood together.

Ryder: I thought it was gone?

D'Arta: Well, we'll investigate the area that used to be Billington's Wood.
