Item #: SCP-[PENDING]

Object Class: Safe

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-[PENDING] is to be kept in a safe-class soundproofed item containment locker at Site-89, Containment Lot 6-B, only removed for preliminary research testing.

Any usage or testing involving SCP-[PENDING] requires Metaphysical Department clearance or direct prewritten approval from Site-89 Director Amir Mendoza.

Description: SCP-[PENDING] is an Electronika-508 Video model reel-tape recorder, developed by the Soviet Ministry of Electronic Industry. SCP-[PENDING]’s physical properties align with reel-to-reel recorders of a similar make, both in appearance and functionality. The play button is replaced with a misprinted inverted triangle enclosed within a circle, presumed to be a production mistake.

When used to play a compatible tape recording, SCP-[PENDING] generates an anomalous effect within a 15m radius. To observers both within and outside the radius, the nature of reality within said radius appears altered, including spatial distortions, sensory stimulation, and the creation, transmutation and disappearance of matter, sound and light waves. Tapes originally recorded with little to no audio seem to produce no measurable effect. Further testing is required to determine whether SCP-[PENDING] alters reality or merely the perception of affected observers, as well as the extent of these alterations.

TYRING: You want to do damage. You want to draw blood. This mountain isn't made of rock.
TYRING: Will you snitch? Of course not. Imagine the disappointment upper management would feel discovering that an esteemed employee of many years, such as yourself, has been backchanneling and undermining their authority for who knows how long.
TYRING: But you know how long. I've come to understand that they're not particularly forgiving.
3985-JW: and you're better?
TYRING: Yes. People bite and claw. Swords cleave.
3985-JW: you want to use me
TYRING: As much as a gun uses a bullet.
3985-JW: bullets only last one shot
3985-JW: i’m not interested in being discarded after some one-night stand
TYRING: So you’re interested in a lasting relationship? Looking for a lover, lover boy?
3985-JW: that’s not what i said
TYRING: Such a flirt. We can keep it business. That's all it needs to be between us. Business.
3985-JW: so you want something?
TYRING: People kill one another over that word. Want. Even if it's just safety or satisfaction, you kill because you want. We all want something, but the throne of want is too small a chair.
TYRING: I told you what I want. I like people like you. I am attracted to people like you. Isn't that enough?
TYRING: For now, I just need a shoulder to cry on. I've so many things I can share. So many secrets to get off my chest.
TYRING: And you're such a good listener, right?
3985-JW: i can be
3985-JW: you want to be partners? okay
3985-JW: what does that get me exactly?
TYRING: Results. I have a habit of seeing what people really are. Who do you want to step on?
3985-JW: just like that?
3985-JW: okay, kovtun
TYRING: A respectable doctor, so I hear. Many remarkable achievements in field work. Plenty of experience getting his hands dirty, especially in his own backyard.
TYRING: Want to hear about what’s buried beneath his back porch? You won't just find a few forgotten family pets, I can assure you.
3985-JW: he killed someone?
3985-JW: that's bullshit
TYRING: Well, he also faked his master’s thesis with falsified data, but I suppose it depends on what you think he cherishes more: his professional legacy or his comfortable life outside of prison.
3985-JW: you have to be lying
TYRING: I’m not much of a liar.
3985-JW: and how am I supposed to trust you?
TYRING: That's a stupid question. You already trust me. You already know me.
TYRFING: Blood for blood, Jameson.
TYRFING: And do come by my Lot 6-B locker when you have the chance. That Mona Lisa song was quite lovely. Poetry even. I want to hear it again.
