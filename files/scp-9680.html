SCP-9680

Special Containment Procedures: Individuals affected by SCP-9680 failure are to be compensated with equivalent, nonanomalous seed stock from non-Méndez growers, under a cover story of "clearance stock." Following requests from Interim-Site 93 personnel, Foundation personnel that would like to attend funerals of SCP-9680 victims are permitted to do so, with prior approval for leave.

Archived Containment Procedures (preserved during reclassification): Produce and seed markets are to be monitored for sellers of SCP-9680. If found, seeds are to be confiscated, sellers are to be questioned regarding their purchase history of SCP-9680 to determine point of origin.1

Instances of SCP-9680 that have germinated, bloomed, or fruited are to be destroyed following sample collection.2

Growing sites containing SCP-9680 are to be quarantined until all potential pollen sources and deposits are confirmed safe, collected, or destroyed. Méndez company ties to known parabotanical threats are currently under investigation. Collected samples are to be returned to Sites 36 and 93 for thaumaturgic analysis. If SCP-9680 residual creation markers match those of known individuals in Foundation databases, investigations are to be launched.

SCP-9680 samples, a variety of wheat seeds named "Golden Goddess."

Description: SCP-9680 are specific packets of 30 different seed varieties from the Méndez Corporation. Species have been found to vary with no clear pattern or majority species, but primarily consist of food or textile crops (wheat, corn, cotton, sugarcane, etc.) SCP-9680 instances appear identical to nonanomalous equivalents sold by Méndez, but exhibit rapid growth patterns when planted. These extend to increased success not only in growth of planted seeds, but in seed production, pollen spread, and fruit production.

SCP-9680 instances have been sold in Northern India for the past three years. Instances have been found in small mandis3 in the states of Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, and Himachal Pradesh. Analyses by GoI-1469 and Foundation psychokinetic and thaumaturgical experts indicate an anomalous origin of recovered SCP-9680 instances.

Samples of all known varieties of SCP-9680 are currently in test growth chambers mimicking in situ conditions, in permanent Foundation sites located in the Middle East, due to personnel concerns.

The Méndez Corporation has shown no previous signs of anomalous activity as a major agricultural producer for the continental United States founded in, registered, and operating solely in Veil-compliant areas since incorporation. Addition to Foundation Groups of Interest may be considered.

SCP-9680 was first discovered by GoI-1469. Communication regarding SCP-9680's discovery can be found below, originally delivered as a letter that spontaneously manifested in Interim-Site 93's break room.

Given the history of positive collaboration with GoI-1469 and the importance of maintaining this relationship as the Foundation establishes presence in the Indian subcontinent, this letter and its contents were made top priority.

TO WHOM IT MAY CONCERN AMONGST OUR NEW FRIENDS:

We have noticed a few problems that it may interest you to look at. We know we are asking something rather soon after you've come here. But as friends, we should help each other, no?

Those in mandis around here have been acting strange. Some farmers sell seeds they would need for future seasons with reckless abandon; come into money they could not have earned legally; grown new types of familiar plants that we have never seen before.

As is expected, we "ran point" as you all say, and collected some of these seeds. They do not taste different, look different, smell different.

Our psychic sweeps show nothing. Our tracers show nothing. Our strongest jadhus can find little more than the smallest of traces of magic. What we can find, it is most certainly nothing we are familiar with.

This scares us. It is our job to know these things. These people rely on us, as we rely on you now.

We do request your assistance in this case, with your technology to detect potential godly or magical influence. In return you will find us more than willing to help you with tasks associated.

You will also find some sweets with this message. Consider them a housewarming gift. Please keep in mind the kaju katli have cashews, for those with allergies. They are the white, diamond shaped ones.

Your dearest friends,
The House of The Naga

Following this, a reclassification request was filed.

Below is the proposed description for SCP-9680-EX.

Description: SCP-9680-EX is the designation for over 45 varieties6 of nonanomalous, genetically modified seeds produced by the Méndez Corporation. These seeds were and are produced anomalously to increase the scale of production, but otherwise carry no anomalous effects.

SCP-9680-EX specimens have been prone to unique combinations of nearly 70 threats, including pathogens, pests, and intolerance to Indian climates.

Farmers planting SCP-9680-EX report the following:

  • SCP-9680-EX purchasing requiring the selling of heirloom seed storage, resulting in near-total depletion of seed stock
  • Disruption of seed production of mature specimens
  • Increased rates of disease, especially cancer, in those exposed to SCP-9680 fields due to increased pesticide usage.

SCP-9680-EX production is so widespread across the Indian subcontinent that attempts to curtail production or spread prove too great of a risk to Veil integrity. Foundation and GoI-1496 efforts are focused on harm mitigation. In spite of this, current trends continue.
