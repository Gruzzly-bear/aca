Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8631 is to be stored in a low-risk containment locker when not in its active state. No effective means of containing SCP-8631 in its active state is known, but it has not been observed leaving Site-102 since its initial containment. Anomalous gifts given by SCP-8631 are to be documented and placed in storage. Non-anomalous gifts may be accepted by staff.

Description: SCP-8631 is a carved pumpkin containing an unidentified light source. It has not shown any signs of aging or rot since its initial containment and becomes intangible in response to physical damage. On October 31st1 each year, SCP-8631 will enter its active state for a period of 24 hours. During this time, it will manifest a humanoid body with the pumpkin as its head and animate. In its active state, SCP-8631 is capable of turning intangible at will and will offer gifts to nearby humans, currently consisting exclusively of Site-102 staff, and occasionally animals. These gifts often have minor anomalous properties.

Little is known about SCP-8631's personal history, besides that it responds to the name "Jack" and was active in the town of Dingle, County Kerry, for several years before it was moved to the nearby Site-102. Anomalous gifts had been removed from the town, with residents amnectized into believing the remainder had been given by friends or relatives. SCP-8631 becomes defensive when questioned about its past, claiming it is "irrelevant".

Addendum:
In response to repeated questioning about its motives, SCP-8631 provided the following letter, stating that it was "as personal as [it is] willing to get".

Dear Jack,

I will not claim to know whether you are truly repentant or merely wish to escape your current situation. But if you are truly sorry, it would simply be cruel not to give you an opportunity to redeem yourself.

While fully resurrecting the dead is beyond my station, I can arrange to have you return for one day a year. You are to use this time to give back to the world you have stolen from. For the rest of the year, I have arranged access to a workshop you may use to make what you will give back. Please write back at your earliest convenience if you accept this arrangement.

I hope you have truly learned not to be so stingy.

Peter
