Department of Pharmacology

Special Containment Procedures: Ongoing research is being conducted by the Department of Pharmacology in order to find a potential cure to SCP-9150. Foundation staff affected by the anomaly must undergo mandated therapy until the anomalous effect is no longer present.

Treatment-R9KL is to be synthesized and sold at Foundation-owned pharmacies under the common name of ██████ ████.

SCP-9150 is to be left uncontained. Treatment-R9KL is available for all Foundation staff on request. (See Addendum 9150.1)

Description: SCP-9150 refers to an anomaly that is present in the mind of every living Homo sapiens.

The anomalous effects of SCP-9150 are triggered when a person experiences a high concentration of negative emotions, and the anomaly will last until the individual has fully recovered from the trigger event. During SCP-9150, the individual will progressively lose a specific memory or concept, with the effect intensifying when the individual speak, thinks, or otherwise directly acknowledge the concept. The effects of SCP-9150 can be categorized into 4 5 distinct stages, and a single affected individual can inhabit multiple stages of SCP-9150 for different memories. An advancement of the anomaly will also be accompanied by a specific activation event.

Stage # Effect Activation Event
1 Individual starts experiencing difficulty in remembering minor details of the memory. Individual freezes in place and stares forward.
2 Individual begins to lose long term memories that are related to the core concept. Individual crouches down and lies flat on the ground. If the ground is not appropriate for the action, the individual will wander around in search of an appropriate surface.
3 Individual have great difficulty in recalling the concept, requiring intense concentration. Individual stares to the ceiling for a few seconds before whispering the phrase "Don't forget."1
4 Individual completely loses all memory of the concept. However, the lost concept can still be retaught from scratch, and memories can be artificially replanted. Individual will continuously scream before passing out. Affected individuals have been recorded to take from five seconds up to three months to awaken.
5 Individual loses ability to observe and comprehend the affected concept. Currently known to be permanent. Unknown2

From various Foundation records, there exists numerous evidence to SCP-9150 having been present in the human history for an indeterminate amount of time, presumably since the first emergence of Homo sapiens. The fact that the anomaly was only recently classified as SCP-9150 by the Foundation suggests that the anomaly was retroactively introduced into the collective noosphere.

To date, there has been a total of 1 4 12 63 221 recorded instances of individuals progressing to Stage 5 of the anomaly.

Discovery: The Foundation became aware of SCP-9150 on 5/7/2020 when Junior Researcher Sasha F. Hiller reported having problems with remembering specific details about their assigned research. Hiller was initially prescribed countertop medications, which were ineffective. After reports of similar symptoms in Foundation personnel, and reports from various Foundation-owned public hospitals of the same illness being present in the general populace, the anomaly was designated as SCP-9150, and the Paramedical Department was assigned for further research on the anomaly and its effects.

Addendum-9150.1
Treatment-R9KL is an anomalous drug synthesized by the Parapharmacology Division, having the ability to permanently cure SCP-9150 in an individual, with a success rate of 100% in all recorded instances. However, Treatment-R9KL cannot recover memories and concepts lost during the anomaly, only removing the anomaly itself from the individual.

More information on the components and synthesis of Treatment-R9KL is strictly classified. If necessary, contact Senior Researcher Stephanie Allen for requesting access to Document-R9KL-1 and Document-R9KL-2.

Addendum-9150.2
PoI-9150-1 was the first civilian to be interviewed by the Paramedical Department as part of Experiment-9150.A, under the guise of a therapy session. PoI-9150-1 was a witness in Incident-8232-Alpha, the containment breach of an anomaly that lead to approximately 253 civilian casualties during the time period between 3/9/1988 to 11/3/2011 before the anomaly was eventually contained by the Foundation on 12/3/2011.

More information on the anomaly and Incident-8232-Alpha is strictly classified. If necessary, contact the Cryptozoology Division and/or Division Director Whittaker for requesting access to the SCP document.

Proposal-10KLL.1
[FILE OPENED]

Proposal: Due SCP-9150's effects potential to completely cripple any and all Foundation research. Treatment-R9KL is to be administered to every Foundation personnel; as a preemptive measure against SCP-9150.

O5 COUNCIL

5 1 7

ETHICS COMMITTEE

YEA ABSTAIN NAY
18 2 30

STATUS
DENIED

Although tempting, due to the cost and time needed to synthesize Treatment-R9KL, wide application across the whole Foundation is neither feasible with our current resources, nor worth the effort. Other methods of reducing the risk of SCP-9150 in Foundation personnel should be considered as an alternative to anomalous drugs.

— Jason L. Graham, Director, Department of Pharmacology.

Proposal-10KLL.2
[FILE OPENED]

Proposal: Due SCP-9150's effects potential to completely cripple any and all Foundation research. Treatment-R9KL is to be administered to every researcher and Foundation personnel assigned to high-importance duties who are prone to encountering potentially traumatic events during work; as a preemptive measure against SCP-9150.

O5 COUNCIL

YEA ABSTAIN NAY
7 2 4

ETHICS COMMITTEE

YEA ABSTAIN NAY
22 4 24

APPROVED

The anomalous effects of SCP-9150 poses a sizable risk to our most important personnel, therefore, many of our important duties, which can further lead to potentially catastrophic consequences later down the line. Although tedious, this is a necessary step that we'll have to take if we want our knowledge to be secured, contained, and protected.

— O5-█
