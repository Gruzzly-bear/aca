SCP-9669

Item #: SCP-9669 Level 3/9669 Object Class: Safe Classified

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-9669-2 through -5 are kept in a standard secure storage locker in Room 08 of Site-81's Transplanar Research Sector.

Routine usage of SCP-9669 for diplomatic and research purposes is to be carried out under supervision of the current acting lead of Site-81's Transplanar Research Sector.

Foundation communications monitoring teams are to consider potential leads to the whereabouts of SCP-9669-6 a high priority.

Update 8/25/2025: Remaining instances of SCP-9669 have been confiscated following Incident 9669-82225. Standard diplomatic and research activities are on hold. Access is restricted without direct appointment by the Incident 9669-82225 Investigatory Committee.

SCP-9669-2

Description: SCP-9669 denotes 6 identical pairs of cosmetic novelty glasses, designated SCP-9669-1 through -6. Instances of SCP-9669 have lenses with a variable but highly saturated tint.

A small slider is present on the left temple of each instance of SCP-9669. When a subject wearing an instance of SCP-9669 adjusts the slider, the subject will exit local reality, coinciding with a shift in the hue of the lenses. This causes the subject to appear to instantaneously physically demanifest.

Subjects who exit local reality via SCP-9669 appear instantaneously in another reality corresponding to the hue of the object's lenses. Manifestation exerts a retrocausal effect which leads the immediate surrounding topography of the destination to have a near identical layout to that of the subject's location in their previous reality. Relative location of the subject remains consistent between realities.

If multiple subjects wearing SCP-9669 are present in the immediate vicinity of one another when a change in local reality occurs, all subjects are transported simultaneously.

[Alexander shouts. Dr. Tolmony tackles him to the ground and plunges the knife into his side. She pulls it back out and swings it toward his neck, but he catches her by the wrist at the last moment.]

Alexander: Who the—

[Alexander rolls to the side and uses both hands to hold back Dr. Tolmony's knife-wielding arm. His expression changes to shock then a slight smile when he recognizes Dr. Tolmony.]

[Dr. Tolmony strains against him and moves her free hand to try to unsteady his grip.]

Alexander: Tolmony. I guess— You really didn't want to take my gamble, huh?

[Dr. Tolmony only grunts in frustration.]

Alexander: You had a second pair, then? Suppose I should've checked. Was wondering where those went.

Tolmony: No, Alexander. I just had— A bit of help.

[Dr. Tolmony suddenly moves back and uses the force of Alexander trying to push her arm away to stand and wrench her wrist free.]

[Alexander takes the opportuinty to push Dr. Tolmony back further and get to his feet. His posture is slightly hunched, and he's visibly bleeding from his side.]

[Alexander holds out a hand. He curls his fingers a few times, as if expecting something to happen, then looks up. Dr. Tolmony is moving for the tablet.]

Alexander: Wait a second—

[Alexander goes after her, but Dr. Tolmony reaches it first.]

[Dr. Tolmony reaches the tablet and takes it. She turns to face Alexander while backpedaling. She stops when her back hits solid stone in the nook between the wall by the altar and a rectangular stone pillar holding up the ceiling.]

[Alexander approaches Dr. Tolmony despite a noticable limp and reaches into his pocket.]

Alexander: Tolmony. What are you planning to do with that thing? Where do you think you can take it that I won't follow? You know it's indestructible.

[Alexander pulls a pistol from his pocket.]

Alexander: You can stop here, Tolmony. Hand it over.

Alexander: We've done a lot to each other, but if you drop the chase, you can go.

[Alexander holds up his pistol and steadies his aim on Dr. Tolmony.]

Alexander: You had a good run, really. You're tenacious. But your escapade ends here, Tolmony, one way or another. Consider your options.

Tolmony: Alexander, you know what that Wondertainment gizmo you bargained for does, but do you know why it was recalled?

Alexander: What are you—

Tolmony: Multiple building collapses when it was applied to loadbearing walls.

Tolmony: In other words, the things it's applied to— They become pretty fragile.

[Dr. Tolmony swings the tablet hard against the stone wall. It shatters to pieces.]

[Several seconds pass in silence.]

[Alexander lowers his weapon.]

[A moment passes. The wind howls.]

Alexander: Tolmony… Tolmony…

[Alexander laughs slightly.]

Alexander: Well, you got me.

[Alexander slumps to his knees and drops the pistol at his side.]

[A small pool of blood grows where Alexander kneels.]

[Dr. Tolmony relaxes her whiteknuckle grip on her knife. She lowers her weapon and moves forward.]

[Dr. Tolmony kneels beside Alexander and retrieves a roll of bandages from her pocket.]

Tolmony: Hey, here, uh.

[Alexander wordlessly removes his vest. He attempts to take off his buttonup shirt, though his movements appear weak.]

[Dr. Tolmony uses her knife to cut through the buttons and remove the shirt herself. She begins applying bandages to Alexander's wound.]

Alexander: That shirt wasn't cheap, you know.

[Alexander smiles and stifles a chuckle. Dr. Tolmony lets out a single laugh.]

[Dr. Tolmony finishes bandaging Alexander's side.]

Tolmony: You think this'll hold?

Alexander: I've lived through worse. Hurts like a bitch, though.

Tolmony: Er, yeah… Sorry about that.

Alexander: You could've done worse. I would've in your shoes.

Tolmony: Eh, I'm not sure about that. But whatever. It's— What's done is done, right?

Alexander: Not much I can do now.

[Dr. Tolmony sits beside Alexander.]

Tolmony: Yeah, uh… Guess this is it, isn't it?

Tolmony: This all went on so long, I… even at the end I wasn't sure I'd do it.

Alexander: Well, here you are.

Tolmony: Yeah. Yeah, here I am.

Tolmony: Our lives are fucked now.

Alexander: I see this as more of a neutral change, really.

Alexander: You weren't happy before anyway, were you?

Tolmony: No. No, I— I always just thought I wanted to settle down. Rest.

Tolmony: Feels like that ship has sailed. But I guess it did a long time ago.

[Alexander points at Dr. Tolmony's chest.]

Alexander: When you got that?

Tolmony: I mean, yeah, but not for the reasons you're probably thinking. I— You know how 81 is. Remote enough I could still live at home close to the site. Just had to take some precautions to protect the veil during my commute. I was never the most social anyway.

Tolmony: The weird hole in my chest happened from a lover. It, uh, wasn't from any world I know about. Pretty large, not really human, but it… cared.

Tolmony: I guess I'll spare you the details, but there was an unrelated breach, the uh, blackout incident, you probably heard of it.

Alexander: I was there for it, Tolmony. I was just in a different sector at the time.

Tolmony: Right, well… I took it home with me. Phalanx. It has a name.

Alexander: You loved this 'Phalanx' but still refer to them like an object?

Tolmony: That's just, uh, how it preferred being talked about, I guess.

Tolmony: Couldn't stay forever, though. It was just— I would've been implicated, so it…

Tolmony: Staged me aiding a patrol in recapturing it. Throw off the scent, I guess.

Tolmony: Haven't really felt… cared for much since.

Alexander: Hm. I assume you don't share that too much?

Tolmony: I'm a fan of not being executed, so, uh, yeah.

Alexander: I have my own regrets. With all of this.

Alexander: And I guess it was for nothing now.

Tolmony: What did you even… do?

Alexander: Those men down there are the same as the ones you were sitting with last time we met, Tolmony.

Alexander: This world was doomed anyway, so I…

Tolmony: Christ, man.

Alexander: The tablet doesn't lie, Doctor.

Tolmony: Well, it doesn't say anything now.

Alexander: I guess not.

Alexander: It's all I can do to make amends and move on. But I don't expect forgiveness here.

Alexander: I don't deserve it, either.

Alexander: Their Administrator was humanity's hope in this world. I put her down myself as my bargain to get that tablet.

Tolmony: Fuck. You really were hellbent on that plan.

[Alexander nods and bears a solemn expression.]

Alexander: I just wanted the still living worlds to be better ones. That's all.

Alexander: If the tablet is destroyed, and this world is still so… You know…

Alexander: Maybe I'll return when this injury's healed. I'm one guy, I can't fix this mess, but… Maybe I could get some people out of it.

Alexander: Not working with the Foundation anymore mind you, not that they would agree anyway after… Just, even under the circumstances of this world… I'd prefer to work individually.

[Alexander sighs.]

Alexander: You know what you're going to do now?

Tolmony: Well, first and foremost, I have someone to thank for my continued existence who's trapped in a hangar in D1 right now.

Alexander: Who's that?

Tolmony: Nobody.

Alexander: Hm?

Tolmony: As in, the guy named Nobody, the— You know what I mean. Don't act coy.

[Alexander nods.]

Alexander: I guess you'll want these, then?

[Alexander retrieves SCP-9669-2 from his pocket and hands it to Dr. Tolmony.]

Tolmony: Yeah. Uh, thanks.

[There's silence for a moment.]

Tolmony: I guess I have one other friend to pay a visit to as well. Not much better to do.

Tolmony: I'm going to need time to figure out where to even go after all this.

Alexander: If you'd like a hand keeping yourself somewhere safe, it's the least I can do after all I put you through.

Tolmony: Despite everything… I wouldn't mind staying in touch.

Alexander: I was half expecting someone from the Foundation to try hunting me down. Wasn't expecting that someone to be a friend by the end of things.

Tolmony: Well, take what you can get, right?

Alexander: All you can do.

[Several moments pass in silence.]

Alexander: Well, I've got enough gas to get us to the nearest town if we're lucky.

Alexander: Let's just…

Tolmony: Yeah. No use sitting here. You're gonna need stitches at the least.

Alexander: No kidding.

[Dr. Tolmony stands and offers Alexander a hand getting up.]

Tolmony: You can lean on me if you want. It's, uh, whole lot of steps to climb down.

[Dr. Tolmony coughs then looks down. She reaches to turn off the camera.]

[END LOG]
