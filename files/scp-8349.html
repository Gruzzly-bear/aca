Entrance to Facility 149-3's West Library prior to SCP-8349's appearance.

Special Containment Procedures: The entrance to the West Library is locked behind Level 3 security clearance. Two Security Officers are to be stationed outside the entrance to SCP-8349, operating on rotating shifts to ensure that the gateway is surveilled at all times. Individuals entering SCP-8349 may only enter once a week with permission from Area administration, and Dr. Paige Turner is not to be given access under any circumstances.

Description: SCP-8349 is an unidentified extradimensional Y-space within Network Area-149’s Facility 149-3.1 SCP-8349 is accessed via a Class-W transportation gateway in the doorway leading to the Narrativistics & Pataphysics Branch West Library, thus rendering a sizable portion of the facility inaccessible.

Exploration missions into the space have been successful, but recording equipment is permanently damaged upon exiting SCP-8349. The following addenda include descriptions of SCP-8349's contents, and should be reviewed before entry.

Interview Log 8349.1:

Interviewed: Dr. Turner2
Interviewer: Area-149 Director Brian Stone

<Begin Log, 6/13/20 5:50AM>
STONE: Alright Turner, tell me the story.
TURNER: Well, this morning, I was in my office, drinking coffee and trying to catch up to the day since I had woken up, like, 3 hours before I usually do. And I got a call on my office phone from one of the Junior Researchers who said they found a cart of books in the West Library with a note attached that said “Paige please sort these.” I immediately went “Oh, yeah, I was supposed to do that yesterday,” and headed down there. Something to do while my brain wakes up.
STONE: Do you remember who the caller was?
TURNER: Wasn't awake enough for that.
STONE: Continue.
TURNER: When I got down there, I opened the door and could not see a thing. It was legitimately pitch-black, and the lights are on a sensor and should turn on once you open the door, right? But they didn’t.
STONE: Containment team told me they’re still not on.
TURNER: Are they? Huh. So I head in, ‘cause I know the place like the back of my hand and know there’s the emergency light switch in the back. But as soon as my foot squished on wet grass, I ran and told the next person I saw.
STONE: See anyone in there? Or anything out of the ordinary?
TURNER: Soon as the light from the hallway ran out, I couldn’t see anything. If there was anyone or anything there, I wouldn’t be able to tell you.
<End Log>

Afterword: Director Stone promptly declared the West Library Hallway a restricted area, and Dr. Turner was given permission to begin documenting SCP-8349.

+ Addendum 8349.1
- Addendum 8349.1: Excerpt From Dr. Turner’s Exploration Report, 6/13/20

I found myself in the most beautiful garden I’ve ever seen, full of dark blue tulips. The grass was covered in a layer of water, and the scent of recent rain hung in the air. In hindsight, I suppose it was strange that such a large garden didn’t have much variety in its color palette, but in the moment it felt quite comforting. It reminded me of a place I had gone to with my family when I was young, but I can’t for the life of me remember where that was.

Interspersed with the hedge walls and wooden gates were bookshelves from the Library, but they became less frequent as I trekked onwards. I pulled a few books from the shelves, but opening them caused the letters on the page to fall out like hair clippings at the salon. One of the books had fallen off a high shelf and nearly hit me on the head, a large leatherbound tome which - judging by its thickness and weight - I can only presume to be a dictionary or something of that ilk. I would have loved to continue further, but one glance at my watch told me that it was time to head back. I had a surprisingly easy time finding my exit, although I have suspicions that I didn’t retrace my steps perfectly.

+ Addendum 8349.2
- Addendum 8349.2: SCP-8349-A

I met a strange creature while inside this time. Multiple, actually. I tried opening a book that had fallen from the shelves, but the cover refused to budge. It was once I heard a shrill screech from inside that I threw it to the ground and ran around the corner.

Group of dormant SCP-8349-A entities.

SCP-8349-A is the designation given to sapient entities found within SCP-8349. SCP-8349-A instances resemble blank sheets of paper, varying in size and wear. Groups of instances may gather to tear out pages of books and replace them with themselves, allowing the group to rest for varying amounts of time. SCP-8349-A entities feed on the pages of written books, and have no desire to consume standard food or water.

SCP-8349-A instances have demonstrated a wide range of emotional displays towards Foundation personnel. Many are ostensibly eager to show operatives an object or place, usually a particular book or bookshelf. All known instances are non-hostile until provoked either verbally or through physical force, in which case they will attempt to use the thin edges of their bodies to inflict cuts on a perceived threat.

They're quite adorable actually. And smart, too. A few of them led me to that same heavy book that almost hit me the other day, and I was finally able to open it. I think it's the first of these books that actually has the letters stay in place rather than shifting all around. I can't remember exactly what it said, but it was some sort of fantasy story about a chosen one destined to slay a dragon. I'm excited to read the rest next time.

+ Addendum 8349.4
- Addendum 8349.4: Excerpt From Dr. Turner’s Exploration Report, 6/20/20

It was perhaps the most thrilling day of my life. I found myself looking up at this massive paper dragon, just about the biggest living thing I've ever seen. It was just going to town on these books that had been piled up on the roof of the castle, tearing them up as if its teeth were made of steel. Every time I tried to approach it, its little crumpled paper servants would try and beat me back, hurling books and sometimes themselves at my head.

And just when I stepped on top of its stash, the dragon knocked me away with a flip of its wing. I don't think it even noticed me. I took a tumble all the way back down to the ground, landing in a convenient pile of shredded paper.

If I really am this destined hero, then you can good and goddamn believe I'm coming back to kill this thing.

+ Addendum 8349.5
- Addendum 8349.5: Audio Log, 6/22/20

AUDIO LOG

Foreword: Dr. Crystal Veejay entered SCP-8349 at 2:15PM, after Director Brian Stone inquired about Dr. Turner's whereabouts. Audio was captured by sending a constant radio signal to equipment in the West Library Hallway, which returned the first intact recording from within SCP-8349.

[BEGIN LOG]

<Segment cut for brevity>

VEEJAY: (yelling) Paige! Christ, what are you doing?!

(Loud sounds of paper rustling at regular intervals on recording.)

TURNER: (yelling) Veejay? Is that you down there?

VEEJAY: Yes, it's me! Everyone's wondering where you are!

TURNER: Crys, look at me! I didn't just kill a dragon; I tamed it!

VEEJAY: That's rather impressive, but you should know this is getting ridiculous! Did Stone approve this?

TURNER: Since when did you care so much about procedure? This is (unintelligible)

VEEJAY: You know what I care about? I care that you've been spending so much time in here instead of out there. Come to think of it, when was the last time I saw you around the building? When was the last time we chatted at the coffee machine? You're missing out on a big social event as we speak!

TURNER: What harm could it do?

VEEJAY: Think of all the things and people that need you, that's the harm it could do! I know the world kinda sucks right now, and I know we've got tough jobs, and I know you (unintelligible), but this is in no way healthy. You're coming with me, and we're going to go see our friends.

(Rustling sounds begin to slow, before stopping completely.)

TURNER: I have friends here! They told me about a new adventure, a-

VEEJAY: Paige, living sheets of paper are not your friends. Neither is this… thing you're riding. This isn't the real world, and the real world is the only one that needs saving. There's plenty to do out there. People miss you.

(Silence on recording for 5 seconds.)

VEEJAY: C'mon. I saved you a cupcake, like you asked.

[END LOG]

Afterword: Dr. Turner and Dr. Veejay both exited SCP-8349 without injury. Dr. Turner began attending weekly therapy sessions with an on-site psychiatrist at Dr. Veejay's recommendation.
