SCP-8177

Item #: SCP-8177

Object Class: Safe

Special Containment Procedures: Due to SCP-8177-2's technical success, mass production for all Foundation holding areas has begun.

Description: SCP-8177-1 refers to a collection of 13 electroshock-based devices designed by the Central Intelligence Agency for the purpose of extracting information directly from the subconscious of prisoners during the Vietnam War, one of which was later reconstructed by the Department of Purgatorial Spaces.

Twelve instances of SCP-8177-1 are not functional, after seemingly having been destroyed. However, a prototype model was found within Phu Quoc Prison, Vietnam. Said instance was promptly brought to Site-219, where the Department of Purgatorial Spaces began reverse-engineering the device, creating a copycat model (designated SCP-8177-2) for Foundation use.

Subjects connected to SCP-8177-2 undergo electrical stimulation, precisely modulated to alter the neural pathways inside the prefrontal cortex based on the prompt entered into SCP-8177-2's primary console. This creates an emulated metaphysical space in one's 'mind', mimicking vivid memories. Once activated, subjects will begin to involuntarily narrate the events they underwent during the duration of the aforementioned memories.

SCP-8177-2 is linked to Athena.V13, an AIC specially designed to interpret neurological activity and transcript it.

Initial Testing: Testing is to begin November 1, 1985 on a Chaos Insurgent, with the prompt "Why I was at Site-18."

Subject: C.I. Field Agent Amelia Cortez (25).
Project Manager: Sr. Researcher J. ██████
Item: SCP-8177

UNKNOWN: Now, when I activate this, it's going to feel strange at first.

<UNKNOWN> checks the papers on his clipboard.

UNKNOWN: It says here that it might feel like you're falling, and then it all goes dark. Think about it like a dream! Darkness, darkness, and then light. You'll wake up as your past self, you won't even know you're looking at a memory.

UNKNOWN: Test 1, beginning in 3… 2… 1…

Subject: Gunfire goes off from the end of the corridor. A facility guard hits TANGO directly in the temple before we can open fire.

NOTICE: THE DEPARTMENT OF PURGATORIAL SPACES

Nov 6, 1985

We have come across a severe design flaw regarding SCP-8177-2's ability to return a subject's consciousness to their physical body, leaving them in a vegetative state following the de-activation of SCP-8177-2. Furthermore; electroencephalogram results have shown that the effects of SCP-8177-2 seemingly continue to occur on repeat whilst in said state. The reason for this is unknown.

After a short period of review, we do not believe that these circumstances justify the decommissioning of SCP-8177-2. Effective immediately, SCP-8177-2 is to be integrated into the standard interrogation procedures for the purpose of extracting information from unwilling, uncooperative, or untrustworthy detainees.
