9480.0.1 - An instance of SCP-9480-A-2 as scanned and catalogued by Project Alexandria

Special Containment Procedures: The cover story concerning the mineshaft containing SCP-9480 is to be maintained until SCP-9480 is deemed safe to relocate. Any trespassers to the mineshaft are to be treated with an appropriate amount of force and treated with amnestics as not to arouse rumours or suspicion.

Description: SCP-9480 is a hollow, vaguely missile-shaped object measuring roughly 20 x 10 x 50 metres composed largely of graphite and diamond. The interior of SCP-9480 is mostly empty, barring a structure loosely resembling a bookshelf along the eastern wall and an additional structure at the rear. This bookshelf-esque structure is designated SCP-9480-A-1 and it is positioned between two black 2 x 10 metre rectangular portals (collectively named SCP-9480-A-0) at the front and back of SCP-9480 respectively.

SCP-9480-A-1 is of indeterminate length1, but only 50m of it is visible at any one time. The remainder is seemingly contained within a pocket dimension (hereafter SCP-9480-A) beginning at either of the afforementioned SCP-9480-A-0 instances. SCP-9480-A-1 is able to slide in the direction of either instance, allowing for different sections to become visible. Attempting to transport living beings across either instance of SCP-9480-A-0 invariably causes instant loss of function to all living faculties and further testing of this kind is suspended indefinitely.

The original contents of the shelf are a series of tablets designated as SCP-9480-A-2 and SCP-9480-A-3. Instances of SCP-9480-A-2 are composed of a durable, unidentified dark grey material, and marked with an unknown golden ink-like substance, in a series of presumed diagrams and a set of repeated symbols believed to be a script. If removed from SCP-9480-A, instances will undergo a process of slow exponential decay into a similar mix of carbon allotropes that make up SCP-9480 that causes them to lose their markings. Instances which degrade enough to lose their markings entirely are desginated SCP-9480-A-3.

The rear structure inside SCP-9480 resembles a statue of a member of the class Caudoviricetes2, although not any one species in particular. However it does resemble many of the diagrams recorded within Project Alexandria3.

Discovery: SCP-9480 was first brought to the Foundation’s attention on 16/06/2021, after discovery by miners outside the town of Kimberley, South Africa. After amnesticization of the locals, the mine was seized under falsified safety concerns with permission from the South African government. Carbon-dating of the graphite portions places the origins of SCP-9480 in the Pre-Cambrian, likely towards the early Archean.

9480.0.2 - Another instance of SCP-9480-A-2. Partial Translation - "█████ give eternally ███████████ take eternally ████"

The Wanderer’s Library was contacted for materials similar to SCP-9480-A-2 by a covert proxy4 who provided us with very few results before communications ceased. The likelihood of another proxy asking about said materials within The Wanderer’s Library without arousing suspicion is unlikely.

Agent Watts provided us with copies of 7 poems, all part of an anthology believed to describe a creation myth. The myth describes many species following one after another, where the last of one species becomes the god of the next. The anthology appears to be written in the same set of symbols as instances of SCP-9480-A-2, alongside direct translations into languages known to the Foundation5. These materials list the original language as "Icosagenian", a name matching a long-extinct, extradimensional parasitic species of which very little is known, other than the species' striking resemblance to both Caudoviricetes and diagrams shown on SCP-9480-A-2, and a purported creation myth very similar to what the poem describes.

Due to these similarities, it is assumed that the Icosagenian language found in the poems and the symbols found on instances of SCP-9480-A-2 are the same language, and under this assumption translations of select words and phrases have been obtained. Of specific interest was an instance of SCP-9480-A-2, with only a direct excerpt from one of the poems written on it:

Brave pilot, go forth and create. Build yourself out of clay, lay down in the dirt, and rest. No one is waiting. Everyone is waiting.

Said instance was placed in the middle of an unusually empty section of SCP-9480-A-1, with extra structure built to secure it in place.

9480.0.3 - The relevant instance of SCP-9480-A-2. No direct translation.

The phrase shown on this instance of SCP-9480-A-2 is not believed to have a human-language parallel. Based on translators notes from the Wanderer's Library documents, the first half of the phrase roughly translates to "artificial" and the second half of the phrase was a word Icosagenians used to categorise any lifeforms they used as their hosts (being a parasitic species). From this it can be surmised the diagram depicts a form of "artificial prey" or geneticallly engineered livestock.
