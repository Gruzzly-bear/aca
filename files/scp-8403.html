Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8403 is kept in a windowless standard humanoid containment cell. To prevent misuse, all experimentation interactions must involve the entire project team, including the team lead.

Description: SCP-8403 is an adult female humanoid of average height and unknown origin. It has dark brown hair and appears to be of Middle Eastern descent.

SCP-8403’s primary anomalous property is its ability to be manipulated. These manipulations can only be brought about by an individual who maintains direct visual contact and is within 20 10 5 meters of SCP-8403. Observed changes thus far have been entirely physical in nature, though it is theorized that other aspects of SCP-8403, including its psychological, temporal, and existential nature1, can also be manipulated.

SCP-8403's secondary anomalous properties consist of near-total partial translucency and intangibility. Light that passes through it is scattered and produces a vague defined shadow. Tactile contact is analogous to moving through a low high density fluid medium, such as oil mercury.

These anomalous properties are likely a natural result of the entity’s Hume level2, which is significantly below that of a baseline human’s. As a result, SCP-8403 is considered mostly slightly detached from baseline reality, which allows for entities with higher Hume levels (including baseline humans) to exert their influence on SCP-8403 in a similar manner to ontokinetic or reality bending abilities, though this influence is entirely limited to SCP-8403 itself and cannot affect its surroundings.

It has demonstrated the ability to communicate fluently in English.

Addendum 8403-1: Initial Interview

Date: 02/04/2011
Interviewers: Senior Researcher Milton Hale, Project Lead and Head Researcher
Researcher Luzia Mensing, Applied Ontokinesis Specialist
Junior Researcher Alfie Xiao, Assistant

<BEGIN LOG>

SCP-8403, who appears largely translucent, is attempting to perform a handstand; however, it falls over as the researchers file into the room.

Hale: Good morning, SCP-8403.

Hale flips through his notebook, Mensing inspects the entity, and Xiao sets up recording apparatuses.

SCP-8403: Oh I'm just peachy, but my room’s way too bland, isn't it? Feels like I'm practically a rat in a cage. I was thinking I could use some cool paintings by the door, above my bed, on the ceiling…

Hale: I see. I’ll pass on your requests to the relevant department, SCP-8403.

SCP-8403: I’ve got a name, by the way. Not sure what this S-C-P stuff is about.

Hale: I understand, but unfortunately, policies dictate that I must refer to you by your designation.

SCP-8403: Well, it's not a big deal. I'm a bit of a special case because, believe it or not…

It cups a hand around its mouth.

SCP-8403: I'm actually a ghost. Boo.

Hale: Mm. Do you remember what caused you to become a ghost?

SCP-8403: Honestly, no idea. I'm not even a good ghost—I've got no chains to rattle, no dolls to possess, no people to haunt. That's what all the real ghosts are supposed to do, right? Oh, I know! How 'bout I start by haunting you?

It points at Xiao, who is standing by the wall with both hands on his clipboard.

Xiao: M-Me?

SCP-8403: Yeah, one-hundred-percent you. You're practically shaking already. It'll be a lot more fun to haunt you than ol' doc or miss grumpy over here.

It glances at Mensing, who frowns, tapping her foot on the floor. Xiao hides behind his clipboard while Hale holds up a hand.

Hale: No. There will be no hauntings of our personnel.

SCP-8403: Why not? It feels like the appropriate thing to do. Don't you want to find out what happens if I haunt someone?

Hale: I… no, I don't think so.

SCP-8403: It could provide some really useful insight into how I work! And it'll just be a small haunting, like pushing cups around and whispering about spiders at night. I think it's important that I start acting like a proper ghost already.

Hale: I… suppose I could consider it…

Mensing: Dr. Hale, we're wasting pre—

She trails off into a yawn, which is mimicked by SCP-8403.

Mensing: Excuse me. I meant to say that we're wasting precious time.

Hale: Why, it seems I let myself get sidetracked. Quite the voluble one, this anomaly.

SCP-8403: Anomaly? What's that?

He straightens his tie.

Hale: SCP-8403, are you aware of your primary anomalous property?

SCP-8403: What? I'm in a jail cell, I don't own any property.

Hale: Not quite. I'm referring to how… remind me how it was discovered, again?

Mensing: I believe it was found harassing civilians.

SCP-8403: Woah, hold on now, I did nothing of the sort. It was uh… hm. My memory's kinda fuzzy, I'll be honest.

Xiao: Um, she was—I mean, it was letting kids change its hair, the file said. Into different colors and hairstyles.

SCP-8403: Oh yeah, that's right! Woo, it's all coming back now. You know I had lilac braids at one point? Can I get those back?

Hale: That property, yes. Why are others able to alter your physiology?

SCP-8403: My what?

Xiao: Your appearance.

SCP-8403: I dunno, isn't that what you guys are here to find out? That's what the cops told me would happen. I mean, I want to know the answer too.

Mensing: Sir, that means it doesn't know anything.

SCP-8403: Well, I wouldn't say anything. Hey, I can tell you the colors of the rainbow! Red, orange, yellow—

Hale: That's quite alright, SCP-8403.

He turns to the other researchers.

Hale: Fortunately, even if it cannot provide meaningful information, I do expect exciting results from experimentation with its properties. Shall we leave to prepare?

Mensing: Yes.

SCP-8403 tilts its head.

Xiao: But don't we still have most of our allotted time remaining? Can't we keep talking to the ghost in case, um, it knows something else?

SCP-8403: Wait, what was that about experiments?

Hale: Why, I suspect we'll get little more than its recount of the visible light spectrum.

Mensing sighs.

Mensing: That is to say, Alfie, part of interacting with anomalies capable of speech is knowing when to end the interview early. This is something you'll learn through experience. If there is nothing to be gained, our time is always better spent elsewhere.

Hale: Indeed, Luzia, especially when it involves the time of three researchers!

SCP-8403: Hey, but I want to keep talking! Don't I get a say in this? Maybe Doctor Alfie can stay back and we'll chit-chat until his ears pop and fall off. Doesn't that sound fun?

Xiao raises his hands to his ears.

Xiao: Wh-Why would you say it that way? Also, I'm not a doctor…

SCP-8403: You're not? But just look at you.

Hale chuckles to himself and shakes his head.

Hale: Let's not dawdle any longer. Come, the sooner we present our proposal, the sooner we begin testing.

Mensing: Alfie?

Xiao: Well, okay…

SCP-8403: Wait, about this testing stuff, why don't we talk about exactly what you're going to—

The researchers exit.

Addendum 8403-2: Project Proposal (Condensed)

Title: Utilizing SCP-8403 as a Medium to Understand Ontokinesis

Researchers: Dr. Milton Hale, Luzia Mensing, Alfie Xao3

Background: Ontokinetic entities, colloquially known as reality benders, possess Hume levels higher than baseline reality. This allows them to manipulate reality at will and makes them notoriously difficult to contain. Even our latest SRA (Scranton Reality Anchor) models are functionally scrap metal against the more powerful types.

Rationale: Due to SCP-8403's primary property, baseline humans can manipulate it through visual contact and imagination alone, as it responds much like how we would to a true ontokinetic entity. As such, SCP-8403 acts as a safe yet valuable learning opportunity, and experimentation with it will undoubtedly advance the Foundation's rudimentary understanding of ontokinesis.

Constraints/Limitations: These interactions may not be a perfect correspondence to how true ontokinetic entities manipulate reality, as they may possess higher intelligence or perceptive capabilities than baseline humans.

Perhaps a detail that is both a constraint and a boon is SCP-8403’s secondary properties. Since it is little more than a specter, it likely cannot produce physical objects, which also significantly reduces the risk involved in testing.

Measures will be taken to ensure SCP-8403’s continued safety and cooperation. Personnel will only be allowed to conduct tests with prior approval from the Ethics Committee.

Addendum 8403-3: Testing Log

The above project proposal was submitted to the Ethics Committee and approved with a 6-3 majority vote. Thereafter, the research team produced a list of proposed manipulations which received partial approval for testing.

Date: 02/18/2011

Tester: Dr. Hale
Procedure: Change its hair color to white.
Result: SCP-8403’s hair lightened over the period of one second until it was fully white, similar to Hale's own. However, this only affected the front half of its hair, i.e. the only section visible from Hale's perspective, and the non-visible section of its hair remained dark brown. The entity did not initially notice this change.
Notes: An incomplete visualization seems to result in an incomplete change.

Tester: Dr. Hale
Procedure: Change its attire to a D-class uniform.
Result: Its shirt and pants morphed into a bright orange one-piece jumpsuit. It expressed disapproval of this change.
Notes: Its anomalous properties seem to extend to its clothing, which also share its physical properties (translucency and intangibility) and as such are likely considered “part” of the entity.

Tester: Mensing
Procedure: Reduce its height by approximately one inch.
Result: SCP-8403 shrank by several inches and appeared slightly slimmer than before. It expressed surprise at its new stature.
Notes: Exact measurements likely cannot be intuitively visualized. Side effects can occur regardless of the specific intent of the change.

Tester: Xiao
Procedure: Manifest a paintbrush in its left hand.
Result: Its left ring finger thickened and morphed until it resembled a small paintbrush. Entity expressed slight distress.
Notes: Manifestation changed the existing body rather than creating a separate object. Whether or not the paintbrush was indeed composed of wood and bristles was unable to be confirmed.

Tester: Xiao
Procedure: Restore its finger.
Result: The paintbrush morphed back into the entity’s ring finger, which appeared mildly discolored.
Notes: Hastily performed manipulations are more prone to error. Xiao was reminded that all restoration was scheduled post-testing.

Tester: Mensing
Procedure: Manifest a small mouse in its left hand.4
Result: A small, eyeless white mouse manifested in the center of SCP-8403’s left hand. It appeared to move its head around and vocalize distress, though its paws were conjoined to the skin of the palm. Despite waving its hand in the air, SCP-8403 failed to dislodge the animal.
Notes: Manifestation of seemingly living entities is feasible. However, it may not be possible to manifest an entirely separate object using SCP-8403 as a medium, as both attempts thus far have resulted in the new mass being fused with the entity itself, despite the tester's intent otherwise. Due to the nature of the procedure and the entity's increasing distress, SCP-8403's hand was restored to its prior state before testing proceeded.

Tester: Xiao
Procedure: Manifest a paintbrush in its hair.
Result: Several strands of the entity’s hair thickened and combined into a paintbrush. The paintbrush, which was seemingly attached to its scalp, broke off, rapidly thinned, and manifested back into hair. SCP-8403 vocalized mild pain and surprise.
Notes: Manipulations which are detached from SCP-8403’s body seem to lose their primary anomalous property, which limits the scope of the entity’s usefulness.

Tester: Dr. Hale
Procedure: Lighten its mood.
Result: The corners of the entity’s lips were pulled upwards and frozen in a “smiling” position. Its general attitude remained unchanged.
Notes: Further investigation needed into achieving emotional changes.

After testing concluded, photographs of SCP-8403 taken prior were used as reference to restore SCP-8403 to its original physical condition. This was done so successfully, though its initial mental state could not be restored.

Based on these findings, the following properties can be surmised about at least low-level ontokinesis performed by a human-level intelligence:

  • Physical manipulations may be the most accessible for human-like minds.
  • Ontokinetic manipulations may weaken when moved farther from the target entity.
  • Visualizing physical signs of an emotional state may be insufficient to manipulate the emotions themselves.

Future testing shall be conducted to validate, strengthen, and expand these findings.

On the same day, Xiao submitted a request for more accommodations to improve the entity's cooperation, which was later approved. SCP-8403’s containment cell was outfitted with several paintings depicting Foundation-approved neutral or uplifting imagery.

SCP-8403: Don't worry about it. Just get me that ghost bottle, pretty please?

Xiao hesitates, biting his lip. A moment later, the entity's fingernails gain a slight purple hue, which it does not notice.

Mensing pauses by the door and turns around with another yawn.

Mensing: Come on. I'll buy you a coffee too.

Xiao: Yes, sure.

Xiao hurriedly follows her out of the cell as SCP-8403 raises a hand to wave goodbye. It gasps and turns its fingers underneath the light, its smile growing wider.

SCP-8403: Ooh, not bad.

Upon returning the next day, Dr. Hale reviewed the prior log and noted multiple minor infractions. Xiao was assigned to mandatory remedial training concerning Foundation standards for professionalism and objectivity, and his unapproved manipulation of SCP-8403's fingernail color was reverted.

Containment procedures were revised accordingly.

Addendum 8403-5: Further Testing Results

Over the next few months, frequent and extensive testing was performed to discover the extent and limitations of ontokinetic manipulations through SCP-8403. However, procedures which involved temperamental, temporal, or existential changes or total transformations of SCP-8403's biology remained banned by the Ethics Committee, despite repeated requests for expansion of the project's scope.

These tests, in addition to validating previous results, produced a variety of findings, which includes:

  • Manipulation requires visual contact with the affected portion of the entity.
  • Some amount of focused intent is required, which lessens the risk of accidental manipulations.
  • Photographs, video recordings, or other attempts to capture SCP-8403’s likeness do not carry its anomalous properties, and viewing them is insufficient to allow for remote manipulation.
  • Abnormal alterations to SCP-8403’s physical form generally cause minimal harm to its physical well-being. It is theorized that its body adapts to these changes to maintain normal function.

The full list of findings was delivered to relevant departments to assist in potential combat and containment applications.

As testing progressed, it was discovered that the maximum effective distance for the entity's primary anomalous property was gradually decreasing over time. These represented reductions of SCP-8403's anomalous properties towards baseline levels. The reason for these changes was unknown, and no method to slow or halt them was discovered.

Addendum 8403-6: Incident Log

On 05/██/2011, SCP-████ breached containment by utilizing its ontokinetic abilities, resulting in the deaths of ██ Foundation personnel and the loss of Site-███.

In response, Mensing and Dr. Hale edited and re-submitted a supplemental project proposal to expand the scope and intensity of SCP-8403’s testing. The proposal reiterated the existential threat of hostile ontokinetic entities as the main impetus for the research and cited current ethical constraints as reason for the recent lack of new findings. Additionally, SCP-8403's physical form had become almost entirely opaque and tangible, which added further urgency to the project's timeline.

On 05/11/2011, the proposal was approved, and testing was scheduled on the same day.

Xiao stomps his heel onto her toes. Mensing yelps and punches him, and he falls onto his back, clutching a hand to his nose. She recoils.

Mensing: Shit!

Hale: Wh—Th… You’ve just struck a fellow researcher!

Hale presses his thumb to the back of his lanyard while Mensing fiercely shakes her head.

Mensing: It was self-defense! Everything was self-defense!

She raises her injured foot and winces.

Xiao: G-Goddammit I need to—

Hale: Luzia, we have established procedures in place for dealing with—

Mensing: I'm sorry but what’s done is done, alright? S-Someone had to take the first step.

She hobbles over to the bundle of fallen clothes, where SCP-8403 has managed to poke its head out. Mensing kneels down and lifts the entity, which squeaks and squirms ineffectively within its former clothing.

Mensing: Think about what we can learn from a completely transformed humanoid under entirely controlled conditions.

Hale adjusts his spectacles.

Hale: I… suppose this does hold potential. It seems unable to communicate, but is it still sapient? Can such a small animal even contain a human-level intelligence? Perhaps we can…

He swallows.

Hale: …no, still, the Committee will have our heads for this.

Mensing: Dr. Hale, with all due respect, why do you let the Ethics board control you? What have they ever done for you?

Hale: Truly, nobody ever enjoys being beholden to oversight committees, but one must learn to obey the rules and regulations they have sworn to.

Mensing: No, no, enough of that. The Committee has failed us. We need to stop wasting our fucking time bickering over trifles so that we can focus on what matters: people who have had their lives ripped away and stuffed into the shells of beasts. You haven't forgotten, have you?

Hale: I… remember your file, Luzia. However, research is a gradual process, and there are procedures we must follow regardless of our personal motivations.

She stands up holding the wriggling bundle of clothes.

Mensing: I'll bear the brunt of the fallout. I don't care if I get arrested or worse, but this research needs to be done right now.

Xiao stumbles towards her.

Xiao: Let me just… turn her back. You’ve done enough already.

Hale: This has gone on too far. Do what the boy says.

Mensing: Really? What kind of scientists are you? All I'm asking for is an hour of—

The cell door hisses open and a site security team bursts into the room. Mensing turns just as Xiao tackles her to the ground, which causes her to release SCP-8403; it immediately attempts to bite her, but a swipe of her hand sends it tumbling away.

Mensing: Alfie, you—

The guards intervene and pull the two apart. One lifts Xiao onto their back, another handcuffs a struggling Mensing, and the last holds SCP-8403 down with gloved hands.

Security Officer: Anything we should be aware of with this anomaly, doc?

Hale shakes his head slowly, his brow furrowed in thought.

Hale: No… nothing.

Mensing: Listen to me! All I need is an hour—no, even just ten minutes! We need this research, goddammit!

She is forcefully escorted out of the cell. Xiao coughs, struggling to get down. His nose is bleeding.

Xiao: W-wait, no, we need to turn 8403 back!

The officer carrying him glances at Hale, who remains silent.

Security Officer: After the debriefing, researcher.

Xiao: You don’t understand, she’s supposed to be human!

They exit the cell, and Xiao's protests fade away. The last officer in the room releases SCP-8403, which attempts to escape but is obstructed by the door sliding shut.

<END LOG>

Following the incident, Researcher Luzia Mensing was reassigned to Provisional Site-███ with autonomous project privileges suspended for 12 months. No further corrective action was deemed necessary.

The Ethics Committee passed a decree with the following provisions:

  • SCP-8403 is to be restored to its prior humanoid form.
  • Further testing with SCP-8403 is forbidden until a new project proposal is approved.

After all post-incident interviews were completed and Xiao was administered basic medical care, he was allowed to return to SCP-8403’s cell to fulfill the first provision. However, it was discovered that no forms of manipulation were effective anymore due to it having become fully corporeal.
