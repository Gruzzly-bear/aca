Reinstated Agent Cody Sparkovsky with local cattle

Special Containment Procedures: All aurochs1 are under surveillance by provisional research team “Homestead” within separate housing containers on-site. Experimentation to artificially reproduce the primary effect of SCP-8646 and all attempts to breed on-site cattle have failed.

Description: SCP-8646 is the spontaneous appearance of Bos primigenius2 within a 100 kilometer radius centered around the Amish settlement of Stolton, Texas. This effect occurs during spring following lights in the sky comparable to Aurora Borealis upon a hill near Stolton. The rate of appearance within local herds of dairy cattle is estimated to be approximately one aurochs per 100 head of cattle; as such, there are five identified and contained living aurochs within the radius.

FINAL MISSION REPORT

DATE: 5/19/2018
NAME: Cody Sparkovsky, Agent, Reinstated

SUMMARY: Loss of life on-site has risked the Foundation's secrecy in the region. Furthermore, general knowledge and deviant interest into the anomaly within the region by normal civilians has warranted immediate action to uphold the Veil and protect the anomaly. As such, command has determined immediate amnesticization and relocation to be of high imperative. MTF Phi-Eohl have proven useful as always in removing and relocating civilians in the region seamlessly. Following aggression from local ranchers and the displacement of the community, at least two Foundation agents will be designated to dedicated surveillance to prevent remigration and encroaching efforts into Stolton, Texas for the following calendar year or until further notice. Amnestics followed by reintegration into normalcy have been authorized for civilians that enter within the 100-km radius of the effect for their protection; force has been authorized against bad actors looking to retrieve specimens within the radius.

PROGRESS: Further research into the anomaly has been greenlit by site command. Research provisions have included a plan for reintegration of the local population if possible. Projected likelihood of this occurring in current Foundation models is less than 4%. Research will press forward.

VISIBILITY: Use of force in order to protect local populace has risked Foundation security in multiple instances, currently mitigated by MTF Phi-Eohl. Amnestics have been administered as deemed necessary. Veil has been upheld.
