Special Containment Procedures: The building containing SCP-9379's entrance is under the control of Foundation front company Stein Construction and Property. Personnel guarding the building have been informed that SCP-9379 is an immobile but inconsequential minor anomaly. The land above SCP-9379 proper is already owned by the Foundation and thus already inaccessible to the public. As SCP-9379 is immobile and defunct, no further containment procedures are necessary. Individuals aware of the true nature of SCP-9379 are to be terminated as soon as feasible, except as decreed by the O5 Council.

Description: SCP-9379 is an abandoned underground facility, the entrance to which is located in an otherwise ordinary apartment in Houston, TX. This entrance was sealed, but has since been opened from the inside via a combination of potent alchemical acids and brute force. The entrance's design is consistent with those found in Foundation heavy containment facilities built between 1977 and 2003, despite the lack of records of this facility's construction.1 A plaque reading "Department of Abnormalities" is installed above the entrance. This Department does not exist.2

SCP-9379 proper appears to have been a testing facility when in use. It contains 7 cells apparently used for containment, as well as holding cells for D-class and other personnel. Fire and acid damage is present throughout the facility, but this appears to have occurred after it was abandoned.

Addendum 17/7/2021:
Following the authorization of the use of explosives, the door to Cell 7 was successfully breached. Inside, an empty box and a hole in the wall were discovered. The box emits trace amounts of Tartarean radiation. The hole leads to the sole empty D-class cell, the other side being hidden behind the bed, and contains residue from various food products. Several papers are strewn across the room. Most have been burnt or otherwise rendered illegible, but three remain readable.
