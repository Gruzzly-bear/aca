SCP-9292

Item #: SCP-9292

Object Class: Euclid

Special Containment Procedures: For containment procedures for specific objects within SCP-9292, see document 9292-PROCEDURES. To minimize complexity, items from SCP-9292 are to be kept in-situ or, if necessary, contained in Provisional Facility 9292.

Staff entering SCP-9292 must always follow instructions given by 9292 experts (currently Doctor Persephone Nightingale, Coroner Linn Conradi, and Field Specialist Ravn Laake). Staff should not touch any human-made objects within SCP-9292 outside of testing or approved exploration. This notably includes, but is not limited to:

  • Roads
  • Tiles
  • Fences
  • Walls
  • Doors

Animal life within the borders of SCP-9292 is to be avoided where possible. Plantlife must not be moved outside of SCP-9292 under any circumstances.

General warning:
Do not contact the two asphalt roads in SCP-9292 in any way. Do not step on the roads.

Description: SCP-9292 is the small agricultural village of Innset, Norway. SCP-9292 lies on the south bank of the Faxälven river in Finnmark, consists of 36 significant man-made structures, and as of 2022 it had a population of 17 individuals. SCP-9292 includes 8 domiciles, a small grocery store, a chapel, a graveyard, and a civic center.

Every single human-made object thus far examined in SCP-9292 is anomalous in nature, as are most naturally occurring objects (wildlife, plantlife, rocks, etc). Objects in SCP-9292 have shown a wide variety of anomalous properties, both hazardous and benign. All such objects are referred to as "artefacts" by SCP-9292 researchers, to clearly distinguish SCP-9292 objects from non-anomalous items brought to the village from outside.

Artefacts have variously featured properties that are ontokinetic, ectoentropic, essophysical, memetic, extra-dimensional, pataphysical, thaumaturgical, or tychekinetic in nature. There is no definite pattern to the anomalies, and no singular origin for SCP-9292's nature has been discovered. While all artefacts were created or gained their anomalous properties between 2017 and 2023, the source of these properties may be unique in each case.

A primary goal of research is to understand the origin of SCP-9292.

Artefact 00001 — The North Barn

Artefact: 20m by 12m wooden barn, painted falun red. The structure has been cleaned from any agricultural grime, and the words "Pleasure and Pain" have been graffiti'd on the front door.

Anomaly: When inside A00001, chronic pain symptoms are significantly decreased.

Notes: I wish I could make this place my office, or bedroom. —Nightingale

Artefact 00002 — Sneezing Man

Artefact: Marcus Doe, a 26-year old man from Delaware. Known member of GoI-0267, the individual was found emaciated on the floor of A00001. Due to regular convulsions, intravenous therapy has not been possible, but the subject has not yet expired from malnutrition. Attempts to interview A00002 have been unsuccessful.

Anomaly: A00002 sneezes loudly every 3 to 7 seconds. Attempts to stop this behavior have not been successful. The incessant sneezing leaves A00002 awake, out-of-breath, and unable to consume food.

Notes: Doe was only wearing a wool sweater with the following text knitted into it: "Untitled". I must interpret the poor guy's condition as somesort of artistic statement. —Conradi

Artefact 00003 — Central body

Artefact: The naked corpse of Laurenz Bronner, a German painter who disappeared 2020. He is taxidermied in the center of the room, looking upwards with his arms akimbo.

Anomaly: When coming into contact with A00003, a subject is struck with a wave of extreme orgasmic pleasure. Thus far, one staff member has died to a myocardial infarction upon touching the subject.

Notes: This seems really crude, even for AWCY. What's worse is that Bronner looks genuinely handsome. Extremely disturbing. —Conradi

Artefact 00004 — Painting of the Sun

Artefact: A pointillist painting depicting a portion of the sun, on a 210cm by 210cm canvas.

Anomaly: A00004 emits light at ~1,000,000 candela, bright enough to blind observers and warm up the room.

Notes: Maybe one of the most gorgeous sights I've ever seen, but not quite worth looking at without eclipse goggles. Just a glance burned me so bad it caused me an instant migraine. —Nightingale

Artefact 00006 — Chemical toilet

Artefact: Blue chemical toilet located just outside the building. While portions of the inside are damaged by thrashing, the toilet is unused.

Anomaly: When an individual sits down in A00006, all urine in their bladder and upper urinary tract is compacted and transmogrified into small crystalized pieces, functioning as kidney stones. The subject's need to urinate typically increases, despite the sudden decrease of liquids.

Notes: No comment. —Nightingale

Artefact 00009 — Photo booth

Artefact: A modified 2012 Danish photo booth, with its curtain removed. The phrase "EVERYTHING IS RELATIVE" is painted on its side.

Anomaly: When a subject takes a seat in A00009, a five-second countdown immediately begins on the booth's digital screen. A subject can leave the booth before the countdown concludes to cancel its anomalous effects. Once the countdown concludes, the screen turns green and displays a second countdown, this time in the form of a white circle that fills itself over the course of 1 minute.

Anything a subject does in the time it takes for the circle to fill up, the subject will retroactively consider to be "their greatest achievement" or apotheosis of their life. The anomaly does so not by elevating the significance of actions taken within the booth, but by diminishing the subjective meaning of everything they had ever done before that point to be less impressive than the actions taken in the booth.

Notes: A saw a man sit down in this thing, do nothing but twiddle his thumbs, wait out the clock, get out, and he was speaking gibberish words, but also, he was ecstatic. I heard he has since quit his job, divorced his wife, disowned his children, and is getting into politics. I do not understand what this thing does to you, but I can tell you I want nothing to do with it. —Nightingale

Artefact 00012 — Labeled plank

Artefact: A 260cm tall untreated wooden plank that is part of the back wall of the structure. The object has a rough texture with visible splinters. The words "LICK ME" are drawn onto it with permanent marker.

Anomaly: Untested.

Notes: Ravn and I have a very definitive "do not touch any planks" policy. We'll get to them someday. —Nightingale

Intermediary Log 1

I wanted to include a recent exploration log to exhibit Specialist Ravn Laake's experience navigating SCP-9292. I regret not having any recordings of how he taught me to traverse the town. Laake's expertise as a local specialist has been invaluable for the research and containment of all objects in SCP-9292. Moreover, this log will hopefully give insight in the experience on-the-ground. —Persephone Nightingale

BEGIN LOG

The footage shows the face of Junior Researcher Rune Tufte, before being turned around and affixed to his winter coat. Once stabilized, the footage depicts SCP-9292 as seen from the east. Several abandoned residences are clearly visible, framed by forest on both sides. Containment Specialist Ravn Laake is visible walking across the field towards the village, keeping a wide berth from pine trees.

Tufte: Hey, wait up!

Tufte stumbles.

Tufte: Could– Couldn't we have taken the footpath? It's right there. It's a dirt path, surely that's safer than the grass? Hey, please, hold up!

Laake stops and turns around.

Tufte: Thanks, woo… After two years in the lab I'm really not–

Laake: Follow in my footsteps.

Laake turns back towards the village and stays still for four seconds. He makes a sudden 90° turn and walks parallel to SCP-9292, hopping over the dirt footpath where he crosses it. Tufte follows.

Tufte: Why did we change direction?

Laake: Squirrel nest.

Tufte: Squirrels? What are they, like, meat-eating squirrels? I heard they have those at Site 19. Or something similar, at least. Maybe they were chipmunks? I always forget what the difference is.

Laake turns 90° again and continues approaching the city. The sound of the river is now vaguely audible on the recording.

Tufte: They probably aren't as scary as the cows we contained in 9292, though, huh? The acid coming out of Victoria's udders would burn those chipmunks to bit if they got near her, though at least she's not as fast and cruel as the goats.

The two personnel pass the Ueland residency. Laake stops again to listen, before slowly taking steps in the direction of the main road.

Tufte: Oh god, I can smell the molten rubber and steel, that is horrid. I didn't even think about how bad the fumes would be. There's the road alright, there's the road…

Tufte: Okay, okay. Just make sure not to touch anything and look where I step. Stay away from the house, stay away from the road, I think I can do this.

The camera movement suggests Tufte is breathing irregularly. Laake stops in place, and Tufte almost bumps into him. Laake turns around and makes eye-contact with Tufte.

Laake: We will wait here until you have calmed down.

Tufte: I'm calm, I think. No, I know, I am calm. I remember your lessons, I remember Linn's lessons, I am fully prepared. Don't touch anything, don't look at anything, follow your footsteps, take it slow…

Laake does not move, retaining eye-contact. Tufte starts regulating his breathing.

Tufte: Okay… In… and out…

Tufte: I am ready.

Laake nods and turns around again. The pair reach the main road and turn left, following a dirt footpath.

Tufte: This is a footpath we can walk on?

Laake: It is A01021. Safe.

Tufte: 01021? Wasn't that… Oh, but I don't feel anything different. I know the file said the universe is identical, but I guess I still expected something to happen?

A slight movement registers in the distance; something seems to peak out from behind the Bergman-family greenhouse.

Tufte: Hey, did you see–

Laake places his hand on Tufte's shoulder.

Laake: Do not approach the chairs.

Tufte: Oh God, I didn't realize.

20 seconds of silence as the two continue walking along the dirt path.

Tufte: There it is, the tile footpath. Ah, I can see the hopscotch tiles. I'll grab my camera, but I gotta keep my distance from them. I really do wonder who drew these. The youngest child in town was already 15 years old, so either these crayon drawings are–

Tufte takes two steps backwards to get a better view of the tiles as a whole, and takes out his photo camera. Laake places his hand on Tufte's shoulder again in order to stop him from accidentally stepping on the asphalt road. Tufte drops his camera on the ground.

Laake: You are still in the village. Prioritize safety.

20 seconds of silence as Tufte slowly picks his camera back up and steps forward, away from the road.

Tufte: I will… Approach them slowly, and look at them one by one.

Tufte: First tile after the hopscotch. Pink, yellow, and white crayon. It shows a princess in a castle. Or just a tower, I think.

Tufte: Tile number 2. Grey, yellow, and blue crayon. A flying saucer in the air.

Tufte: Tile 3 suggests this is a comic strip. Grey, yellow, blue, and white crayon. The UFO is either beaming down or beaming up a person. That long hair and dress probably suggests a girl?

Tufte: Tile 4. The same girl in yellow and white. Green for a t-rex. She seems to fight it with a magic wand with a star on top?

Laake: That is A02154. Persephone found it in the bushes 300 meters in that direction.

Tufte: The wand is real? You're saying these tiles might depict a real series of events? Wait, what does this artefact do?

Laake: It lets out a laser that destroys things.

Tufte: Oh.

Tufte: I guess that explains tile 5. The girl next to a crater with a puff of smoke coming from the wand. That's another picture…

Tufte: Tile 6 has the girl reaching the tower with the princess. Oh, I almost forgot; which one of these have we tested so far?

Laake: Someone stepped on the first tile during first exploration and was sucked into it. You can still see the princess blink sometimes. When there is no wind and no one else around, you can hear her cry out. Persephone placed a test block on what you called tile 2. It was transformed into a small UFO. That's it.

Tufte continues to look at the individual tiles one at a time, taking pictures of each and mumbling to himself.

Tufte: That's the last of them, they're all properly documented now. It's a sweet adventure story, but the ending seems really sad. The two girls leave together in their spaceship, the princess leaving her family behind. What do you think of this raygun in tile 21? Maybe it has somesort of amnesticizing property on the family?

Laake: I do not recognize it. Place a test block on it.

Tufte: You reckon we should make it our first experiment?

Laake: The tiles is why we are here. Just do not touch them yourself.

Tufte removes a standardized test block from his backpack, waits ten seconds, and then slowly places it on the tile with the 'raygun' drawing. The moment Tufte taps the stone with the block, the tile begins to glow in a green hue.

Tufte: What the–

Tufte pulls back when a shape begins to form on top of the tile. The camera feed glitches as the light on the tile cycles through different colors. A metal object resembling the drawn 'raygun' appears on the tile. The crayon illustration lies unchanged beneath it.

Laake: Mystery solved.

Tufte: It's real? Can I just pick this up?

Laake: Follow gun safety rules.

Tufte slowly picks up the item, places it in a plastic bag, and pockets it.

Tufte: Can't wait to test this in the lab. Can we do another one?

Tufte briefly rotates to look at Laake as he asks the question. Laake nods.

Tufte: How about… the alien girl fighting the t-rex in tile 4?

Tufte walks over to the left-most tiles.

Tufte: I've always liked dinosaurs. The story starting with an epic t-rex battle before all of the enchanted woods and magical creatures is pretty silly.

Laake: This experiment might be dangerous.

Tufte: I hope it won't be that bad. These are children's drawings, after all… I should probably knock on wood after saying stuff like that.

Laake: The house's fence is safe to touch, but step around the tiles if you must.

Tufte: It's just a saying. Worst part is that I can never tell if you're serious or just making fun of me.

Laake: I can do both simultaneously.

Tufte sighs and taps Tile 4 with his standardized test block. The tile begins to glow and Tufte takes a step back. The camera feed glitches again as a shape begins to form above the tile. The glowing form quickly increases in size and Tufte takes another step back. As the shape grows beyond an ~80cm diameter, Specialist Laake steps into frame, takes hold of the materializing object with one arm, and throws it in the direction of the Hjelle-family residence.

Tufte: What the–

The shape stops glowing and growing when it collides with Artefact 48201, the impermeable invisible forcefield that extends up from the Hjelle-family's garden fence. Dropping to the floor, the manifested creature is revealed to be a reptilian deinonychus. The dinosaur slowly stands up and extends its tail against A-48201. It is over a meter tall and over two meters long.

Tufte: Is that a velocirap–

The dinosaur crouches and leaps towards Junior Researcher Tufte. Specialist Laake intercepts its jump and pins the deinonychus to the ground, keeping out of range from its talons.

Laake: Run… 20 meters.. south…

Tufte: What? Oh, uh, alright!

Tufte turns away from the scene and runs 40 meters south between the tile path and the road, before turning around. In the distance, Specialist Laake is visible charging into the dinosaur from the side, pushing it onto the asphalt road. Laake is only barely able to stop his own momentum before he would fall onto the road himself. The deinonychus cries out a shrill call as its feet, tail, and lower body melts into a thick liquid.

Tufte: I can't believe it.

Tufte slowly starts walking back to Laake

Tufte: I can't believe it. Did you really just fight a freaking dinosaur and won? That was… that was incredible! How did– Oh god, is that really what it smells like when a velociraptor melts into liquid? God… I didn't know these roads could get any worse, that's just awful! But still–

Laake: That concludes testing for today.

Note that Rune Tufte returned from his first field mission uninjured, and Ravn Laake sustained only minimal injuries despite confronting a highly dangerous entity.—Persephone Nightingale

Artefact 00495 — The fishing shack

Artefact: A 4 meter by 5 meter wooden structure, positioned on the dock near the river. Structure is continuously leaking water from its seams, and its door does not open. All surfaces of the structure are covered with a layer of fine particles.

Artefact 00700 — River water

Artefact: Water of the Faxälven River.

Anomaly: Water is contaminated with an emulsion of polylactic acid and small suspended pockets of petroleum. The mixed liquid glows a faint yellow in the dark. Humans can respirate while submerged in this liquid, but the low oxygen levels result in hypoxia (confusion, disorientation, hallucinations, headaches, etc). Unintentionally inhaling petroleum bubbles is unavoidable during longer freediving sessions and is lethal.

Notes: I am investigating the source of the contamination. Downstream of the Faxälven River is non-anomalous due to artefact 00701. 10 km upstream is non-anomalous as well, but dense forestry impedes exploration. —Laake

Artefact 00722 — Acidic stickleback

Artefact: 500–1000 Three-spined stickleback (Gasterosteus aculeatus).

Anomaly: Typical in appearance and behavior, but body conducts itself as an extremely acidic liquid when interacting with solids or liquids besides water. Species derives nourishment from dissolving nutrients within their bodies.

Notes: Dangerous. —Laake

Artefact 00733 – Oleaginous crab

Artefact: Atelecyclus undecimdentatus crab, a species typical of Atlantic coastal regions and not endemic to Norway.

Anomaly: 20% of the crab's body mass (predominantly its limbs) has been converted into a solid black grease substance. Any organic matter that contacts this substance is instantly converted to a similar, inert material.

Notes: All the blackened spots of dead, solidified grass give a very clear view of where the crab comes and goes. I feel like it's in pain. Can crabs feel pain? —Nightingale

Intermediary Log 3

I took the liberty of copy-editing Specialist Laake's recent investigation report for wider publication. I'm actively working with him to write a comprehensive paper on the river anomalies, but until then this might suffice as a summary of Ravn's immediate findings. —Nightingale

On June 5, 2023, I finally reached the point-of-origin of A00700. The forest in which it is nestled is covered by cancerous dust, but I have marked out a navigable route through it. The forest is too quiet. I have reported six human corpses discovered so far (lumps of cancerous flesh in overalls).

Three kilometers up-river, well outside town borders, stands the skeleton of a 1980s-style factory building. It is not visible on any satellite imagery. Its subfloor is filled with a thick, foul-smelling mixture of liquid plastic and crude oil, which slowly leaks out into the river through small pipes.

The structure is large, but seems to have consisted exclusively of workfloors and ovens. Six collapsed chimneys are distinctly recognizable. We have not found any semblance of administrative offices, computers, or desks. There are a few rusted remains of conveyors and cutting machines. Rusted rebar protrudes from the concrete walls and floors, which are piercing hazards. The degree of overall decay is inconsistent with the age of the structure.

The only unstained objects are thirty steel pallets standing in the center of the building. Each hold 50,000 transparent "iPhone 6 Plus"-fitted phone cases in unmarked plastic packaging. —Laake

Laake is of the impression that this Factory was not build by people. I would like to believe so too. Conradi is adamant some billionaire capitalist set it up, but even she can't explain the outdated phone cases. Regardless of what the Factory is, it is by far the most unbearable place I have ever been in. —Nightingale

Only known historic photograph of the chapel.

Chapel

Lead researcher: Persephone Nightingale

Built in 1905, the chapel was known to host weekly Lutheran mass by Dr. eccl. Ártne Ártnesen since 1999. There are no records of the Diocese of Nord-Hålogaland visiting the chapel since 1965, and the Church of Norway fully lost contact with Ártnesen in 2016.

Artefact 01106 — Crucifix

Artefact: A crucifix sculpture of Jesus Christ hanging above the chancel in the chapel.

Anomaly: Every night between sunset and sunrise (thus significantly longer in winter than in summer), the sculpture becomes animate and capable of vocalizations. It primarily spends these periods moaning and screaming in apparent agony.

Notes: It honestly feels validating to hear Jesus cuss me out and tell me to go to hell. We've requested the higher-ups if we can put this guy out of his misery. —Conradi

Artefact 01126 — Church pews

Artefact: Four rows of wooden benches with minimal back-support, flanking a central aisle.

Anomaly: What initially appears as a rough surface of splinters are actually microscopic syringes. When one or more subjects sit on any of the pews, the syringes puncture the skin at multiple locations on the buttocks, causing significant discomfort. Blood is drawn out by some of the splinters and transfused with the other subjects. It is unknown how the blood travels between the benches.

This process does not result in alloimmune reactions, but does spread bloodborne diseases. The shared immune-system created by A01126 can serve as a method to fight diseases faster, at the cost of infecting everyone involved. After a half-hour session on A01126, blood levels equalize between all subjects, but it is theorized some blood is lost in this process.

Notes: The altered behavior of white blood cells traveling through A01126 is subject to study. Creepy as these things are, their therapeutic potential is real. —Nightingale

Artefact 01128 — Votive candles

Artefact: A set of 28 small candles, set into a glass altar.

Anomaly: None of the candles have extinguished since the first exploration of the chapel. The glass altar is unstained despite the large amount of carbon released by the candles. The fire is 500° hotter than expected from this type of candle. Material testing of the wax has revealed it to consist of human adipose tissue (body fat).

Notes: Is it ironic to use the remains of a dead body to pray for the dead? Either way, it stinks. —Conradi

I think they smell weirdly good.—Nightingale

Artefact 01134 — Pulpit

Artefact: A hexagonal raised stand seemingly carved out of a single massive piece of bone. Carvings in its exterior do not match known Christian iconography. Its five accessible sides each depict a central figure overcoming violent trials laid upon him by larger figures above him. One side is entirely obstructed by a wall.

Anomaly: Interior of the pulpit (accessible by climbing the mobile stairs chained to the structure, A001136) is filled with organic matter. While most of the material resembles human intestines and esophaguses, the mass also contains an unknown number of small eggs of anomalous entities. Since discovery, eight of these eggs have hatched to reveal skinless serpentile creatures, which have been successfully stunned and contained.

Notes: I expect some of these snakes may have escaped the chapel and are brooding elsewhere. I keep my eye out for them in my scouting. —Laake

Artefact 02331 — Central altar

Artefact: A wooden altar in the center of the chancel.

Anomaly: Anomaly is only functional when the church doors (A01101) are closed. Pouring human blood on five specific decorations on the altar's surface activates its effect. The altar splits in two and each half slowly slides out of the way to reveal an opening in the floor. Stairs have been hewn into the foundation, leading to a secret basement.

Notes: Took some real sleuthing to figure this one out. —Nightingale

Artefact 02334 — Russo-Solomonari sigils

Artefact: Basement floor and walls are covered with sigils of hybrid proto-Karcist design. A large central sigil, 2m in diameter, connects to all the smaller sigils in the room with intricate linework. Glass flasks are positioned on each smaller sigil.

Anomaly: When two or more individuals stand within the central sigil simultaneously, all sigils begin to radiate a dim red light. Over the course of 30 to 120 minutes, the two individuals are physically pulled together, their skin blending together. The bodies merge into a single gestalt form, and excess blood and viscera are transported into the glass flasks.

Notes: See my article in the July and September 2025 issues of Anomalous Transactions, or alternatively, read my story in Astonishing Anomalies 5/25 for the real insights. —Conradi

Artefact 02335 — Organic desk chair

Artefact: A desk chair of professional construction.

Anomaly: While its wooden frame appears non-anomalous, the leather backing and seating flutters rhythmically. Both pieces of fabric make distinct squealing noises when an individual sits down.

Notes: Both are marked with Karcist tattoos, and both match human DNA. A man and a woman, but we've otherwise not been able to identify them. I think they enjoy it when people sit on them. I wonder if they signed up for this. —Conradi

Artefact 02337 — Dictation machine

Artefact: A Zoom H4n digital portable dictation machine, resting on the fleshy mass of A02336.

Anomaly: Machine's playback function has been engaged since discovery, and cannot be turned off. Limited investigation of the device' interior has revealed masses of biological matter, predominantly matching sealife DNA.

Notes: Segment of A022337's 4-hour loop of a sermon held by priest Ártne Ártnesen included below.

– Recording Log A02337

Ártnesen: When Karcist d'Ambrosia went down here and saw my father… All he saw was heresy. But he didn't even see a fifth of all the heresy! You! Yes you! You are turtles all the way down of heresy! You commit irreverence in your dreams, but it is your dreams which present to you the gospel. One heart, one mission, one true religion. Only once one can grow beyond God could one control the Halkost flesh within you, but only once one grows beyond the flesh can one reach the stars. We have been distracted, brainwashed, lulled into a sense of belonging. We do not belong! Not to ourselves, not to eachother, and most of all we do not belong to the stars. Belonging is a myth we must cleanse ourselves from. We must not think of needs, because even the sheep of God could leave his needs behind. The Karcists have taught us the techniques to reach ultimate penance, but even this is merely a need we must forego. We will let go of not just every part of ourselves, but the whole of ourselves, destroying the Elysium Fields in our wake and grasping the universe itself.


Artefact 02338 — Ceiling fresco

Artefact: A rock-hewn stairway (A02336) in the office room leads down 5 meters into a circular domed room, 5 meter in diameter. The room's ceiling features an intricate and largely accurate fresco painting of a starlit sky. Constellations are clearly visible, but unmarked and slightly offset from their factual positions.

Anomaly: Stars in the painting move in a circular pattern over the course of 24 hours, matching actual stellar motion. Stars emit a low level of light, dimly illuminating the otherwise dark room.

Notes: Five bright equidistant stars have been added to the painting.

Artefact 02339 — Star Signals

Artefact: A book discovered on a lectern (A02340) in the domed room.

Anomaly: An instance of SCP-1425.

Notes: Incinerated.

Artefact 023010

Artefact: An organic heart valve discovered beyond a crack in the wall of the domed room. The organ is 1 meter in diameter and is embedded in the rock floor.

Anomaly: Organic structure is unexplained and continues to pulsate.

Notes: Indirect DNA mapping suggests a match with Ártne Ártnesen (DNA mapped through Ártnesen's aunt who participated in a Sámi genetic study).

Artefact 02311

Artefact: A 20cm–40cm flat stone, discovered in the cavity 20 meters down from A02310.

Anomaly: Artifact is warm to the touch.

Notes: Sieidi. —Laake




Closing Log

Nightingale: So, why do you think this town is inundated with anomalies?

Conradi: Are we really doing this now? Percy, please, can't we–

Nightingale: Hey, we're all together! You know tomorrow morning Ravn will go out early again to continue cleaning up the forest. Now is as good a time as any!

Tufte: He's actually, uh, going to set out with me again.

Nightingale: Oh? More clues about the kids?

Tufte: Ravn spotted something that might be a treehouse. It's covered by the dust so I'd rather not get too close to it, but it might match the tower from the drawing?

Conradi: You don't think the UFO is actually out there, do you?

Tufte: I have to believe.

Laake: It is a new structure. We should investigate.

Nightingale: I agree, but we've gone off-topic.

Conradi: Oh Christ, you're recording this? At least warn us before you do that.

Nightingale: It's for the record. I want to finally publish something significant, I can't keep waiting for the two of you to complete your focal projects. Our scip file has been three paragraphs for years now and the higher-ups are breathing on my neck here!

Conradi: Ok, ok, I guess it beats whatever game Rune brought.

Tufte: Aw, but–

Nightingale: Please someone just answer the question!

Conradi: Why is the town all magical? There's a dozen reasons. The Chicago Spirit built stable timeloops here to smuggle Daevite artifacts. An old man mail-ordered hundreds of Egypt-themed Wondertainment toys until he was raided by ORIA for some godforsaken reason. There's no pattern to the madness here.

Laake: I don't think that's what Persephone meant, Linn.

Nightingale: Yes, it's clear enough that dozens of anomalous people and organizations are involved. But, for example, the village Ravn is from is only a few kilometers downstream. Why–

Laake: Was.

Nightingale: Uh, yeah… Point is, why is this village specifically such a… rich hub of magic. For better or worse.

Tufte: Aliens?

Conradi: I was thinking leylines, maybe. But I don't think it's an interesting question at all.

Nightingale: Not this again..

Conradi: No please, hear me out. We're dealing with magic here. The anomalous. I think it was inevitable for a place like this to come about. Once magic was invented, there had to someday be a place where all of it would come together.

Ten seconds of silence

Conradi: It's like life in the universe! If life is possible, then there must be a single spot somewhere where you can find all sorts of life all huddled together. And that right there, that's the earth. Most of the world is just boring mundanity, but there's one magical spot where everything is interesting and vibrant.

Five seconds of silence

Tufte: I still think it's aliens.

Laake: I've never heard someone describe my home as "vibrant" before. Tens of people died here.

Conradi: It isn't a big vibrant city, but lots of people still lived fascinating lives here. It's just a shame it turned out to be so… unstable.

Conradi: Deadly.

Twenty seconds of silence

Nightingale: I could spend an entire lifetime here and only see a fragment of all the amazing things people have built here.

Conradi: Yeah.

Nightingale: But at the same time…

Conradi: Hmm?

Nightingale: My world has become so small. My entire universe is inside of this village's borders now.

Tufte: You should've come with our group to see the glaciers last month. They were gorgeous.

Laake: We are only an hour's drive away from the Tysfjorden.

Conradi: You never visited Tromsø, did you?

Nightingale: I get it, I get it, I should take a vacation.

Conradi: I think this is one of the most beautiful places on earth, at least if you get away from the polluted river, the molten cars, and the screaming Jesus.

Nightingale: Maybe you two aren't the only ones that are obsessively sucked into this place.

Conradi: I still work for the Foundation. I do my job, but I can appreciate some nature.

Laake: I live here.

Nightingale sighs

Nightingale: Well, maybe once I've edited this interview log into a reasonable format, I'll go on a hike through the mountains. It would be good for me.

Conradi: Girl, just publish it as-is. You've been working on this for months now. Let Site 19 figure things out from here.

Nightingale: Funny for you to say that.

Conradi: I'm not going to leave a single stone unturned in my research, but the overview file just needs to be accessible. I am doing a single house, you do the whole town. Publish it and see what people think afterwards.

Nightingale: I'll sleep on it and see tomorrow…

Nightingale: For now though… any concluding thoughts?

Conradi: You're a nerd.

Laake: The town is dangerous and it is a grave, but Conradi is correct, it is also wonderful. I am glad that I have been able to investigate it with such a passionate team, and I am looking forward to investigate it more.

Nightingale: Well said, Ravn. Thank you.
