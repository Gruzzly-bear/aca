Special Containment Procedures

SCP-8197 must be kept in a reinforced theropod-class containment enclosure measuring 60 m × 40 m × 15 m, constructed of high-density alloy mesh and triple-layer observation glass.
The habitat is to simulate a natural forested environment, such as where SCP-8197 was first located, so it feels at home and less stressed.
And, at all times, must be around 18–22 °C

Personnel entering SCP-8197’s chamber must undergo Level-3 clearance authorization and remain under constant observation from the upper control platform. No more than two individuals are to enter the enclosure at once.

Feeding occurs twice weekly with live Elk.

In the event of agitation or containment breach indicators (rapid bioluminescent activity), SCP-8197 is to be calmed using low-frequency sound emitters tuned to 27 Hz, which have been shown to induce a subdued state.

Description
SCP-8197 is a large, bipedal, theropod-like organism approximately 9 m in length and 3 m tall at the head. Morphologically similar to Maip macrothorax, SCP-8197 possesses dense feathers and quills all over it's back and tail, believed to aid in temperature regulation.

The entity’s coloration is grey at the base, darkening toward the head, with a series of blue, vein-like luminescent structures visible beneath the skin along the torso and throat. These luminescent veins pulse in synchronization with the creature’s heartbeat, intensifying during stress or aggression. The function of this phenomenon is undetermined but hypothesized to be related to communication or emotional display. Eyes are dark green with vertical pupils.

SCP-8197 possesses a proportionally large skull housing numerous serrated teeth capable of exerting an estimated bite force of 3 000 psi. The forelimbs terminate in three elongated claws capable of inflicting severe trauma; hind limbs exhibit strong musculature enabling bursts of speed up to 38 mph.

Behavioural Summary:

At first: SCP-8197 was extremely aggressive and attempted to attack any personnel or mechanical presence in sight, displaying signs of disorientation consistent with post-traumatic environmental shock.
Now: SCP-8197 demonstrates significantly reduced hostility, showing curiosity and limited social recognition toward specific handlers. Bioluminescent activity slows markedly when interacting with familiar staff, suggesting lowered stress and adaptive trust responses.

Addendum 8197-A – Discovery:

SCP-8197 was recovered on 12 / 6 / 2012 following reports of sightings of a large reptilian animal at "Great Bear lake", Canada. Mobile Task Force Eta-6 located the entity at the edge of the lake drinking. The moment it was shot with one of the darts, it bolted to the forest, luckily it didn't take long for the anaesthetic to take affect and slow it down. Tranquilization required four high-yield anaesthetic darts and two reinforced containment vehicles for transport to Site-65.

[D-7712 starts to walk to the containment exit.]

Dr. Elijah: "What do you think you're doing?! Get back there now!"

Post-Experiment Note:

Following Incident 8197-01, D-7712 was declared deceased. Recovery of D-7712’s body was deemed unsafe due to SCP-8197’s heightened aggression.
Approximately three hours after the event, SCP-8197’s bioluminescent markings were observed pulsing at an accelerated rate before gradually returning to baseline. Researchers speculate this response may be stress-related or a display of agitation.

Further testing involving live human subjects has been temporarily suspended pending review by Site Command.

SCP-8197's Current Status:

SCP-8197 remains contained. Continued behavioural and neurological monitoring is authorized to evaluate its potential for higher cognitive processing and emotional learning.
