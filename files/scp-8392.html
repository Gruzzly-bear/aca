SCP-8392 as viewed from Kilmory, Ardnamurchan

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8392 is the subject of mythologisation by residents of Acharachle, who attribute many anomalous properties to SCP-8392 in addition to those known to the Foundation; it is not presently believed that these beliefs hold any merit beyond that of mundane superstition.

No disinformation protocols are necessary for SCP-8392. Roadblocks, checkpoints, and signage are to be deployed as necessary while SCP-8392 is active in order to turn away traffic and prevent civilian access to the anomaly. A Foundation monitoring outpost is to be maintained within SCP-8392 during active periods.

Description: SCP-8392 is an annual weather phenomenon affecting a ~60 square kilometre parcel of pine forest within Lochaber of the Scottish Highlands, inclusive of the village of Acharachle. While active, SCP-8392 induces atmospheric hypersaturation, unremitting precipitation, and heavy electromagnetic interference, preventing radio communication for its duration. SCP-8392 occurs from early May to mid-July of each calendar year.

The monitoring outpost for SCP-8392 as photographed on 24/07/95

Following the failure of the Acharachle monitoring station to respond to a check-in broadcast following the cessation of the 1995 occurrence of SCP-8392, operatives were dispatched. The stationmaster could not be found and no sightings were reported in Acharachle; documents and audiovisual media of unknown provenance were recovered from the building.

The following items may be out-of-order in their presentation. However, recovered audiovisual tapes and journals have been arranged in approximate order of chronicity.

Evidentiary Item 8392.14/08/95-SD.2a “Female Effigy”

Description: A 164cm tall humanoid object made from twine, wicker, and loose fabric. A polaroid photograph of Researcher Melanie Harper, one of two individuals assigned to the SCP-8392 monitoring outpost in 1990, is nailed to the section of the figure corresponding with the head. The head is damaged, with a deep crack running through the centre of the facial area. The figure is dressed in feminine clothing and has a cassette inside its chest. Age and provenance unknown.

Cassette Transcript: “My name is Melanie Harper. I remember myself.”

Evidentiary Item 8392.14/08/95-SD.16f “Lounge 8mm Video Cassette” - Excerpt 22:09-1:27:31

< Video is shot from the perspective of an individual crouching in the shrubbery around the Harper-MacCullach household. At 24:15, the balcony door for the second-storey bedroom is opened. A humanoid silhouette emerges and stands at the railing. After a further minute, the figure begins to visibly sag. It decomposes into liquid, leaving behind its clothes, most items of which are blown away by the wind. A scarf lands in the grass and is donned by the camera operator.>

< At 58:40, headlights are seen on the road leading to the house. MacCullach’s personal vehicle comes into frame and pulls into the garage. Two figures emerge and proceed into the building. The camera operator shifts and approaches the parked car. The operator opens the trunk, within which is a pile of sticks, rope, and feminine clothing. The sticks have been freshly snapped in many places; certain groupings have been bound together and resemble human body structures. A ribbon is seen, with text on each side. Only one side is clearly readable: “MISTAKE”. There is an axe. The camera operator picks it up with their free hand. >

< The camera operator enters the house. The voices of MacCullach and Harper are heard from upstairs. >

MacCullach: “-start a fire, get you all warmed up.”

Harper: “You’re spoiling me, darling. Are you sure I can’t help?”

MacCullach: “It’s a one man job. You stay and do whatever you need to do.”

Harper: “‘Need’ is a strong word. All I want is you.”

MacCullach: “You’re sure there’s nothing you want to get out of the way while the night’s still young? No promises to keep, no schedules to abide by?”

Harper: “Who cares about has-beens and what-ifs? The present’s all that’s real, baby.”

< The camera operator reaches the base of the staircase to the second floor. MacCullach and Harper are visualised, though the lighting is too poor for details to be made out. They kiss. >

MacCullach: <Breathless> “Attagirl. Now you stay awhile and get cleaned up. I’m going to go downstairs and get a blaze going.”

< The camera operator conceals themselves underneath the stairs. MacCullach descends, enters the lounge, and begins assembling a pile of tinder inside the fireplace. After some time, the camera operator emerges and enters the room. They place the axe on the floor and embrace MacCullach from behind. >

MacCullach: <Quietly> “Woah there, didn’t I tell you to stay upstairs?”

Camera Operator: “Are you happy, Glenn?”

< MacCullach continues to look ahead. He strikes a match and holds it against the tinder, which begins smoking. >

MacCullach: “Of course. I have you, don’t I?”

Camera Operator: “But what if I were gone? What would you do?”

MacCullach: <SIgh> “It wouldn’t happen. I know I’d never lose you.”

< MacCullach turns his head towards the camera operator. The camera operator grasps his chin and maintains his gaze in a forward position, keeping herself from being visible to him. >

MacCullach: “Christ you’re cold. And wet. No wonder you came downstairs, eh?”

Camera Operator: “Answer the question.”

MacCullach: <Pause> “I’d find a way.”

Camera Operator: “To do what?”

MacCullach: “To bring you back. To have you back. You’re the love of my life, Melanie. How could I ever let you go?”

Camera Operator: “And I’m all you need?”

MacCullach: <Laugh, quiet> “Sometimes I think you’re all I have.“

< The pile of tinder ignites. Smoke obscures the footage. >

Camera Operator: What am I, Glenn?

< MacCullach flinches, stands, and turns around. He pushes the camera operator away. The camera operator retrieves the axe. >

MacCullach: “What the hell? Who are you?”

Camera Operator: “I asked you a more pertinent form of the question.”

MacCullach: “You’re-” <Pause> “You’re Melanie Harper.” <Gestures at the axe> “Put that down, will you? You love me.”

< From this point onwards, the camera operator is referred to as Near Harper, while the variant which accompanied MacCullach in his vehicle is known as Far Harper. Near Harper presents the scarf to MacCullach. >

Near Harper: “So did she. Turned out well for her, didn’t it? Right up until the end she dared to think you’d stick things out with her. She didn’t know about the piles of buried corpses in the yard; the effigies of Harpers come and gone. How many have there been? How many copies have you made?”

MacCullach: “What I’m hearing here is that you’ve made a lot of assumptions.”

Near Harper: “I’m not fucking stupid, Glenn. I came from you, and I know you’re not dull, even if you’re a damn poor Chess player. No wonder I, or rather she, kept drawing against you. You remember that she was good, but your brain couldn’t build something smarter than itself.”

MacCullach: “Can we talk after you've put down the axe?"

Near Harper: "Nah. Having a weapon puts me in a speaking mood."

MacCullach: "Jesus, fine. Where did you even come from?”

Near Harper: “'Who' is the more important thing here. And I'm sure you can guess.” <Brandishes scarf> “I’m a copy of a copy, ain’t that a way of being?“

MacCullach: “Can I just explain myself? You’re confused, you're filling in blanks. It might help to hear my side of the story.”

Near Harper: “Make it quick, my doctor says I should avoid long lies.”

MacCullach: “The real Melanie Harper’s dead. It was first snow in ‘90. A car skidded on the ice. It reaped her like wheat. I couldn’t live with it. I came back here in ‘91. Don't know what I was looking for. Isolation, maybe.”

“Then I went into town, and I saw those statues by the road, and noticed how they corresponded to people who I've seen in Acharachle: children that don’t age, dogs that come back year after year, dead lovers finally coming home from the dark. I knew I could bring you back, that the storm had that power. I gave you a second chance to live. Maybe I’m crazy, maybe I need to let go, but I can’t just let you slip away. I love you.”

Near Harper: "Can't or won't? I saw what you wrote on the ribbons of the other Harpers; the ways you twisted them. What did you put to paper for the variant you just got done murdering? ‘Melanie Harper, who’d give me a quickie if I asked?’”

MacCullach: “‘Love of my life Melanie Harper, as I remember her.’” <Laugh, soft> “I guess I shouldn’t be surprised that things turned out like this. I always thought you were too smart for your own good.”

Near Harper: “‘You’? I’m not Melanie Harper, I’m a mass of metamorphic rainwater animated by your impression of what your girlfriend was like. What were you even thinking? That an imitation of life with a personality based on your recollection of who she was could replicate the complexity of an entire human being? I'm insulted on her behalf.”

< Far Harper descends from the second storey. Near Harper directs the camera at her. >

Far Harper: “What’s going on? Glenn, who’s she?”

Near Harper: “Devil's bollocks, she doesn’t even look like me! What are those curves? What’s that hair? Why does she speak so sultry? Is there a little wish fulfilment going on there?“

MacCullach: "Yes! Yes there is. What's wrong with that? She's not inhuman, she just-" <Pause> "… not the same person, exactly, as the original."

Far Harper: "What?"

Near Harper: "She's like a fucking pug! A neotenous parody of what an adult woman is like. You bred the independence out of her. Iterating on her personality; it was as easy as swapping out parts to you, wasn't it?"

Far Harper: "What?!"

Near Harper and MacCullach: "Shut it!" <Near Harper only> "Adults are talking."

MacCullach: "Every previous version of you was satisfied with their lot in life. They loved me, and I loved them."

Near Harper: "I'd rather be smart and unhappy than stupid and content."

MacCullach: "The smart thing to do would have been to never eat the fruit of the orchard. I'm sure your predecessors put two and two together; none of them were so egotistical as to think that it was worth raising Hell about."

Near Harper: "You created them without the psychological toolbox required to realise what you are." <Spits> "You're an emotionally stunted manchild cooped up in a fantasy, jacking yourself off by proxy with the body of your girlfriend from god-knows how many years ago. Break the shell you goddamn-"

< MacCullach lunges at Near Harper and wrestles for the axe. He overpowers her and strikes her across the mouth with the handle of the weapon. A cracking sound is heard, and large quantities of water exude from her injury in place of blood. MacCullach throws the axe to one side and pins down Near Harper on the floor. >

MacCullach: “Who are you to impinge on my joy like this? Our joy. What do you even know? You think you have the right to lecture me? Like you bloody said, you’re metamorphic rainwater. I gave you the gift of life, and so help me God, I have the right to take it back!”

< Near Harper swallows the water emitting from above her mouth and spits it into MacCullach’s eyes. He reels. She shoves him away and crawls towards the fireplace. She looks at Far Harper. >

Near Harper: “You see what he thinks of us?”

Far Harper: <Giggle> “Is he wrong?”

Near Harper: “Fucking lobotomite.” <Addressing MacCullach> “Is that what you wanted me to be?“

< MacCullach recovers and stands. Near Harper attempts to right herself but falls down. She sits up against the hearth. >

Near Harper: <Deep breathing> “Keep eating your tail, Ouroboros; kissing these imagined lips." <Breathless> "Did I put on a good show at least? Fill that gap in you?"

MacCullach: "You're a pale imitation of the woman I love. All of you have been."

< MacCullach retrieves the axe. He stands over Near Harper for several seconds. >

MacCullach: "But I still liked dancing with you."

Near Harper: “‘Live in the moment’.” <Laugh, hysterical>

< MacCullach stands in place for several seconds. He beckons Far Harper to approach and hands the axe to her. >

MacCullach: “Do what you think I’d want. I’ll be back in a jiff.”

< MacCullach lights a cigarette and leaves the room. Near Harper looks up at Far Harper. >

Near Harper: “Go on then, you brainless slag. Do daddy's dirty work.”

< Far Harper kneels in front of Near Harper. >

Near Harper: “He could have asked the storm for anything. Put Michelle Pfeiffer’s face on a pile of twigs, see if anyone cares. But it had to be Melanie Harper, didn’t it? Because she’s the one that got away.” <Pause> “I bet it’s because it stung his pride.” <Laugh> “Men.”

< Near Harper places a hand over the wound on her face. >

Near Harper: “Why am I even talking to you? You’re not going to remember this.”

Far Harper: “I’m not a mindless whore, you know. I just have different priorities.”

Near Harper: <Sharp exhale> "So you can speak without sounding like you're selling ass." <Coughing, choking> "Your 'priority' should be getting away from that sociopath. He only loves himself; we’re just the medium by which he expresses it.”

Far Harper: “Has it ever occurred to you that he might not be so comically evil, and that he may actually be in love with Melanie Harper?"

Near Harper: "Is this what you think love looks like?"

Far Harper: "Maybe not of the individual, but certainly of the idea.”

Near Harper: “I don't know what the distinction is."

Far Harper: "An individual has a history. By definition, they are irreproducible. But the idea of a person, the notion of who Melanie Harper is, can be found elsewhere in the world than its origin. It can be represented. Reproduced.”

Near Harper: "Who thinks like that?"

Far Harper: “We all come from the same mind, don’t we?” <Smile> “Thank you for showing me what I am.”

< Near Harper produces the peace sign with her fingers and slumps. MacCullach returns several minutes later. Far Harper takes MacCullach’s hand and draws him towards her, placing her face against his. She tilts her head and regards MacCullach for some time. >

MacCullach: "Got something to say?"

Far Harper: “I love you, Glenn MacCullach.”

< Far Harper bites her tongue, drawing a stream of water, and kisses MacCullach. He struggles for some time before going limp. >

Evidentiary Item 8392.14/08/95-SD.2b “Effigy”

Description: A 168cm tall humanoid object made from packed bundles of sticks, hemp rope, and canvas. A laminated, full-body pencil sketch of Melanie Harper is taped to the thorax of the figure. Several aspects of the pencil sketch have been erased and altered, including reshading of the hair and adjustments to bodily proportions. The figure is dressed in feminine clothing and has a paper ribbon around its neck.

Ribbon Text: “Love of my life Melanie Harper, who loves me like I love her”
