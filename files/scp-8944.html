Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8944 is to remain within a standard anomalous humanoid containment chamber at Site-78. The containment chamber is equipped with multiple Scranton Reality Anchors to prevent SCP-8944 from using its anomalous abilities outside of scheduled testing. It is during periods of scheduled testing that SCP-8944 will be given opportunities to use its anomalous abilities to heal any damage sustained due to its secondary anomalous reaction. Notably, no amount nor degree of reality stabilizing apparatuses or other para-technology utilized by the Foundation appear to have an effect on the secondary anomalous reaction. Currently, the only known method of sating the secondary anomalous reaction, thus healing any / all damage sustained from it, is through the utilization of SCP-8944’s anomalous abilities in an act of thievery.

SCP-8944’s periods of scheduled testing are to take place within one of Site-78’s myriad exclusion chambers, small pockets of space strategically designed to fall outside of the influence of any reality stabilizing apparatuses utilized in the containment of Type Green entities and objects within the site. Due to the nature and specific locations of said exclusion chambers, the use of an anomaly’s abilities (in this case, those of an ontokinetic variety) is only possible within each chamber, as exiting its designated exclusion area would place the anomaly within the zone of influence of the reality stabilizing apparatuses and para-technology utilized at Site-78.

Due to the nature of SCP-8944’s scheduled testing involving the utilization of its anomalous abilities to perform various acts of thievery, its person is to be searched by a member of Foundation personnel before it is allowed to reenter its containment chamber at the conclusion of every test.

Description: SCP-8944 is an eighteen-year-old Caucasian male of European descent, originally from Harlem, New York City, prior to its transfer to Site-78. It is a Class-I Type Green humanoid possessing reality bending abilities which it utilizes exclusively to pickpocket or engage in other forms of thievery. It appears unable to utilize its ontokinetic abilities or manipulate baseline reality to any degree when it is not actively doing so with the intention of bringing something into its possession. Due to a secondary anomalous reaction of its primary ontokinetic abilities, the true extent of its potency as a Type Green is currently unknown.

SCP-8944’s secondary anomalous reaction will become active following a specific period of time in which it does not utilize its abilities. All data collected appears to indicate that the secondary anomalous reaction operates on a seven day timeline. Should SCP-8944 neglect to utilize its abilities for two days, it will begin to sustain physical damage to its body. The damage will continue to manifest with an exponential severity in accordance with the condition that SCP-8944’s abilities go without use.

While it cannot be tested and subsequently proven, all data collected supports the conclusion that following seven days without SCP-8944 utilizing its abilities, it would expire from the damage sustained due to its secondary anomalous reaction. Regardless of the quantity and severity of damage that SCP-8944 has sustained, the only known method of healing said damage is through the usage of its ontokinetic abilities in an act of thievery. However, this method exclusively heals the damage sustained by SCP-8944 due to its secondary anomalous reaction. It will not heal damage sustained from a third party or outside source.

The damage sustained by SCP-8944 following prolonged neglect of its ontokinetic abilities manifests as varying injuries consistent with those seen in cases of domestic / physical abuse.

Addendum - 8944.1: Discovery

Hide Addendum

SCP-8944 was discovered on November 17th, 2025, outside of a dive bar in Harlem, New York City, following an attempt to unknowingly pickpocket an agent of MTF Pi-1 (“City Slickers”) while she was off duty. The agent (code-named “Wichita”) was able to successfully place SCP-8944 under arrest due to her confidential position within the area’s local law enforcement. Later that night, SCP-8944 was transferred to the nearest Foundation containment facility following an incident in which it exposed its anomalous nature in an attempt to steal the key to its handcuffs from Agent Wichita’s person. When SCP-8944 was discovered by Agent Wichita, it had sustained severe damage, nearing the limit of its secondary anomalous reaction.

Following SCP-8944’s initial containment and the discovery that its anomalous abilities were ontokinetic in nature, it was immediately transferred to Site-78 in the town of Enth Degree, Oregon, where Foundation personnel specialize in the containment and study of Type Green entities and objects. Agent Wichita was transferred alongside SCP-8944 at the request of assigned project lead and Senior Researcher Simon Andvari, who believed that her confidential position within Harlem’s local law enforcement would be indispensable when researching the anomaly’s background and aiding in its containment. The masquerade of a death in the family was chosen by Agent Wichita to explain her leave of absence from the Harlem Police Department.

Addendum - 8944.2: Initial Interview

Hide Addendum

Interviewed: SCP-8944

Interviewer: Senior Researcher Simon Andvari (with Agent Wichita present)

Date: 11 / 19 / 2025

Foreword: With SCP-8944 in containment, the primary objective of this interview was to inquire about the location of its currently missing sibling and uncover whether or not she possessed similar (if any) ontokinetic abilities.

<BEGIN LOG>

SCP-8944 sits across from Andvari, a metal table between them. Wichita stands behind Andvari, her back agains a wall, and her arms crossed. Andvari clears his throat.

Andvari: …statement begins. Please state your name for the record.

SCP-8944 does not respond.

Andvari: State your name for the record, please.

SCP-8944 does not respond. Andvari clicks his tongue.

Andvari: …the anomaly’s documentation should reflect an unwillingness to comply with even the most standard of Foundation procedures. The anomaly’s name is Robin, last name pending, designation “SCP-8944.”

SCP-8944 does not respond. Andvari stares over the rim of his glasses.

Andvari: Moving along. How long have you been living on the streets of Harlem?

SCP-8944 does not respond.

Andvari: When did your… penchant for thievery first develop?

SCP-8944 does not respond. Andvari sighs.

Andvari: Do you know the whereabouts of your younger sister?

SCP-8944 immediately straightens in its chair, its eyes widening.

Andvari: Do I have your attention now?

When SCP-8944 speaks, the molars on the right-hand side of its mouth become visible through the deep laceration to the corner of its mouth.

SCP-8944: …do you think I’m an idiot? I’m not saying a word until my lawyer gets here which, by the way, you have to provide me with.

Andvari: You just said around 25 words without an attorney present.

SCP-8944 opens its mouth, pauses for a moment, then closes it.

Andvari: You won’t be provided with any legal representation.

SCP-8944 leans forward, placing two balled fists on the table in front of it.

SCP-8944: Hey, I know my rights—

Andvari interrupts.

Andvari: Then you must know that anything you say can and will be used against you in a court of law. And, again, I thought we weren’t talking without an attorney present?

SCP-8944 does not respond. It slowly leans back in its chair and crosses its arms. Behind Andvari, Wichita smiles.

Andvari: …you’re not being provided with legal representation because you’re not going to prison.

SCP-8944: …no?

Andvari: No. However, you’re not exactly walking free, either.

SCP-8944’s voice softens.

SCP-8944: …then what do you want from me?

Andvari leans forward, resting his elbows on the table in front of him.

Andvari: Why do you think that you’re here, 8944?

SCP-8944 does not respond. Andvari starts to speak, then SCP-8944 starts in kind.

SCP-8944: …me? I don’t know—

Andvari quickly interjects.

Andvari: You do. You do know.

SCP-8944’s gaze flicks toward Wichita before returning to Andvari.

SCP-8944: Look, I… I didn’t know what I was doing, alright? It was a mistake, I’m sorry, it won’t happen again.

Andvari does not respond. Beneath the table, one of SCP-8944’s legs begins to bounce.

SCP-8944: Come on, man. Can’t I, like, get off with a warning or something? I’m really sorry, I made a mistake!

Andvari: Why are you here, 8944?

SCP-8944: Stop calling me that.

Andvari: Why are you here?

SCP-8944 tilts its head to one side, genuine confusion visible on its face. It nods toward Wichita.

SCP-8944: …because I tried to steal something from her?

Andvari: …that’s not the answer I’m looking for.

SCP-8944 chokes out a laugh.

SCP-8944: I don’t know what the fuck you’re looking for, man! Are you gonna let me go or not?

Andvari: You think we don’t know what you are? What you’re capable of?

SCP-8944: …what?

Andvari: Why are you here? Come on, give me an ounce of honesty.

SCP-8944: I already told you, man! I tried to rob a cop—

It throws a hand in Wichita’s direction.

SCP-8944: —and she caught me! What more do you want from me?!

Andvari shakes his head.

Andvari: Try again.

SCP-8944 explodes, laughing wildly, throwing its hands into the air.

SCP-8944: I’m here because I’m a thief, isn’t that right?! A dirty, no-good thief!

Andvari: How?

SCP-8944 rolls its eyes.

SCP-8944: How, what?!

Andvari: How do you steal things?

SCP-8944 drops its elbows onto the table in front of it and buries its head into its hands, dragging its hands down its face.

SCP-8944: I—I—I don’t know how! I just do!

It turns its hands palms up and stares at them.

SCP-8944: I walk past someone and… and I just take from them, I don’t know! I think about having what belongs to them, and my hand twitches, and then their wallet is in my pocket or their watch is in my hand… is that what you want to hear?!

Andvari is silent for a moment before responding.

Andvari: …can your sister do the things that you can do, 8944?

SCP-8944 chokes out a humorless laugh.

SCP-8944: This is insane… this is fucking insane…

SCP-8944’s tongue darts out of its mouth to lick the wound.

Andvari: Where is your sister? If you tell us, we can bring her here. We can help you both, if you help us.

SCP-8944 stares forward, its gaze flicking between Andvari and Wichita. Following several seconds of silence, it responds.

SCP-8944: …fuck you.

Andvari sighs, clasping his hands together on the table in front of him.

Andvari: Where is she, 8944? You need to tell me so that we can get to her before someone else does.

SCP-8944 leans back and crosses its arms.

SCP-8944: Go fuck yourself.

Andvari: This is your last chance. I know it’s difficult to see now but we really are here to help you… both of you.

SCP-8944 does not respond. It looks toward Wichita.

SCP-8944: …how did you know that I was looking to rob you? I thought I was being pretty subtle.

Wichita: I’ve dealt with things that can do what you do in the past. I’ve honed my skills, my senses against them like a butcher sharpens a blade on a whetstone.

Wichita shrugs.

Wichita: …I also grew up in the city. I know when someone’s itching to pick my pockets.

SCP-8944: Christ… you’re all fucking crazy…

Andvari sighs, waving a dismissive hand toward the interview room’s door.

Andvari: Take it back to its containment chamber.

Wichita stands up straight and starts toward SCP-8944. It also shoots to its feet and takes a step back.

SCP-8944: Wait a minute, what’re you talking about?! You can’t just keep me here, YOU CAN’T FUCKING—

<END LOG>

Closing Statement: Following SCP-8944’s continued disunity to Foundation questioning, Senior Researcher Andvari enlisted the assistance of Foundation-sanctioned psychiatrist Alexander Sloane. Doctor Sloane was enlisted to conduct psychiatric evaluations of SCP-8944, with the express purpose of these sessions being to persuade it to reveal the location of its sibling as well as ensuring its compliance while testing is ongoing. Additionally, Senior Researcher Andvari tasked Agent Wichita with looking into SCP-8944’s background and Researcher Cassidy Moon with attempting to recover the remainder of the UIU’s report on SCP-8944 from one of the Foundation’s liaisons within the United States government.

Due to the increasing severity of SCP-8944’s secondary anomalous reaction, a list of proposed tests bordering on the esoteric have been postponed until a more in-depth understanding of the definite limits of said reaction can be identified, and pending approval from Senior Researcher Andvari. Several of the aforementioned postponed tests include (but are not limited to) having SCP-8944:

  • Steal an object that it has never seen (e.g., a mug from Researcher Moon’s desk)
  • Steal an object from an extreme distance (a palm-sized stone from the Appalachian Plateau)
  • Steal an object that does not exist (the “One Ring” from J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Lord of the Rings)
  • Steal an abstract concept (the color blue)

Test Log #1

Date: 11 / 21 / 2025

Testing Location: Exclusion Chamber 78-Φ

Foreword: Despite Doctor Sloane remaining in transit to Site-78, SCP-8944’s first test was deemed undemanding enough to conduct without a prior psychiatric evaluation. During the two day containment period preceding its first test, SCP-8944 advanced to the early stages of its secondary anomalous reaction. This stage is primarily characterized by small burns beginning to appear on SCP-8944’s skin. The burns typically manifest on its arms, ranging from its shoulders to its wrists. However, on multiple occasions, the burns have also manifested on its knees and thighs. The marks are consistent with standard burns one would receive from a lit cigarette.

Subject’s Mark: A wallet

Experiment: A member of Site-78’s Class-D personnel (D-8658) was given an empty wallet to place in his pocket and instructed to walk about Exclusion Chamber 78-Φ alongside four additional Class-D personnel, taking extra precautions to ensure that the wallet remained in his possession. SCP-8944 was outfitted with standard Class-D garb and instructed to take the wallet from D-8658 in no more than five minutes. Though initially hesitant to cooperate with Foundation staff, SCP-8944 was eventually convinced to participate when told that its cooperation with testing could reduce its time in Foundation containment. After the five minutes had passed, D-8658 was instructed to return the wallet, finding that it was no longer in his possession. When SCP-8944 was instructed to return the wallet, it returned it to Senior Researcher Andvari.

Conclusion: Upon reviewing surveillance footage of the test, it was noted that SCP-8944 completed its assigned task non-anomalously, approaching D-8658 from behind and taking the wallet from his pocket. This first test served to prove the hypothesis that any damage SCP-8944 sustains from its secondary anomalous reaction will not heal if SCP-8944 commits an act of thievery without the utilization of its ontokinetic abilities.

Additional Comments: Somewhat unrelated to SCP-8944’s first test, Researcher Moon and I are beginning to theorize about the peculiarities regarding how its ontokinetic abilities function. The UIU’s Standard Report Form correctly questions whether or not SCP-8944 is capable of utilizing its abilities to achieve an outcome that does not directly involve an act of thievery and / or an instance of bringing something into its possession. From what we have observed in the UIU’s reports and the data collected during our own burgeoning tests, Researcher Moon and I are inclined to agree that SCP-8944’s ontokinetic abilities function based on intention. Therefore, in order for SCP-8944 to be capable of bending reality, its intention must be to commit an act of thievery or otherwise bring something into its possession. But are SCP-8944’s intentions capable of being blurred, skewed in such a way that it becomes capable of utilizing its abilities in an effort to achieve an end not previously recognized? More testing is required.


Following SCP-8944’s first test, the searching of its person by a member of Foundation personnel prior to reentry into its containment chamber at the conclusion of every test began. A member of Site-78’s in-house security detail was chosen by Senior Researcher Andvari to conduct SCP-8944’s post-test searches, as well as accompany it to and from Exclusion Chamber 78-Φ and Doctor Sloane’s office.

Addendum - 8944.3: Psychiatric Evaluation, Session #1
⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀
Access Addendum
Hide Addendum

Interviewed: SCP-8944

Interviewer: Foundation-sanctioned psychiatrist Alexander Sloane

Date: 11 / 24 / 2025

Foreword: As previously stated, the primary objectives of Doctor Sloane’s psychiatric evaluations of SCP-8944 are to persuade it to reveal the location of its sibling, uncover whether or not she possesses similar (if any) ontokinetic abilities, and ensure that SCP-8944 remains compliant while testing is ongoing.

<BEGIN LOG>

Sloane: Test… test… test… 1, 2, 3… alright.

Sloane clears his throat.

Sloane: Robin Crowe should be arriving any minute now for our first psych eval per Simon’s orders.

Sloane reaches between his leg and the arm of his chair, grabbing a small notebook. He opens the notebook and flips through it, scanning over the pages. He reaches for the breast pocket of his coat and retrieves a fountain pen.

Sloane: …the goal today is to establish a good rapport with Robin, try to show him that despite his living conditions in containment and Simon’s testing, there are people here that want to make his stay more comfortable and easy to manage. Also, whether it’s during this session or another, I’d like to probe for a potential case of kleptomania. Drawing from the initial documentation on Robin that Simon provided me with, there’s definitely a possibility that Robin’s necessity to steal things in order to heal himself may have morphed into an addiction to the act of stealing itself… but I won’t force that discussion, certainly not during our first session.

Sloane pauses for a moment.

Sloane: I should break the ice by telling him something about myself… maybe he’ll feel more open to sharing later on if we cultivate a more accepting environment?

Sloane bites down onto the cap of the fountain pen and pulls it off, dropping it into his lap. He jots something down in his notebook.

Sloane: In any case, I’ll avoid diving too deep into the questions that Simon has tasked me with getting answers to. I’m sure that the opportunity will present itself later down the line… you’ve just got to be patient. We should start—

The door to Sloane’s office swings open and the member of Site-78’s in-house security detail charged with conducting SCP-8944’s post-test searches holds it open while SCP-8944 stands in the doorway. A small burn manifested from its secondary anomalous reaction is barely visible on its collar bone. Sloane closes his notebook, slots it next to the arm of his chair, and stands. He caps the fountain pen and clips it into his breast pocket.

Sloane: Right on time! Very punctual, I appreciate that.

Sloane looks toward the security guard accompanying SCP-8944.

Sloane: Thank you for escorting him, Jude. I can take it from here!

Jude nods and gently pushes SCP-8944 through the door before closing it behind it. It has yet to make eye contact with Sloane. Sloane gestures to an empty chair across from his own.

Sloane: Would you like to sit, Robin? You don’t have to, of course! But if you’re going to stand, I’m going to stand, too. So, I’ll leave the fate of my comfortability in your hands.

SCP-8944 begins to look around the room.

SCP-8944: Christ… I thought they were fucking with me when they said I’d have to see a shrink.

Sloane does not respond. SCP-8944 meets his gaze.

SCP-8944: …and if you ask me how that makes me feel, I’m going to lose my mind.

Sloane chuckles.

Sloane: Honestly, I was a bit surprised myself when they brought me in. You see, I typically work as a counselor for pets and their owners. You’d be surprised by just how much emotion can be conveyed through a single bark, meow, or tweet! Although one time, this young lady brought in her ant farm, and that one was a bit of a doozy. I’m 90% sure they were in the process of forming a union before I stepped in.

SCP-8944 raises an eyebrow.

SCP-8944: Are you serious?

Sloane smiles.

Sloane: No. That was a complete lie.

SCP-8944: Are shrinks even allowed to lie?

Sloane: There are… special circumstances in which lies are appropriate, yes. For example, as long as the lie is funny, I think it’s alright.

Sloane gestures to the empty chair across from his own.

Sloane: Take a seat, Robin.

SCP-8944 looks around the room, looks over Sloane, then slowly shuffles around the empty chair and sits down.

SCP-8944: …aren’t you going to call me “SCP-8944” like everyone else in this fucking place?

Sloane shrugs.

Sloane: Well, that depends. Are you going to call me “Foundation-sanctioned psychiatrist Alexander Sloane” like my bosses? Or are you just going to call me Sloane?

Sloane extends a hand toward SCP-8944. It flinches and slowly leans back in its chair. Sloane retracts his hand.

Sloane: …just call me Sloane, yeah?

SCP-8944: …Sloane it is.

Sloane smiles.

Sloane: Then Robin you are.

Sloane and SCP-8944 sit in silence for a moment before he speaks.

Sloane: Hey. Did you know that most psychiatrists… that’s what we’re called by the way, not shrinks. But most of us actually start out as clients of therapy ourselves!

SCP-8944: Since that’s not very funny, I take it you’re telling the truth. What was wrong with you?

Sloane: Another common misconception. People who seek out psychiatric assistance aren’t wrong, or broken, or anything like that. They just want help. And it’s never wrong to ask for help.

Sloane chuckles, leans back in his chair, and crosses his arms.

Sloane: I guess I was pretty fucked up at the time, though. You see, my father had recently passed away, which is what prompted me to seek out said psychiatric assistance in the first place.

A prolonged moment of silence passes before SCP-8944 responds.

SCP-8944: …oh. I’m sorry for your loss.

Sloane smiles.

Sloane: Thank you, Robin. His death left me with some… lingering and conflicting emotions regarding how I felt about the old man, so I sought out someone that I could talk through these emotions with. Don’t get me wrong, I loved my father. It’s just that the ways in which we chose to express that love was… stunted. Complicated. He was a very complicated man and, in the wake of his death, I found myself left to grieve with no closure to our relationship.

SCP-8944: …and did it help?

Sloane: I like to think that it did, if only a little. In my opinion, there’s no such thing as being fixed, there’s no fixity to achieve. Therapy isn’t a pill that you can pop twice a day to cure an infection, right? Sure, someone or something might go away, but your experiences with them will always be there… they’ll always be with you. And no matter how long it takes to accept that, your acceptance of it will reveal the path to nirvana. It’s sitting first class on the Polar Express, it’s a private tour of the Chocolate Factory! Right?

SCP-8944 clears its throat.

SCP-8944: …okay. Hey, that’s a nice pen.

SCP-8944 nods toward Sloane. He looks down at his breast pocket and unclips the fountain pen. The majority of the pen’s body is a glossy black, broken up only by bands of gold and varying width. Sloane holds the pen up in one hand, like an obsidian obelisk, and twists it with his fingers.

Sloane: Thank you… truth be told, I was wondering when you’d notice it since you seem to have such a penchant for shiny things that belong to other people.

Sloane slowly extends his hand toward SCP-8944. It hesitates for a moment before extending its own hand and taking the pen from Sloane.

SCP-8944: I mean, who doesn’t? That must date back to caveman times or something, getting excited over an extra shiny rock in a cave or the stars in a pitch black sky. If you think about it that way, I’m really just a slave to millions of years of evolution…

Sloane and SCP-8944 laugh. It finishes its examination of the pen and hands it back to Sloane.

Sloane: It seems we’re both haunted by the things that came before us, huh?

SCP-8944 chuckles, its laughter dying down, and nods.

SCP-8944: …it would seem that way.

Sloane sighs, fidgeting with the pen. He twists and twirls it in his hands, uncapping and recapping it.

Sloane: And like their storybook counterparts, ghosts of the past seem to haunt you wherever you go… my father gave me this pen on his deathbed.

SCP-8944 does not respond. Sloane chuckles, mostly to himself, then continues.

Sloane: He’d always fancied himself a writer, dreamed of writing the next “…great American novel,” whatever that meant. But… he never got around to it, I guess. You should’ve seen our garage, it was chock-full of boxes of notebooks containing half-baked ideas and random scrawlings. He gave me this pen in hopes that I’d use it to fulfill his dream for him but… I don’t consider myself much of a writer.

A moment passes before SCP-8944 responds.

SCP-8944: …my dad is dead, too.

Sloane: …ah. I’m sorry to hear that, Robin.

SCP-8944 shrugs.

SCP-8944: It’s alright. He was a piece of shit through and through, so don’t worry about it.

Sloane: Can I ask you a question?

SCP-8944 does not respond.

Sloane: …will you tell me what happened to your mouth?

SCP-8944 looks away. The teeth on the right-hand side of its mouth can be seen through the laceration to its lips.

SCP-8944: …I was running with a pair of scissors and slipped. My teachers always told me not to, I just wish that I’d listened.

Sloane: …right. Well, in any case, whoever dressed your wound did a terrible job. I’m shocked that it’s not infected.

SCP-8944 turns back toward Sloane.

SCP-8944: Oh, okay, so you’re a physician now? Shouldn’t you be showing me ink blots or something? Fuck you, man. Maybe they dressed my wound the best that they could with what they had!

Sloane raises an eyebrow and smirks. SCP-8944 looks away again.

SCP-8944: …that’s just a guess, though. I don’t know.

Sloane: Has anyone taken a look at it since you arrived?

SCP-8944 slowly shakes its head.

Sloane: Okay. I’ll send an on-site medic to your containment chamber after your test to take a look at it. Is that alright?

SCP-8944 slowly nods, turning back toward Sloane.

Sloane: Good. Thank you, Robin. While we’re on the topic of injuries, I’d like to talk about those if you’re willing to…

Sloane gestures to the small burns on SCP-8944’s body. It rolls up one sleeve of its containment jumpsuit, revealing several more burns scattered across its forearm, as well as slightly bruised skin farther up its arm.

SCP-8944: …what about them?

Sloane: You don’t find it strange that these injuries appear on your body out of nowhere?

SCP-8944: Not nowhere, it’s…

SCP-8944 shrugs.

SCP-8944: …look, it’s been happening for so long now that I’ve almost stopped noticing, you know? It’s like how when you walk past the same things every day they start to just… fade away.

Sloane: Robin. Did your father do that to you?

SCP-8944: …no? You’ve been here the entire time, haven’t you?

Sloane: Come on, kid. Not those burns specifically, just… did he burn you?

SCP-8944: Oh.

SCP-8944 looks down into its lap for a moment before returning Sloane’s gaze.

SCP-8944: …yeah, he did. I deserved it, though, I was a bratty kid… am a bratty kid, I guess. I just… I never knew when to shut up, you know?

Sloane leans forward.

It unclenched and opened its fist approximately ten seconds later and a small key was in its palm. The injuries manifested from its secondary anomalous reaction immediately began to heal themselves. It inserted the key into the padlock, removed the padlock, and opened the lockbox to find an identical key to the one in its hand inside. Rather than stealing the key from within the lockbox in order to unlock it, SCP-8944 had stolen the twin key that was used to lock the original key inside of the lockbox from a desk drawer within Senior Researcher Andvari’s office. When questioned as to its methods, SCP-8944 claimed that it had simply desired a way of accessing the lockbox’s contents. But lacking the required knowledge of its mark, its ontokinetic abilities interpreted its desire in an unexpected manner. Following this test, the strength of all Scranton Reality Anchors immediately adjacent to Exclusion Chamber 78-Φ were increased.

Additional Comments: As our knowledge regarding SCP-8944 has deepened since its containment, so to do the theories developed by Researcher Moon and I regarding the disclosing intricacies of its capabilities as a Type Green entity. The effort to extremity ratio of this third test (i.e. the energy expended in relation to the difficulty of the task) is leading us to believe that there exists a more complex relationship between SCP-8944’s ontokinetic abilities and its secondary anomalous reaction than was previously believed. SCP-8944 expended less energy when compared to its previous tests, despite its assigned task theoretically necessitating an increase in effort on its behalf. Simply put, in a similar process to adrenaline boosting oxygen delivery to the muscles, the more pain that SCP-8944 is experiencing, the more heightened, almost desperate, its ontokinetic abilities become in order to heal its body. However, as we’ve already discovered, the utilization of SCP-8944’s ontokinetic abilities exclusively heal the injuries that it’s sustained through its secondary anomalous reaction, leaving injuries sustained from an outside source or third party completely unaffected. There are so many possibilities to consider. More testing is required.

Sloane: Of course.

SCP-8944: Same old, same old. Meh.

SCP-8944: …is there anything that you’d like to talk about?

Sloane laughs uncomfortably.

Sloane: They finally coughed up a part of their original report on you that we’d been missing. But before we talk about it… I’d prefer to hear about what happened from you.

SCP-8944: What’re you…

It slowly trails off. Its eye widens and it seems to sink into its chair.

SCP-8944: …they found him, didn’t they?

Tears begin to gather at the corners of its eyes.

SCP-8944: Look, I—I didn’t mean to, it—it was an accident, I swear!

It bites down onto its lower lip and swipes at its eyes with its uninjured hand.

SCP-8944: Fuck, fuck, fuck… you’re not gonna send me to jail, are you?! I—I—I—

Sloane leans forward.

Sloane: Robin, we’re not sending you anywhere. Trust me. And even if they were going to, I wouldn’t let them, not on my life.

SCP-8944 is sucking in gasping breaths.

SCP-8944: That’s why they were all acting so weird… they fucking knew!

Sloane: What? Who was acting weird?

SCP-8944: Simon, his assistant… during our last test you’d think that they were defusing a bomb!

Sloane sighs.

Sloane: Yeah, yeah… that sounds about right. Simon’s always been more obsessed with protocol than he is with results and Cassidy? Well… a bit of a brown-noser if you ask me.

SCP-8944 swipes at its nose with the back of its uninjured hand, laughter bubbling up as its tears begin to subside.

SCP-8944: I can see that.

Sloane: But… at the same time…

Sloane rakes a hand through his hair.

Sloane: …can you entirely blame them? It was an intense report to read, kid.

SCP-8944’s smile drops and it looks away.

SCP-8944: I didn’t mean to, it just… it just happened. I don’t know what else to say.

A moment passes. Sloane’s eyes widen.

Sloane: It was the first time your father hit Wren… wasn’t it?

SCP-8944 chokes out a strangled and humorless laugh. Its eyes sting red with fresh tears.

SCP-8944: No. It was the second time.

Sloane nods slowly. SCP-8944 continues.

SCP-8944: …the first time that he hit her, I just stood back and watched. I listened as she cried, pleaded with him to stop. It chokes out a sobbing laugh.

But then… it did happen again. And I knew. I knew what he was truly capable of. I knew that because he’d hit her a second time, there would be a third time, and a fourth, and a fifth, and sixth.

It pauses for a moment before continuing.

SCP-8944: It’s funny… it feels like it happened yesterday but I can’t even remember what she could’ve done to set him off like that. It could’ve been anything… not even five o’clock and he was piss drunk. She probably didn’t even do anything at all.

It turns its gaze downward.

SCP-8944: …it started like it always did. With screaming… screaming so loud that I could feel it resonate in my bones, even if I was miles away. I’d know. I could recite every word like it was a poem. But… life goes on, you know? She must’ve said something between her pointless apologies and pleas for mercy that didn’t sit quite right with him because the next thing that I know, my ears are ringing, abuzz with the sound of skin hitting skin. I decide that it’s time to get involved like the fucking superhero that I am.

It appears to sink into the chair.

SCP-8944: …I tried to get between them but he wouldn’t let me. I told him to stop. I screamed at him to stop, to leave her alone. I begged him to just forget about it, just this once. I said that I’d walk the streets of our neighborhood day and night for an entire week, an entire month, stealing for him. I said that I’d walk the entire length of Manhattan, anything to get his attention away from her, and onto me. He didn’t care. And I didn’t know what to do. I cursed him out, I threw every garbage word that I knew at him. I called him a coward, told him to beat on somebody his own size… that got his attention.

It pauses for a moment before continuing.

SCP-8944: He dropped her. He walked into the kitchen like he was taking a field sobriety test and pulled a butcher’s knife from the knife block. For a moment, I actually thought that he was going to kill me, right then and there. And part of me… a small part of me would’ve been fine with that. I was tired, I… I didn’t know how much more I could take. In that split second, I glimpsed an entire lifetime. My father was intimately familiar with the law and its list of do’s and don’ts but not enough to get away with murder. I would’ve hated leaving Wren behind but in that lifetime, with him out of the picture and her placed into foster care, I let myself dream that maybe, just maybe, there was some semblance of a better life waiting for her. Away from all of this… without me.

SCP-8944’s eyelids droop. It stares blankly, expressionless at nothing in particular as tears flow freely down its cheeks. It draws its knees to its chest, wrapping its arms around them.

SCP-8944: …but he didn’t kill me. Of course he didn’t. He forced me to the ground like it was nothing, like I was a piece of paper. He got on top of me and I couldn’t move. Wren was screaming, crying… he stuck the knife in my mouth and I couldn’t move.

It hugs its legs tighter, burying its chin into its knees.

SCP-8944: He said that he always knew my mouth would get me into trouble one day… he just didn’t know that it’d be by his own hand. He said that I just never learned when to shut up.

Tears drip onto the pants of its containment jumpsuit en masse, coloring the orange a mottled brown.

It chokes out a sobbing laugh.

SCP-8944: My mouth was full of so much blood that I thought I’d drown before he was finished. My face felt like it was on fire. I struggled beneath him but there was nothing I could do. She slammed her tiny fists against his back, still screaming, still crying, telling him to get off of me, to leave me alone. I watched her but I couldn’t say anything, he wouldn’t let me. I tried to talk to her with my eyes, somehow tell her to get away. Let him air his frustrations with me, I can handle it. I’m still your big brother, I can still protect you.

It lets out a shuddering sigh and its fists tighten around its legs.

SCP-8944: But it was too late. He’d finally had enough of her. He told her to shut the fuck up, to quit her bitching, and sent an elbow back at her. It collided with her nose and she screamed… God, she screamed…

Its eyebrows furrow.

SCP-8944: Blood gushed from her, I… it was all that I could see. The red was all that I could see. She collapsed, blood pouring from her nose, and he just… he got back to work on my mouth. I didn’t allow myself a second to think before my palm was pressed against his chest. I was so tired. I just wanted everything to be over, I just… I don’t know.

It draws in and exhales a shaky breath. It shrugs.

SCP-8944: …next thing I know, his heart is in my hand, and he’s dead weight on top of me. His hand was still gripping the knife’s handle, its blade still in my mouth. I couldn’t tell if the metal I was tasting was from the knife or the blood. Wren was crying.

It wraps its arms tighter around its legs.

SCP-8944: She was crying and that was all that mattered. I wrapped his body in a bedsheet and stuffed him in the closet. I was getting us ready to run when the cops showed up… and you know the rest, I guess.

Silence. An impossibly dense silence permeates the room for what feels like hours. SCP-8944 stares at the floor. Sloane stares at it. He slowly removes his glasses, folds the temples inward, and places it in his lap.

Sloane: Robin, I… I don’t…

SCP-8944 sniffles and wipes its uninjured eye with the sleeve of its containment jumpsuit. It hesitantly returns Sloane’s gaze and attempts to smile.

SCP-8944: It’s okay, Sloane. Thank you… for listening. Even though it’s your job.

SCP-8944 laughs. Sloane does not.

Sloane: Thank you, Robin. Thank you for telling me. I… I struggle to imagine the pain that you’ve endured just to end up here. And the worry that you must feel for Wren, the pressure that you shoulder… I don’t… no words can…

Sloane trails off. A moment passes. He leans forward.

Sloane: …none of this, none of what happened, is your fault. Robin. Listen to me.

SCP-8944 is trembling. Sloane stands and steps forward. It flinches and leans back in its chair. He freezes. Only when it begins to settle does he slowly step forward again, crouching down next to its chair.

Sloane: …it’s your father’s fault, Robin. He’s to blame, not you. If anyone were to be arrested, taken away, it would’ve been him. Not you.

Tears begin to roll down SCP-8944’s face.

SCP-8944: But I killed him! I killed him, he’s dead! …but he’s not really gone, is he?

SCP-8944: I’m such an idiot… God, it’s pathetic. I let him put his hands on me too many times and now they won’t come off… I can’t get them off…

Sloane: He can’t touch you anymore, Robin. He can’t hurt you, or Wren, or anyone else. You saved her. You saved yourself. You were doing your job as an older sibling, you—

SCP-8944: Tell that to them!

It nods toward the door.

SCP-8944: Contrary to popular belief, I don’t enjoy hurting people. I don’t want to hurt anyone.

Sloane: Come on now, nobody thinks that!

SCP-8944: Then why do they all look at me like I’m a monster, like I’m some sort of freak?!

SCP-8944 buries its head into its knees.

SCP-8944: Yes, she does! She does… you just don’t see it. You do too, I know you do. You’re afraid of me.

Sloane: I’m not afraid of you, Robin, I’m afraid for you! I…

Sloane pauses for a moment before continuing.

Sloane: …I can’t stand seeing you like this, in this state that they’re keeping you in! I don’t want you to get hurt, I just… I want you to be safe.

SCP-8944 slowly leans into the arm of the chair between it and Sloane.

Sloane: I can talk to Simon, I can get him to stop! I know that I can! But if I don’t have anything to give him, he won’t listen to me. He won’t. Wren. Can you tell me anything about where she might be? About what she might be? What she can do? Anything at all? I just want to help, Robin, I just want to…

Sloane trails off. He lowers his forehead to the arm of the chair.

Sloane: …please. I just want to help.

SCP-8944: I… I can’t…

A moment passes.

Sloane: …okay. That’s okay, Robin. We can figure something out. We’ll find another way through this.

Sloane picks up the cup of water from the floor and hands it to SCP-8944. It looks from the cup, to him, and back. It takes the cup and slowly begins to drink. Sloane walks to his chair and sits down. Approximately one minute later, there is a knock at the door.

Sloane: …that’s time.

SCP-8944 stands, sets the empty cup down, and steps toward the door.

SCP-8944: Thank you, Sloane.

Sloane nods.

Sloane: You’re welcome, Robin.

SCP-8944 opens the door. It glances at Jude briefly, then turns away, walking out of the room. She looks at Sloane. He shrugs. She nods and closes the door.

Sloane: …supplemental. This is it. Robin’s inability to utilize his ontokinetic abilities for anything outside of acts of thievery has nothing to do with the limits of his capabilities as a Type Green and everything to do with his secondary anomalous reaction. Robin wasn’t born this way… with burns, and bruises, and cuts appearing on his skin like the world’s worst magic show. No. He became what we see before us through years and years of mental and physical abuse suffered at the hands of his father, the very same man who instilled into him a crippling fear that if he didn’t use his incredible abilities to steal, rob, and thieve, he’d be beaten within an inch of his life… and his sister would be left without her only bastion of salvation.

Sloane pauses for a moment before continuing.

Sloane: The Foundation is no stranger to the unquantifiable ways in which a potent enough reality bender can affect the environment around them at will. But what we rarely discuss… or, perhaps, what we choose to avoid discussing… is how a reality bender’s environment, pre-containment, can affect them. How it can change them, mold them and their abilities in those subtle and invisible ways that our surroundings are apt to do. Robin is, of course, one such example… albeit an extreme one. I just…

Sloane: …I need more time with him. We’re moving too quickly, I… I need more time.

Interlude: Written in Ink

Alexander Claudius Sloane was kicked out of his home by his mother when he was 16 years old.

Alexander bounced from couch to couch for a while before landing with distant relatives a few states over. While living with the family of his second cousin, he conquered an addiction to oxycodone that had grown inside of him like a parasite, slowly commanding more and more of his attention, more and more of his energy, his strength. He had been involved in an accident when he was 14 years old, you see, and required the painkiller in order to dull the constant ache pulsing from his shattered ribs and broken leg. His mother was fine, though, don’t worry. The driver’s side airbag released without issue.

Alexander received his GED diploma when he was 20 years old. He applied to the farthest community college in the state and was accepted, even being offered a merit scholarship for showing promise in the writing field. When deciding what to study, he landed on psychology because he thought that it would be his easiest path to receiving a doctorate and, therefore, making the big bucks. He also liked the idea of people referring to him as “Doctor Sloane” very much. And, well, when it came down to a coin toss between psychology and business, he trusted the coin to make the right decision more than he trusted himself.

Alexander graduated, gathered all of his earthly possessions into the second row and trunk of his clunker sedan, and moved to a nearby city. He signed a lease on a small apartment and managed to pay rent for the first few months with cash that he’d been saving since he was a child. His job hunt came to end when he was offered a position at a local psychiatric clinic, where he would work alongside two additional licensed therapists. This was it, the start of something beautiful. It was all going to be downhill from here, in the sense that walking down a hill is easier than climbing up one.

Alexander’s first client was a young woman who was three months into the first semester of her freshman year at the Ivy League university whose acceptance she had committed to. She expressed to him that she felt as if she didn’t belong, as if everyone around her was deserving of their presence while her own must’ve been a cosmic fluke. Her academic performance had seen a substantial decline from what her father had come to consider average and her social life appeared entirely nonexistent. He met with her for just shy of one year as they discussed her feelings of inadequacy. He had always detested talking to doctors and strived to cultivate a less interrogative, more casual atmosphere amongst his clientele. Toward the end of their time together, they even began to see each other as friends of a sort. In fact, to this day, he still has dinner with her family whenever the opportunity arises. She’s a good kid, a smart kid, even graduating Cum Laude, or so he’d heard.

Alexander was approached by the young woman’s father when their time together had finally come to an end. The father was beyond thrilled with the progress that his daughter had made over the past year and exceedingly appreciative of Alexander for his assistance. The father asked Alexander if he would be interested in a potential job opportunity, claiming that the foundation at which he worked as a Senior Researcher was currently hiring within their Ethics Committee. Alexander, in turn, asked the father how much money he would be making in a polite sort of way that beat so far around the bush as to miss the forest entirely. The father threw out an estimate given Alexander’s age and the position that he would be applying for. Alexander immediately accepted and was hired two weeks later, due in no small part to the father’s glowing recommendation. Or so he’d heard.

All of Alexander’s colleagues were brilliant. He deeply admired their ability to build upon the foundations of their knowledge in pursuit of an understanding of the Pandora’s Box of esoterica that was opened to him. Each one of them had been brilliant prior to joining the Foundation and their excited willingness to toss facts and information that they’d previously taken as gospel out the window to achieve a higher understanding of the reality around them was inspiring. They were so amazing, so adaptable, while Alexander’s own job remained wholly unchanged from its civilian counterpart. He was still just a therapist. A shrink. He had zero excuses for not excelling in a position that he knew so well, not while everyone around him excelled, thrived despite the foundations of their own respective fields crumbling to dust with the introduction of the anomalous. Alexander had to excel. He had to be the best psychiatrist in his department, in the Foundation, on the planet. He would be.

Alexander Claudius Sloane felt as if he did not belong.

Foundation-sanctioned psychiatrist Alexander Sloane walked out of the containment chamber feeling much lighter than he’d felt when he entered just an hour earlier. Of course, despite feeling as if the weight of a 120 pound (soaking wet) young man had been lifted from his shoulders, Doctor Sloane knew that there was virtually zero chance of him catching even one wink of sleep. In fact, he hadn’t been getting much sleep at all as of late. Each night, as he lied down and closed his eyes, rather than counting sheep jumping over a fencepost, he’d himself counting how many burns he was able to see scattered across Robin’s body during their latest session, despite nearly his entire body being wrapped in blood-stained bandages. Every time he closed his eyes, all that he could make out in that choking blackness was the aging purple-yellow of the bruises on his legs, the torn flesh at the corner of his mouth, monsters with long tentacles and snapping beaks, the kind you’d only find at the bottom of a bottle. But today, that was alright with Doctor Sloane. What were a few more sleepless nights? He had finally stood up for the kid, stood up for himself. Doing instead of just saying. Forget sheep jumping over fenceposts, he had enough spring in his step to leap over a building.

Doctor Sloane flashed a toothy grin at Jude as he exited the containment chamber, adjusted his glasses, and thanked her for her help. He said that he’d see her soon and she nodded. She was a woman of few words, that much he’d picked up on. Although, now that he thought about it, not many of his colleagues outside of the Ethics Committee ever made a real effort to go out of their way and talk to him. If he had to guess, it’s because of his position as a psychiatrist within the Foundation. The most interesting thing about him is the fact that he conducts psychiatric evaluations with the people in containment but he can’t break doctor-patient confidentiality, not with those who have nothing to do with each respective client. And if he can’t discuss work at the water cooler, he supposes that he understands why his colleagues keep their distance. He guesses that it may be difficult for people to talk to psychiatrists without feeling as if they’re being analyzed themselves, regardless of the setting. He guesses that it’s difficult to ignore the buzz on the back of your neck, the quiet voice in your head telling you to watch what you say around him. One minute, you’re talking about how stressed you are, or about a new project that you were assigned to, or about a strange dream that you had. And the next, you’re on a mandatory mental health leave of absence, or you’re psychologically unfit to do your job, or you’ve been infected by a cognitohazardous tarot deck that makes you dream about your own death. Like he said, he supposes that he understands. But not even those thoughts could dampen his mood as he walked the hallways of Site-78, humming the tune of a song that he can’t quite remember the name of.

When Doctor Sloane arrived back at his office, he grabbed his notebook from atop his chair, and sat down at his desk to jot down some quick notes about the session. This was a little post-session ritual that he’d gotten into the habit of doing after allowing himself some time (in this case, the walk from Robin’s containment chamber to his office) to process what he and his client had discussed. His notes would often vary. They might be about what progress was made (or ground lost) regarding what topics, how his clients had been behaving, what he could improve upon regarding his own approach. And, if nothing came to mind on any given day, what he’d enjoyed about the session. This was not one of those days. He found that he had much to write about regarding his most recent session with Robin, much indeed. He peeled back the softcover of the notebook, turned to the most recent page, and was settling in his seat to write when something unusual happened. Doctor Sloane had the first bullet point all written out in his head, he could practically see the ink on the page. But when he gently grasped at the breast pocket of his coat…

…his fountain pen wasn’t there.

Andvari turns toward Sloane, his teeth grit.

Andvari: You… you—you—you—

Moon: Use your words, Simon.

Andvari’s head whips between facing Moon and Sloane.

Andvari: …words? You want me to use my words?! Words can’t even begin to describe—

Sloane steps toward Wichita. He can barely speak, finding every syllable caught in his throat.

Sloane: …W—Where did he…

The question goes unfinished. Wichita sticks out her thumb and points it at her face.

Wichita: Right into its—

Sloane moves toward a small trash can in the corner of the room, tripping over himself as he falls to his knees, and vomits.

Andvari: Christ almighty… would you please just give me a break?! What is it, Alexander? Are you sad that the anomaly hurt itself? Or maybe your guilty conscience at giving it the keys to the fucking castle is finally catching up with you? You know, none of this would’ve happened if you’d just followed protocol, and didn’t acquiesce to a psych eval inside of its goddamn containment chamber!

Wichita steps forward and places a hand on Andvari’s shoulder.

Wichita: Simon. Reprimands can wait. We have more pressing matters to attend to.

Andvari puffs out several more ragged breaths before beginning to settle.

Andvari: Right, you’re… you’re right. Why don’t you get a team together and—

The door to the containment chamber slides open. Jude rushes inside, trailed by three additional members of Site-78’s in-house security detail. Wichita nods at them and gestures to the fissure.

Wichita: I trust that Jude has briefed you all regarding our current situation. Go out and start the search. Begin to establish a perimeter within a two mile radius of Site-78. It’s injured, it couldn’t have gotten far. I’m right behind you.

Jude nods and looks back at the three behind her, who each nod in kind.

Jude: Come on, let’s move.

The four agents run through the fissure, disappearing into the forest beyond. Andvari clears his throat.

Andvari: …right. Good work.

Wichita nods, quickly backpedaling toward the fissure.

Wichita: One of you, I don’t care which, keep a radio handy. I’ll call into channel two if we find anything.

She turns and runs through the fissure, disappearing into the forest beyond. Sloane clumsily turns over, sitting on the floor with his back against a wall. He swipes the back of his hand across his mouth and hangs his head.

Sloane: …I don’t understand. I don’t understand!

Andvari sighs and crosses his arms.

Andvari: What don’t you understand, Alexander?

Sloane: The reality anchors. Why didn’t they stop him from using his abilities? And on top of that, why didn’t his injuries heal themselves afterward? With the state that he was in, there is zero chance that I wouldn’t have noticed.

Andvari leans his head back and pinches the bridge of his nose.

Andvari: Damn it, Alexander, it’s because it didn’t use its abilities! If I had to guess, it swiped the pen from you normally while you were distracted.

Sloane’s gaze softens, his eyes unfocused. He sniffs and rubs his eyes.

Sloane: Huh… ain’t that a bitch?

Andvari: I have to go make some calls in preparation for the calls that I’m going to have to answer. You two—

Andvari points two extended fingers at Moon and Sloane.

Andvari: —just… be on standby. We’ll debrief when Wichita gets back.

He shifts his full attention onto Sloane.

Andvari: …and try not to give any other anomalies a means of breaching containment while I’m gone. The Ethics Committee will hear about this, Alexander. It’s just a shame that I suspect they’ll be much more lenient with you than my superiors will be with me.

Andvari moves toward the door. As he passes the cot, he plucks Sloane’s fountain pen from it, flicking excess blood onto the floor. The door slides open.

Sloane: You asked me what was wrong earlier.

Andvari stops in the doorway. He doesn’t move.

Sloane: …you’re right.

He chuckles.

Sloane: God, you’re always right, aren’t you? That must be nice.

He pauses for a moment before continuing.

Sloane: I shouldn’t have held our last psych eval in here. It was against protocol, I should’ve ran the idea past you first, whatever. I shouldn’t have done it. But…

He pauses again.

Sloane: I just…

He looks up at Andvari’s back and shrugs, tears gathering at the corners of his eyes.

Sloane: I didn’t want him to be in any more pain. I didn’t want him to hurt. I’m sorry.

An eternity seems to pass in a moment before Andvari exits the containment chamber without a word, the door sliding closed behind him. Sloane sighs and rakes a hand through his hair.

Sloane: Shit…

Moon turns back toward the fissure, appearing spellbound by its very existence.

Moon: I don’t understand… I can’t understand how this happened.

Sloane chokes out a laugh.

Sloane: Not you too, kid. What’s there not to understand?

Moon: After so many tests, after…

Moon pauses for a moment before continuing.

Moon: …after so much pain. I just thought that we’d finally nailed down how its ontokinetic abilities functioned.

Sloane: How do you mean?

Moon extends both arms and gestures around them, turning to face Sloane.

Moon: 8944 had never shown any signs that it could alter physical space by way of its ontokinetic abilities. The Hume levels that we recorded during its tests were entirely average when compared against a larger pool of Class-I Type Green entities currently in containment. And on top of that, according to the UIU’s report form, our own observations, and its own accounts, it should’ve only been capable of utilizing its abilities to steal things… and I know, I know that we should take what the anomaly claims with a grain of salt. But this? There’s no way that it could’ve hidden something like this from us. There’s no way.

Sloane chuckles to himself.

Sloane: You’re new, right?

Moon shrugs.

Moon: Relatively speaking, I suppose. I’ve been with the Foundation for… just under two years by now.

Sloane nods.

Sloane: Happy early anniversary, then. Let this experience be a cornerstone of your, hopefully, long career… knock on wood.

He raps his knuckles on the trash can and a metallic CLANG echoes throughout the containment chamber.

Sloane: …it’s the thought that counts. Anyway, let this teach you a valuable lesson. Right now, you’re looking at things from a binary perspective. Ones or zeros, X or Y, right or wrong, and so on. You’ll quickly realize that rarely, and I mean almost never, is something that the Foundation encounters black and white.

He gestures aimlessly in the fissure’s direction. Moon looks from him, to it, and back.

Moon: …I’m sorry, I don’t understand.

Sloane smiles.

Sloane: Don’t apologize. You were right. Robin can exclusively utilize his abilities to steal things. We all just underestimated him. He used the unimaginable pain of… what he did… like fuel, feeding the potency of his ontokinetic abilities like a cigarette catching some dry brush and turning a spark into an inferno. Do you understand? Robin’s containment breach was a theft. He couldn’t escape, so he stole his freedom. He stole the outside world into his containment chamber.

Moon chokes out an appalled laugh.

Moon: But that… no, that—that—I mean… the level of reality bending that that would take is—is—is—

His own rant is cut off by a yawn. Sloane slowly stands, steps toward him, and places a gentle hand on his shoulder.

Sloane: Get some sleep, Cassidy. I’ll deal with Simon and Wichita for the time being.

Moon rubs one eye with the back of his hand.

Moon: Are you sure? I don’t mind waiting.

Sloane: I’m sure. Go on. I’ll get you when he’s ready for us.

Moon: …alright.

Moon walks across the containment chamber, toward the door, tossing a wave over his shoulder as he exits.

Moon: …see you later, Mr. Sloane.

Sloane smiles and returns the wave before turning back toward the fissure. He walks to SCP-8944’s abandoned cot and sits down, his eyes never leaving the fissure. A veil of snowfall slowly drifts into the containment chamber, turning drops of crimson blood a bleary pink.

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8944 is not currently in containment. Any and all information regarding the whereabouts of SCP-8944 and / or its sibling are to be reported directly to Senior Researcher Simon Andvari.

Description: SCP-8944 is a Class-III Type Green entity possessing exceedingly potent ontokinetic abilities which it is capable of utilizing exclusively to engage in varying forms of thievery. The degree to which SCP-8944 is capable of manipulating localized reality shares a positive correlation to the degree of physical pain that it is experiencing. The upper limit of SCP-8944’s capabilities as a Type Green entity are currently unknown.

Debrief Log Transcript

Date: 12 / 10 / 2025

Foreword: The morning following SCP-8944’s containment breach, Senior Researcher Andvari called for a meeting with Doctor Sloane, Researcher Moon, and Agent Wichita. This meeting was held within one of Site-78's staff lounges.

<BEGIN LOG>

Wichita enters the room. Andvari is pacing around the room, Moon is standing in place, and Sloane is sitting on a nearby couch. Andvari whips toward her as she enters the room, pointing to the door.

Andvari: Lock it.

Wichita nods, locking the door, and joining the others further into the room.

Andvari: Due to your radio silence, I’m going to wager a guess that you didn’t recover the anomaly?

Wichita shakes her head.

Wichita: No. We didn’t just not find it… we couldn’t even find a trace of it.

Andvari sighs and buries his head into his hands.

Andvari: …this is perfect. Fucking perfect. I have a meeting with McNaught and The Warden2 later today and I have nothing to give them, nothing to stop the axe from falling!

Sloane sits up and leans forward.

Sloane: Simon, why would the Site Director and the Overseers be angry with you? This was my fault and it’s only right that I fall on my sword for it. None of you deserve to be punished for my mistake.

Andvari shakes his head.

Andvari: A noble gesture, I suppose, but ultimately a pointless one. Even if you went to McNaught or, God forbid, the Overseers and took responsibility for the containment breach, the blame would still fall onto me. You’re a glorified shrink, Sloane.

Sloane frowns.

Andvari: I was assigned project lead, I was the one who brought you on board in the first place. And they’re not angry because I enlisted your help, they’re angry because one of the most potentially dangerous reality benders this side of Abaddon escaped from containment under my watch.

Sloane looks toward Moon, raises an eyebrow, and mouths the word “Abaddon?” Moon shrugs. Andvari sighs and pinches the bridge of his nose.

Andvari: Just… just don’t question me right now, alright?

Moon clears his throat.

Moon: Simon? I’m starting to put together a theory as to why I think we won’t find 8944 anywhere near Site-78.

Andvari nods.

Andvari: …alright. Let’s hear it.

Moon: Well, I don’t think that the hole it created in the wall of its containment chamber is just… you know, an ordinary, everyday hole in the wall.

Andvari: …mhm.

He continues.

Moon: We know that 8944 is incapable of utilizing its ontokinetic abilities to alter its surrounding environment. So, with that in mind, it must have breached by physically “stealing” the forest outside of Site-78, bringing the outside world to it, and allowing it to escape.

He glances toward Sloane, who smiles and nods.

Moon: 8944’s potency as a reality bender, taken at face value and with all relevant stipulations to said ontokinetic abilities in mind, are nowhere near unordinary when compared against the standard deviation of Hume levels recorded during tests of the Foundation’s larger pool of Class-I Type Green entities currently in containment. However, what distinguishes 8944 in particular from that larger pool is the extraordinary degree to which its Hume level is capable of fluctuating, due to the unique secondary anomalous reaction that was imprinted upon it.

He pauses for a moment before continuing.

Moon: The relationship between 8944’s primary ontokinetic abilities and its secondary anomalous reaction is simple: the more pain that it is in, the greater degree of reality bending that it is capable of. As we know, it was able to breach containment by utilizing the self-inflicted pain of impaling its eye with Doctor Sloane’s pen.

Sloane casts his gaze downward.

Moon: But how can we quantify pain? The human body can be dealt an unimaginable amount of pain before death is permitted to occur. So, that begs the question… if death is the definitive limit to the amount of pain that 8944 can be subjected to, what is the limit to how powerful its reality bending can become in tandem with its experienced pain and prior to its death?

Andvari: …okay. Go on.

Moon: This unknown ceiling, this X factor, is what makes 8944 such a potentially dangerous anomaly. So, to what degree did the pain of stabbing its eye boost its reality bending capabilities? We don’t know. Maybe it heightened them just enough to allow it to create a fissure in its containment chamber leading immediately outside of Site-78. But maybe…

Andvari: …maybe it increased its ontokinetic abilities to such a degree that it was able to “steal” anywhere outside of the site, able to create a fissure capable of taking it anywhere…

Moon nods.

Moon: Right. It’s not a fissure at all, it’s more like a… a gate. It’s own personal way, one that only it can access.

Andvari: It’s a tear in the fabric of space itself, it’s—it’s—

Sloane: …a bend in reality?

Andvari pauses for a moment, then sighs.

Andvari: …yes. Thank you, Sloane.

Wichita: So, the true objective is figuring out where it chose to flee in the first place, then.

Andvari nods.

Andvari: Yes… good work, Cassidy. I’ll make sure to mention your theory during my meeting.

From his spot on the couch, Sloane gently pokes Moon with his elbow, smiling up at him. Moon smiles back.

Andvari: Now, aside from its creation, have we made any progress while studying the way?

Moon nods.

Moon: It’s currently being monitored by a small team of researchers and appears to be slowly regressing toward a non-anomalous state as the recorded Hume level of the containment chamber begins to settle at numbers closer to what we’d expect from most Class-I Type Green entities. Although, I’d like your approval to continue surveillance of the containment chamber for the time being. We’ve found ourselves a bit… confounded regarding 8944’s ability to completely alter the geography of Site-78 while confining its manipulations exclusively within its containment chamber. As I’m sure you all know, 8944’s containment chamber did not share an outer wall with the exterior of Site-78, leaving the containment chamber quite non-euclidean in the wake of its breach.

Andvari nods.

Andvari: Right, right. You have my approval, Cassidy.

He glances at Sloane before returning Moon’s gaze.

Andvari: Speaking of, what was 8944’s recorded Hume level at the time of its containment breach? The more numbers and raw data that I can bring into this meeting, the better.

Moon clears his throat, retrieving a tablet from a nearby table.

Moon: Well, I checked the counters last night…

Moon glances at Sloane.

Moon: …I couldn’t sleep much. But I ran the numbers again, and again, and again, and I… I can’t really make any sense of them.

Andvari raises an eyebrow.

Andvari: …what do you mean?

Moon taps the tablet’s screen several times.

Moon: Well… according to all of the Kant counters active at the time of the containment breach, 8944 was seven thousand, seven hundred, and seventy-seven…

Andvari stares at Moon, his mouth agape.

Moon: …and a half horseshoe crabs above baseline reality while simultaneously being seven thousand, seven hundred, and seventy-seven and a half…

Moon narrows his eyes at the tablet and moves it closer to his face.

Moon: …quantum wave allocations below baseline reality.

A moment passes. Sloane slings an arm over the back of the couch and turns toward Moon.

Sloane: Wait a minute, don’t our Kant counters only go to 500?

A moment passes.

Andvari: …I’m so fucked.

He groans and rakes a hand through his hair.

Andvari: Alright, alright, alright… Cassidy, send me the data. I’ll try to make it make sense… Wichita.

He turns toward Wichita.

Andvari: I want you to return to your team in New York City but keep 8944’s old apartment under constant surveillance. I honestly doubt that that’s where it’d go but as it stands, that’s our only lead. Aside from that, you may return to your regular duties. Thank you for joining us.

Wichita nods.

Wichita: Yes, Sir. But…

She pauses for a moment before continuing.

Wichita: …however slim the chances may be that my team will find the anomaly, should we encounter it… how can we engage it without simply making it that much more of a potent reality bender?

Andvari shakes his head and sighs.

Andvari: I… I don’t know. We’ll discuss how we’re going to move forward during our meeting today and then… I’ll get back to you regarding how you’re to proceed.

Wichita nods.

Wichita: Yes, Sir. Thank you for having me. Moon. Sloane.

Wichita nods to both of them and exits the room.

Andvari: Cassidy.

Moon: Yes, Simon? I just sent over the data by the way.

Andvari nods.

Andvari: …thank you. Get on the horn with the Industry Plants3.

Moon: How many of them?

Andvari: All of them. The GOC, MC&D, Wondertainment…

He pauses.

Andvari: …the Serpent’s Hand. All of them. You’re asking after the name “Wren Crowe.” I don’t care if they’ve met her, heard her name in passing, or saw it written inside of a goddamn bathroom stall, I want to know about it. If we find her, we can lure 8944 back to Site-78.

Moon nods.

Moon: I’m on it.

He starts walking toward the door but pauses, standing in place.

Andvari: …Cassidy? What is it?

Moon turns toward Sloane.

Moon: …no matter how many times I pour over the UIU’s files, our own interviews, our testing logs… something’s just never made any sense to me. Can I ask you something?

Sloane nods.

Sloane: Shoot.

Moon: I pulled your personnel file after your first psych eval with 8944… you never knew your father, he left when you were a child. How could he have given you that pen on his deathbed?

Sloane chokes out a surprised laugh. He retrieves a black click pen emblazoned with the Foundation’s insignia from the breast pocket of his coat, fidgeting with it in his hands.

Sloane: You really did your homework, huh? I bought that pen at a drugstore right after Simon assigned me to Robin and I got a peak at the kid’s files. You’re not the only one who studies. Truth is, I’ve actually always fancied myself a writer… I dual-majored in college, Psychology and Creative Writing. I guess… at the end of the day, I thought that I could spin a story to someone who’s far too acquainted with being caught in a web.

Moon appears to ponder this for a moment. He nods to Sloane, nods to Andvari, and exits the room. Sloane turns to Andvari and smiles.

Sloane: What about me, boss?

Andvari: You…

He sighs.

Andvari: You’ll be transferred to a different site, forced to sit through a disciplinary hearing for posterity’s sake, and fill out more forms than you’ve ever seen in your life. Then, the Ethics Committee will give you a new assignment, I reckon. Seeing to it that you take your notes digitally, this time around.

A moment passes wordlessly between the two.

Andvari: …I had no choice but to file a report with the Ethics Committee, Alexander. Although, I filed a report of negligence rather than one of insubordination, which would’ve been a real blight on your personnel record.

Sloane stands from the couch with a small grunt.

Sloane: …thank you, Simon. I know that I don’t deserve your help but… it is appreciated. As always.

He steps forward.

Sloane: I’m sorry that Robin escaped, Simon. I truly am. I didn’t mean for it to happen and… and it was a mistake. I’m aware that certain people see me and think that I don’t take my job seriously enough, that I’m not cut out for our brand of work.

He casts his gaze downward.

Sloane: …whether you believe me or not, my work is the most important thing in my life. Which is why I can’t apologize for how I treated Robin while he was in containment. I won’t. He’s a good kid, Simon. And he didn’t deserve the treatment that he received after he’d just escaped from one hellish prison, only to be thrown into another cage.

Andvari rolls his eyes and scoffs.

Andvari: It’s an anomaly, Alexander. I’d have thought that if anyone would understand the importance of keeping their patients at an arm’s length, it would be you.

Sloane: You didn’t come to me asking for help with Robin because you thought that I’d treat him like a monster, Simon. You came to me because you knew that I’d do the one thing that so many of us are becoming incapable of doing… treating them like what they are. People, Simon. People who are stuck in terrifying situations, people who don’t know what’s happening to them, people who we have the ability to truly help! I mean, Christ, the fear mongering is getting worse by the day! The Foundation’s end won’t come from any super weapon, or insurgency, or wrathful deity. No. Our apathy will be our destruction. Every anomaly that we contain, like a leech, drains us of more of our humanity as we turn a blind eye to suffering that we are responsible for.

Andvari shrugs.

Andvari: We can stand here waxing philosophical all day if you’d like, Alexander. But we have a job to do. And at the end of the day, we must prioritize our maintenance of the veil of normalcy over the wellbeing of those who stand in direct opposition to it.

Sloane: Listen to the recordings of my sessions with Robin and then you can preach to me about priorities.

Andvari does not respond.

Sloane: …I know that you’ve been having Cassidy listen to them and report back to you. Are you that afraid of allowing yourself to feel for them?

Andvari: Oh, please, do us both a favor and step down from that high horse. What gives you the right to lecture me about how I choose to go about my business?

Sloane shrugs.

Sloane: I don’t know. Because we’re friends, Simon. Because you’re my friend.

A moment passes. Sloane sighs.

Sloane: …what can I say? This place hasn’t taken my heart from me quite yet, I guess.

He freezes.

Sloane: …no pun intended.

He turns and starts toward the door, pausing just before he reaches it.

Sloane: …will you tell me if they find him?

Andvari knits his brow and crosses his arms.

Andvari: I can’t, Alexander. After this, I… I just can’t.

Sloane chuckles.

Sloane: Yeah… I figured as much.

He opens the door but does not step through.

Sloane: …what a horrible world to live in.

Andvari: Excuse me?

Sloane glances back toward him.

Sloane: When your only means of defense depends upon your willingness to mutilate yourself.

A moment passes. Andvari casts his gaze downward.

Andvari: …ah. Yes… I suppose it is.

Sloane: Goodbye, Simon. Let me know if you need me. I’m always a call away.

Sloane exits the room. Andvari sits in silence for an additional five minutes before exiting as well.

The Monocular Marauder!

by The Second Wandsman of Estofauk’s Crimson Emporium (currently residing within the city of Three Portlands)

Local law enforcement officials and agents of the United States government’s Unusual Incidents Unit are cautioning all Three Portlanders, parahuman and otherwise, who frequent Cambium Circle to be on the lookout for pickpockets. Investigators say they are seeing a rise in theft crimes in the Circle and while most instances are nonviolent, with a majority of victims unaware that they’ve been stolen from until after the fact, citizens are advised to tread with caution while in the public park, especially when traveling alone, at night, or both. The identity of a primary suspect for multiple reported thefts is still under investigation, however, numerous victims have reported seeing a young man (age 16 to 18) with a patch over his left eye and a surgical mask covering the lower half of his face prior to their theft.

Others have reported seeing a young man of a similar age with a discolored left eye, potentially glass or some other kind of prosthetic, and a wound to the right-hand corner of his mouth. Are these two young men completely unrelated? Partners in crime? One and the same?

Coincidence? Or cycloptic parahuman with a penchant for petty theft? Either way, make sure to keep an eye peeled in Cambium Circle, report any suspicious activity to your local law enforcement officials, and we’ll keep you updated as investigations continue.
