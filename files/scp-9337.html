SCP-9337

The edited image of SCP-9337 provided in published research papers.

Item #: SCP-9337

Object Class: Euclid

Special Containment Procedures: In order to limit abuse of or research into SCP-9337 by external groups, the existence of SCP-9337 has been published in research papers produced by a Foundation front company. The papers in question reference multiple related bacteria, and utilise data manipulated to obfuscate the anomalous properties exhibited by samples of SCP-9337.

In cases where the symptoms of an SCP-9337 infection reaches stage 4, the afflicted individual, henceforth designated SCP-9337-2, is to remain in Foundation custody under direct supervision until stage 5 is reached. During this period, the SCP-9337-2 individual is to undergo daily psychological and emotional evaluation by a Foundation therapist.

Upon an individual reaching stage 5, actions taken must have direct permission from a minimum of four distinct personnel holding Level 4 clearance. Depending on the mental state of the SCP-9337-2 individual after this point, they may be released into the public with an appropriate cover story, or, in cases of favourable symptoms, offered a place as a Foundation researcher.

Description: SCP-9337 is a species of bacterium of the genus Streptococcus, with the published species name Streptococcus aduncus. Individuals of SCP-9337 share traits common to all members of the Streptococcus genus, such as the ability to perform natural genetic transformation1, presence in the upper sections of the human respiratory system, and performing cell division along a single axis2. SCP-9337 can be differentiated from other Streptococcus bacteria at a cursory inspection by its primary anomalous characteristic, being that its body contains an organelle, in place of where DNA would be in other related bacteria, that is an exact, albeit smaller, duplicate of the full bacterial body. Due to this duplicate organelle containing another, smaller, organelle with the same properties3, the structure of SCP-9337's biology becomes recursive. To date, all attempts to find the end of this recursive pattern have remained inconclusive, often due to abandonment of the related project.

Like the majority of Streptococcus bacteria, SCP-9337 is present as a common inhabitant of the human salivary microbiome. Currently, Foundation testing has proved SCP-9337 to be present in approximately 37.22% of the human population, with significantly higher percentages found in areas with extreme conditions, such as deserts, polar regions, and mountainous areas4. Individuals carrying samples of SCP-9337 within their body are referred to as SCP-9337-1. In its natural state, SCP-9337 causes no symptoms or measurable effects on the human body. This is considered Stage 1 of the SCP-9337 infection. For the full set of stages and associated symptoms, see Addendum 9337-a.

Addendum 9337-a:

Stage Typical Activation Stimulus Symptoms
Stage 1 N/A No observable symptoms.
Stage 2 SCP-9337-1 individual experiencing considerable emotional strain, such as grief after a loved one's death. Itching in the upper throat.
Stage 3 SCP-9337-1 individual remains in a period of emotional strain sufficient to activate Stage 2 for a period of time exceeding 10 months. Anxiety, paranoia and scopaesthesia5. Often accompanied by worsened symptoms similar to those experienced in Stage 2.
Stage 4 Intent to leave their current life, either by moving away from home or by suicide. Can also be triggered by continuous study of SCP-9337, which results in significantly worsened symptoms. Mental clarity significantly exceeding that of the average human, and the inherent ability to understand oneself and the circumstances of one's life. Usually correlates with an improvement in memory.
Stage 5.1 SCP-9337-2 individual accepts their failings, and comes to peace with their life, after having remained in Stage 4 for between 11 and 2415 hours. Continued mental clarity and significant increase in memory. This effect will continue for the remainder of the individual's life.
Stage 5.2 SCP-9337-2 individual is unable to accept their failings, after having remained in Stage 4 for between 11 and 2415 hours. Similar, albeit worsened symptoms similar to Stage 4. These will fade after 48 hours.
Stage 5.3 SCP-9337-2 individual accepts their failings, but still has residual doubts or feelings, after having remained in Stage 4 for between 11 and 2415 hours. Similar, albeit lessened, symptoms to Stage 4. These will fade after 24 hours, resetting the individual's resident samples of SCP-9337 back to Stage 1. Additionally, a feeling of apparent failure is often reported.

Addendum 9337-b:

This is the full log of recorded psychological evaluations for the 120th recorded SCP-9337-2 individual, a man named Marian Wood.

+ OPEN LOG 09/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Senior Researcher ████████: Hello, Marian.
Marian Wood: Yes.
Senior Researcher ████████: You do understand what you're here for, right?
Marian Wood: What do you expect me to say? All I was told was "Stage 4, bacteria" and shoved in a prison cell.
Senior Researcher ████████: Ish.
Marian placed his head in his hands for a few minutes. He did not respond until prompted by Senior Researcher ████████.
Senior Researcher ████████: Listen, if there's anything you want to say, just say it. I'm here to talk, any time you need it.
Marian looked up briefly as Senior Researcher ████████ as she left the evaluation room.

△CLOSE

+ OPEN LOG 10/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Senior Researcher ████████: You said you wanted to talk?
Marian Wood: Yeah. I'm sorry about yesterday. I've… been thinking. Y'know, about things. Life. Got a bit… Angry.
Senior Researcher ████████: Yes, that is consistent with the symptoms of-
Marian Wood: But it doesn't feel like symptoms. It doesn't feel like anything, really, except my own thoughts. I wonder if your people have ever thought about it. Maybe what you call symptoms aren't really symptoms. Maybe it's more cause than effect.
Senior Researcher ████████: Perhaps.
Marian Wood: But, well… I don't know exactly what I wanted to say. I just, kinda, felt like I needed to apologise.
Pause.
Senior Researcher ████████: Thank you, Marian.
Pause. Marian smiles.
Marian Wood: No worries, doctor.

△CLOSE

+ OPEN LOG 23/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Senior Researcher ████████: Marian, what did you want to talk about?
Considerable pause.
Marian Wood: I'm not really sure. Still working things out. I don't really sleep much.
Senior Researcher ████████: Do you know why?
Marian Wood: Yeah. 'Cause I can't stop thinking, about everything. My life. Everything I did wrong.
Pause. Marian drums his fingers on the table quietly.
Marian Wood: Do you mind if I tell you a story?
Senior Researcher ████████: Go ahead.
Marian Wood: Okay. So, back when I was in school, I think I was around 15 when it happened. I was studying for a really big test, the kind of test that seems like it matters a lot in the moment, but then doesn't. I always regarded that test as a waste. So, I didn't revise as much as I should have. I stayed up late, talking to my friends. The next day, I failed it.
Senior Researcher ████████: That must have been-
Marian Wood: Not finished. Anyway, I remember walking out of that exam hall, knowing I should have done better. Not that I could have, but that I should have. Like an obligation to myself, that I failed. I said to myself, walking home, that I was going to do better next time.
Senior Researcher ████████: Did you?
Marian Wood: No. And looking back on it, I was never going to keep that promise.
Pause.
Marian Wood: That's all. I know it's not much, but thank you for listening to me.
Senior Researcher ████████: No worries. It's my job.

△CLOSE

+ OPEN LOG 26/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Marian Wood: Yeah, thanks again for talking to me at this time. I would've just said no in your shoes.
Senior Researcher ████████: Honestly, I almost did. 4 AM, man. Jesus.
Marian Wood: I'd apologise, but I really want to talk to you about something.
Senior Researcher ████████: I'd hope so. What is it?
Marian Wood: Have you ever gone to prison?
Senior Researcher ████████: No.
Marian Wood: I have. I stole something. Don't even remember what it was, but it was valuable, and my sister said she wanted it. Didn't know what a security camera was.
Senior Researcher ████████: I have a sister. She's…
Marian Wood: Dead?
Senior Researcher ████████: Yeah.
Marian Wood: I suppose it comes with the line of work. Sorry about that.
Senior Researcher ████████: It's alright. Keep telling your story.
Marian Wood: Okay. I'd been in prison for about a week at this point. Quit college, even though I was around the top of class. Some guy came up to me, said he recognised me, from this old newspaper about raising money for charity.
Senior Researcher ████████: How'd you do it?
Marian Wood: A hike, I think. The guy started asking questions, about what I did to be there, why someone with "such a promising future" was in such a place. Hearing that, it hurt. Made me think about everything that went wrong. I think there's a name for it.
Pause.
Marian Wood: Gifted kid syndrome.
Senior Researcher ████████: Why do you think they call it that?
Marian Wood: What do you mean?
Senior Researcher ████████: Gifted kid syndrome. Like you chose to be "gifted". Like it was something given to you, and you didn't have to think or work or do anything to get it. They just spring that title on people, and then those people think they don't have to work as hard, because they're gifted.
Marian Wood: You seem stressed.
Senior Researcher ████████: Somewhat. I've seen so many people fall to that trick.
Marian Wood: I guess I was one of them.
Considerable pause. Senior Researcher ████████ stands, and leaves.

△CLOSE

+ OPEN LOG 28/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Prior to this evalutation, Senior Researcher ████████ had been registered as having reached Stage 4 of the SCP-9337 infection.

Senior Researcher ████████: What do you think is the meaning of life?
Marian Wood: Damn. You've been thinking about that?
Senior Researcher ████████: A lot, actually. It's strange to think that a bacterium can change the entire way you think, and the way you look at yourself.
Marian Wood: Yeah, I know what you mean.
Senior Researcher ████████: So what do you think?
Marian Wood: That's hard to answer. I'll think about it.
Pause.
Senior Researcher ████████: Tomorrow?
Marian Wood: Tomorrow.

△CLOSE

+ OPEN LOG 29/11/2021

▽ CLOSE

Senior Researcher ████████: So…
Marian Wood: Yeah, I know. Didn't sleep last night.
Senior Researcher ████████: Sorry, I didn't think you'd be-
Marian Wood: No worries. So, I guess I'll start. The meaning of life, I suppose, is whatever meaning you assign to it. If you choose that the meaning of your life is one thing, that thing it will be. But only for you. If you choose your life's meaning to be finding the meaning in your life, you will only end up in a circle. People that are happy are people that set out to do something, and do it. Happiness and meanings are entirely from within oneself. There isn't anything else.
Pause. Senior Researcher ████████ begins to cry.
Marian Wood: ████████? ████████, are you okay?
Senior Researcher ████████ stops crying and looks up. She smiles.
Senior Researcher ████████: You said exactly what I was going to say.

Subsequent to this evaluation, Marian Wood advanced to Stage 5. He was permitted to join Site-210 staff in a Junior Researcher position.

△CLOSE

Addendum 9337-c:

I think people should learn to look at themselves. Consider their failings. Perhaps SCP-9337 being so widespread implies hope for our species. That we, eventually, can all learn to accept ourselves. I've failed so many times. I don't even remember most of them. Until last November, I'd never really looked at myself before.
I'm grateful, I really am. But, I think to myself, must we truly continue to contain SCP-9337? At worst, it is harmless. At best, it teaches you how to live.
Why do we hide that from the world?

- Junior Researcher Marian Wood
