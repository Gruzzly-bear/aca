Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8708 is currently held at Site-510 at Scapa Flow within the Orkney Islands. SCP-8708 is to remain moored in its containment pen. SCP-8708-1 is inert and requires no additional containment procedures. Site personnel must conduct daily leak inspections of the seal plugs fitted to the exterior ends of SCP-8708's rear torpedo tubes, as well as the interior tube hatches.

Description: SCP-8708 is a type IXB U-boat utilized by the German Kriegsmarine during the Second World War. SCP-8708 possesses all standard equipment for a U-boat operating in mid-1941. The object exhibits signs of weathering, with the forward hull in particular showing buckling and warping indicative of repeated violent frontal impacts. Internally, the object exhibits signs of habitation typical of a U-boat deployed for an extended patrol. The rear torpedo compartment of SCP-8708 has undergone advanced corrosion due to prolonged seawater exposure. Due to severe warpage, the interior hatches of the rear torpedo tubes cannot seal properly. The outer torpedo hatches are missing and have been replaced with seal plugs.

SCP-8708-1 is a large device of Obskuracorps origins. It occupies one-fourth of the internal space of the object's conning tower, roughly the size of SCP-8708's torpedo data computer. SCP-8708-1 is heavily damaged, having suffered an apparent power surge and electrical fire. The object possesses three rows of rune-like symbols on its exterior casing. These symbols most closely resemble Elder Futhark1, but are not derived from it. Foundation linguists have been unable to translate the runic symbols. The first two rows of symbols are engraved on the device. The third row is disproportionately scaled to the other two rows and written in blood.

Within the device's internal cavity, Foundation staff discovered a single humerus bone2 suspended in the center of the cavity, covered with similar engravings to those found on the outer casing of SCP-8708-1. The bone is suspended by electrical cabling connected to SCP-8708's battery cells. All attempts at reactivating SCP-8708-1 have failed.

According to several recovered personnel accounts, SCP-8708 was designated as U-112 prior to containment. No records of an operational U-112 or its crew exist within German naval archives.

RECOVERY REPORT: SCP-8708

DATE(S): 2/14/1946

NOTE: The following is the official recovery report for SCP-8708. Information detailed in the report includes both background information (i.e, events leading up to recovery) as well as recovery information.

[REPORT START]

08:00, 2/14/1946: An RAF Coastal Command Sunderland aircraft conducting a patrol of the Western Approaches reports the first sighting of SCP-8708.

9:20, 2/14/1946: A ship belonging to His Majesty's Coast Guard arrives at the given coordinates of SCP-8708. It is quickly confirmed that the object is a German submarine, which prompts the Coast Guard to contact the Royal Navy.

10:00, 2/14/1946: A Royal Navy destroyer arrives at SCP-8708's location with a detachment of Royal Marines. When the Royal Marines board the object, it is quickly determined that it is abandoned. Marines note the presence of pack ice deposits on the object's deck and conning tower as they board.

10:30, 2/14/1946: A tug boat arrives on the scene. Tow lines are attached to SCP-8708, and the object is towed to Scapa Flow naval base. During the transit, the Foundation is contacted concerning SCP-8708-1 and its likely anomalous nature.

18:45, 2/14/1946: SCP-8708 arrives at Scapa Flow naval base. Foundation field agents are dispatched to receive SCP-8708 and conduct an initial inspection of SCP-8708-1. Divers conduct a full hull inspection of the object, discovering the presence of Fucus gardneri on the object's prop blades3.

18:49, 2/14/1946: With field agents confirming the anomalous properties of SCP-8708-1; SCP-8708 is officially transferred to Foundation custody.

[END REPORT]

Foundation staff discovered the personal logs of Captain Karl Fischer, Executive Officer Phillip Lenz, Senior Radio Petty Officer and boat's Doctor Christof Fenstermacher, and Obskuracorps researcher Arnold Hahn during their initial sweep of the object. These logs have been arranged in chronological order, along with recovered Obskuracorps communications, to construct a timeline concerning the events that transpired aboard SCP-8708 prior to containment.

Entries for 5/7/1941

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date: 5/7/1941
Situation: U-112 pulled into port today, bringing my third war patrol to an end. Battery trouble forced our early return home; there's always something with these new boats. Four boats were sunk this past month, four. We haven't had losses like that since March, and some people still call this the "happy times".

War Diary - Phillip Lenz
Time: War Patrol 3, Day 17

This has been a disappointing deployment. We were barely out three weeks before our damned batteries forced us to limp back to France, with nothing but one kill to show for it.

Karl and I spent the whole trip back in the engine compartment with Klaus, troubleshooting the battery issue. I had to force Karl back to his rack at one point; he was hardly eating or sleeping, and I thought he was going to pass out a few times. Of course, he won't admit that was a problem; that's his way, I suppose. I can't help but think back to the night before our second patrol. We had been out for a long night of drinking when his facade fell. For the first time, he opened up to me.

He spent most of the twenties floating around the country, working odd jobs to make ends meet. He practically jumped at the opportunity to re-enlist back in '35. The service is all he has left at this point, so he says, but I know it's killing him inside. Having to go through all this again, it's too much for him, but he buries it under his need for a purpose.

He took me under his wing, helped me get acclimated to this life, we have to look out for each other. I don't care what he says; I'm making sure he rests while we're back.

From: Dr. Arnold Hahn
To: Ostuf. Karl Hoffmann
Subject: Field Testing

Obersturmführer Hoffmann,

I am once again requesting permission to conduct field testing for Project Hermod. We've confirmed the existence of the "Otherworld" corridors through lab testing with the recovered runic scripts; it is no longer a hypothetical. The new specimen is far more stable than the original; searching for additional samples is pointless at this stage. Raeder will never accept the Hermod device without a proper field test, and if he doesn't accept it, then our sacrifices will have been for nothing.

I understand conducting a field test using my original test vessel is not feasible given recent events. That is why I am willing to downgrade my request from a cruiser to a U-boat. I eagerly await your response.

HH

I hope we get back out to sea soon, though. Tommy ships are getting sunk left and right, and I don't want to miss out on it.

This is a delicate situation, Arnold. After the Bismarck fiasco, relations with the Navy are on thin ice. For your sake, I hope you're successful. You know the price of failure.

Karl got called up by command again; he was not happy when he got back. He saw that Kurt fellow again, and met a new guy who told him we're going to be testing some kind of "wonder weapon", as he put it. I'm annoyed too; we're going to be sorely missed on the convoy lanes.

I can't entirely blame him, though. We obviously did not tell him the true nature of this upcoming test, only that it would change the course of the war. Still, though, he should be honored to even get the opportunity to work with us.

Entries for 10/7/1941

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date: 10/7/1941
Situation: They were right about the quick turnaround, the work is done, and U-112 is getting ready to depart for a fourth patrol. The crew and I were given access to the boat this morning, provisions are being loaded, and we are on track to depart by this evening. Phillip is itching to get back out to sea and is leading the crew in making final preparations for our departure. He's drunk on pride and seeking glory. Reminds me a lot of my younger self.

It's been welded onto the wall, and a tangle of wires connects it to the batteries. The crew seems unsettled by it; I can't say that I blame them, though it hasn't impeded their work.

Arnold arrived around noon to conduct a final check on the device and to give me my orders for this patrol. They're triple sealed, and I'm not to open them until I've left port. The game begins.

The new gadget in the conning tower is certainly something. I've never seen a machine look so plain yet so alien at the same time. Most of the guys are anxious about it, so I'm trying to take their minds off it with a contest. The guys are split into teams; whichever team gets their allotment of provisions loaded first gets some Swiss chocolate I picked up the other day. It seems to be working so far. At this rate, we'll be out of port by this afternoon.

Test Log - Arnold Hahn

The test vessel is in good order, and its crew is making final preparations for our departure. I gave Captain Fischer his orders for the duration of this test; he's still as dull as he was during our last meeting. I'll be going over final prep work to ensure a smooth testing of the Hermod device. This project will finally gain the recognition it deserves.

Entries for 12/7/1941

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date: 12/7/1941
Situation: Where do I even begin? The machine hummed when Arnold activated it, and things seemed to be going well before a sharp whine came from the conning tower, and the boat was plunged into darkness. U-112 made noises I didn't think were possible for a while before it all stopped, and the lights came back on.

We lost a few battery cells; the last count put us down four, but other than that, the boat seems fine. Arnold told me that his machine had experienced a power surge and was inoperable. Climbing up the conning tower, I could see the damage for myself. The machine was scorched, the glass on its gauges shattered. He's lucky it didn't destroy the whole tower.

On the bridge, everything seemed fine until I tried taking our position. I must have stood with my navigator, Albert, for two or three hours poring over every star chart on board. I've sailed in the Atlantic, Pacific, and everywhere in between. Never have I seen a night sky look like that before. As I headed back below, all I could think about was the smoldering runes on Arnold's machine. What have I gotten into here?

Diary - Christof Fenstermacher
I had convinced myself that we would be tracking convoys, testing radar, or collecting weather data. Why else would the brass take a cruiser boat out of the struggle with the British? When we started sealing up the boat, I didn’t question it, even though I thought it was odd. It was only after the noises started that I knew something strange was going on.

That thing —the machine —squealed. I've heard the sound more times than I can count back on my family’s farm; it sounded like a dying pig. Some of the guys were talking about it when the lights cut out. We all stood in silence, listening to the horrible noises coming from the conning tower. When the lights flickered back, I saw a couple of the guys with their hands clasped in prayer.

We’re checking the boat for damage now. Thankfully, nothing serious has come up. Albert and the captain went up on the bridge an hour ago. There's no word on what they’re doing up there. Oskar is telling us everything went fine, and that we have nothing to worry about. But that uneasy feeling is still sitting in my gut, and I have to wonder if he even believes the words coming out of his mouth.

Entries for 13/7/1941

He wants one of the crew. Apparently, human sacrifices are more potent than inanimate offerings. I know just who he can take.

Entries for 1/8/1941?

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date: 1/8/1941?
Situation: I woke up this morning with the worst headache I've ever had; it felt like my forehead was going to explode. The dank air of the boat did not help, so I decided to go up on the bridge for some fresh air.

I got up just in time to see Phillip's optical nerves snap as he tore his eyes out of his head. The bridge crew and I stood motionless for a moment, completely dumbfounded. I shouted down the ladder for Christof, and that got the men moving. Phillip, good God, he was still tearing. Blood and mashed eyeball smeared on his face as he clawed out ribbons of flesh from his eye sockets. It took all of us to hold him down, and that's when the screaming started. He saw something this morning. We don't know what it was; he had tried to point it out, but no one else saw it.

It was all so surreal. I've had friends die before; hell, all my old friends got ground into mulch on the fields of France. But those deaths were distant; I always heard about them after the fact. I was never with them as they died, never tried to comfort them in their last moments. Christof gave him morphine for the pain, and then he was gone.

We buried him a few hours later. Got most of the crew on the deck for it and gave him all the honors we could. He deserved that much. I nearly lost it up there, but thankfully, no one seemed to notice. I can't falter now; I have to stay strong for the men.

Diary - Christof Fenstermacher
I was finally getting around to that boat-wide physical when the captain called me. I didn't waste time getting to the bridge because, well, I'd never heard him scared before.

When it was all over, no one wanted to carry him below, so we decided to give him to the sea. I helped the Captain wrap Phillip in a bed sheet before corraling the crew on deck to see him off. The Captain was struggling during the burial, fighting hard to hold back tears. We pretended not to notice.

Entries for First Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: I haven't had much time to sit and write. I don't even know what day it should be. The mood on the boat has been bleak since Phillip's death; all the life has been sucked out of us. I've decided to lift Arnold's restrictions, despite Klaus's protests. If even just a fraction of what he told me is true, we have to get out of here as soon as possible, and he's unfortunately our best hope for that.

The "shadow" sightings have increased as well, and they're getting closer to the boat. I've ordered the guns manned at all times, though this has done little to alleviate my lookout's anxiety. I still haven't seen one of these damned things, but it's impossible to deny their existence anymore.

Test Log - Arnold Hahn
Project Status: Desolation Behind Me
Planned Work for the Next 24 Hours:

  • Commune with Alex

Notes:
We're close, so very close. With Phillip out of the way, I regained my freedom to move about the boat. The captain is still proving to be somewhat stubborn towards me, despite lifting his restrictions. Alex says he will speak with him and try to convince him to aid our cause. I'm not sure how he'll do it, but he says he knows how to make Karl follow us.

In other news, I've concluded that I can not get the machine working with the parts at my disposal here. It matters not, Alex assures me that he has one last plan.

Despite the sacrifice not giving him the strength to open a portal, it did grant him a better insight into the corridors. He says that the Hermod device is simply incomplete, and that all I need to do is add another set of runes to the outer casing. He will reveal them to me in the coming days, and I will finally get us out of here. These men who tried to kill me before will be groveling at my feet soon enough!

Entries for Second Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: I saw Phillip today. I was up on the bridge with the watch crew when it happened. A large shadow came over the boat, and when I turned to look for its source, I noticed that the lookouts were gone, and Phillip was standing behind me. His body was bloated and pale, covered in a thin film of sea slime. I should have been horrified, but I couldn't muster the feeling.

Not knowing what else to do, I asked what was happening, and to my surprise, Phillip answered. He said that Arnold had been right about this place, and that he had come back from the dead to "help me". He raised a stiff arm towards me, but his hand hung limp; he couldn't seem to move it beyond twitching his fingers. I guess he was trying to offer me a handshake, but I refused. Despite my lack of fear, I was not about to shake the hand of a corpse.

Even without his eyes, I could tell he was angry at me. Before I could say another word, the shadow passed, he was gone, and I found myself surrounded by the watch crew again. They said I had been hallucinating, thinking I was going to go the way of Phillip, and kindly but firmly suggested I get below deck and out of the sun.

I know what I saw, and it was not a hallucination. It's bad luck to have a corpse on board, even worse if the corpse comes back.

Entries for Third Unknown Date

Test Log - Arnold Hahn
Project Status: Salvation Before Me
Planned Work for the Next 24 Hours:

  • Escape

Notes:
The captain couldn't be convinced. A pity, but it matters not. Alex has given me the final runes; the power he has gained from my offerings allowed him to materialize outside of my dreams, and he guided me as I wrote them. Since I lack any good engraving tools, I've used my blood to mark the casing of the Hermod device. Unfortunately, as I was adding the runes to the machine, one of the crewmen came up the conning tower ladder.

He saw Alex and yelled. I had to act quickly, so I kicked him in the face and sent him tumbling down below. Alex and I sealed up the inner conning tower and got back to work. The crew has been pounding and shouting from above and below, but it's a futile endeavor on their part. Alex has left now; he will ensure we make it through the gateway. He has my everlasting gratitude.

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: That bastard was working against us this whole time.

Christof was headed up to the bridge for the night watch when he saw it. He says Arnold was talking to a shadow and making strange markings on his machine with his own blood. When he called it out, Arnold kicked Christof down the ladder and sealed himself inside the tower.

We tried to stop him, but we weren't fast enough. When the humming started, I knew he had started up his machine again, and then he started screaming. For a few agonizing minutes, we listened to his pained cries, then he went silent. It took us an extra half-hour to get the inner hatch open. The compartment was filled with smoke, but Arnold was still alive. His mouth was moving, but no words came out, and his whole upper body was burned red and black.

When we got the outer hatch open, we found something worse. The bridge crew was gone. They weren't anywhere on deck or in the water. We don't know what happened to them.

Arnold is tied up in the control room. If the men hated him before, they must be seeing red every time they pass him now. Klaus was the first to speak up; he demanded that I kill Arnold as soon as it was clear he was alive, but I refused. He doesn't deserve such an easy out. Oskar and I are taking turns watching him; he's the only one I trust to hold my pistol when I'm not in the control room.

Entries for Fourth Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: It's been a day since the incident with Arnold, and the strangest thing is happening.

There's wind. For the first time since this whole ordeal started, I feel a breeze on my face standing on the bridge. Waves are lapping at the sides of the boat, and the men crowd around the conning tower ladder to hear it whistle. I've also started seeing clouds gather on the horizon. What's better, none of the lookouts reported seeing shadows during the night. They needed that; they've been taking the loss of the original nightwatch very poorly.

I thought maybe Arnold's insanity had actually paid off.

That was until I had to help fish out a watchman who had jumped overboard this morning. He was a boy, couldn't have been older than nineteen or twenty, he said he saw his mother in the water and "had to go to her." I've taken him off the watch rotation. It seems our ordeal is not over yet.

Entries for Fifth Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: The light wind turned to a strong breeze, and then a gust, and then a maelstrom. We were hit by a storm in the night and forced to dive deep. We're sitting at 80 meters and can still feel the undercurrent of waves in the boat. The air is damp, sweat glistens off our bony bodies. We'll be able to stay submerged for a couple of days before we are forced to surface, assuming the electric motors don't give out. If the storm is not gone by then, God help us, we'll have to face it.

Arnold is gone, or at least his mind is. He speaks with labored, rattling, wet words. All he ever talks about is an old friend, a lab assistant, coming to save him. Coming to save us all. When the storm hit, he wouldn't stop laughing, hacking up half a lung in the process. There is something evil inside that man, and it is becoming harder to justify keeping him alive.

Personal Account - Christof Fenstermacher
This will likely be my last entry. Things have gotten a lot worse on the boat; we're sitting deep now. The creaking of the hull and constant swaying are making it hard to concentrate.

I was in the engine compartment with Klaus when a loud groan and shudder rocked the boat. I thought it was just pressure, but a commotion from the rear torpedo compartment proved me wrong. Klaus and I headed back there to find all the guys crowded around the rear torpedo tubes.

Despite the exterior hatch controls still being in the closed position, the indicators showed both tubes as full. The torpedomen tried to cycle the system and expel the water, but the indicators didn't budge. One man suggested we open the tubes to see if the indicators were just faulty, but Klaus shut that down quickly. If they are full of water, he doesn't want to risk flooding the boat. He said we'd check them when we surface.

As we turned to leave, I swear I heard a tapping sound from one of the tube hatches. There are torpedoes in those tubes, but they wouldn't make a noise like that. It was rhythmic.

Tap, tap, tap. Some of the torpedomen heard it too, but there is nothing we can do about it. As Klaus and I made our way back to the engine compartment, I saw that bastard Arnold staring at us through the access hatch leading to the control room. He was grinning ear to ear.

Entries for Sixth Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: I was sitting in my bunk trying to get some sleep when Klaus roused me. Something was wrong in the rear torpedo compartment. As we made our way towards the back of the boat, I became aware of a loud banging sound. Klaus said it was coming from inside the torpedo tubes. We made it halfway through the engine compartment when there was a burst. The hatches on the rear torpedo tubes flew open, and water poured into the rear torpedo compartment.

Klaus and I broke into a sprint; the torpedo compartment was filling fast, and we had to seal it off. One man made it through the hatch before we closed it. Before I had time to process what had just happened, the machinists were crying out. There was a shadow on the torpedoman's leg, not a normal one, one of the ones the men had been seeing up on the bridge. For the first time I saw it, it looked like a bad bruise, pulsating and writhing on him. When the torpedoman saw it, he tried to claw it off. Poor bastard was already dead.

The thing moved up his leg, over his shirt, and onto his face. It entered through his eyes, ears, nose, and mouth. He went limp and dropped to the ground after that. Before we could process what had just happened, a loud bang from the control room drew our attention. That's when he jerked up, moving stiffly. He was dead; his wide-pupiled death stare gave it away. He lunged at Klaus and took him down; the shadow jumped onto Klaus' face, taking him in an instant.

I ran for the hatch leading into the control room. The shadow, now in Klaus' body, came lunging after me. I was the only one to make it through; the other machinists weren't fast enough. It took them quickly, and the engine compartment went quiet. There was some banging on the hatch, but it didn't budge; it seems these things can't open doors.

When I pulled myself away from the access hatch, I saw three lookouts from the morning watch, along with Oskar, standing around Arnold. He was dead. The lookouts had gagged him with a grease rag and beaten him bloody before Oskar shot him with my pistol. They think Arnold's death will deliver us from this nightmare. That these creatures will leave us alone now that he is dead.

Entries for Seventh Unknown Date

Personal Log - Karl Fischer
Date:
Situation: There comes a point in this life when you come to terms with your own death. You know there's a good chance it'll happen, get blown up by a bomb, or crushed by the sea. I made my peace with it long ago, but it wasn't supposed to be like this.

Oxygen is low. We've broken out the rebreathers, but they're not doing much. The men are either in bunks or lying on the floor to conserve energy. The banging from the engine compartment stopped a while ago, but those things aren't gone. I can see several wispy shadows wriggling around the edges of the hatch; they're forcing their way through the seal. Killing that bastard didn't do a damn thing.

Phillip, my friend, I'll see you soon.
