SCP-8827-2 Instance

Special Containment Procedures:

Due to the current limitations regarding knowledge of SCP-8827, containment measures focus on mitigating damage and upholding the Veil via integration of anomalous effects into consensus reality.

Site-88 is to coordinate a mass media project regarding SCP-8827. The Foundation front company AccuWeather should improve the gathering and dissemination of inclement weather data from the Gulf of Mexico. Reporting on increases in SCP-8827-2 instances must be attributed to anthropocentric climate change. News stories regarding SCP-8827-1 instances should be suppressed to prevent public attention.

Gulf Coat residents that are unaffected by SCP-8827 are to be subject to ad campaigns and incentive programs encouraging their departure from the region. Other programs should also work to erode local cultural connections, inflate insurance prices, and artificially increase the cost of living along the U.S. Gulf Coast.

Any civilians closely associated with Foundation personnel are to be moved out of the area.

Mobile Task Force Beta-2 (“Bayou Boys”) will be tasked with assisting in the relocation of SCP-8827-1 instances and at risk civilians. All appropriate means are authorized at the discretion of the Site–88 director.

MTF Gamma-5 (“Red Herrings”) must be dispatched immediately to neutralize dissenting elements of MTF Beta-2 and assume their role in the containment of SCP-8827.

Description:

SCP-8827 refers to a series of human habitation and weather patterns in the United States Central Gulf Coast region. Affected individuals – classified as SCP-8827-1 instances – display an irrational draw toward areas impacted by hazardous weather conditions (SCP-8827-2). This results in both a refusal to leave in the face of impending storms and a tendency to move back into danger-prone areas, even after widespread devastation. Instances have been observed to turn violent at the suggestion of relocation. How individuals become SCP-8827-1 instances is a topic that’s under investigation, however, amnestic treatment (especially class D amnestics) have proven effective in removing SCP-8827's effects.

Research has revealed a direct relation between casualties suffered due to SCP-8827-2 events and both the frequency and severity of further SCP-8827-2 instances. In addition, SCP-8827-2s show a tendency to target areas of higher SCP-8827-1 concentration. Critically, the storms are threatening multiple containment sites along the coast. More decisive action is pending O5 approval for additional resources.

Special Containment Procedures: Containment centers around maintaining expected anomalous exposure levels for Foundation staff. To that end, the Foundation must keep a weighted population count of all administrative regions. Weights are as follows:

D-Class: 0.0
Security Officers: 0.5
Mobile Task Force Operative: 0.7
Containment Specialists: 1.0
Researchers (Below Level 4): 2.0
Field Agents: 3.0
Researchers (Level 4+): 5.0

An anomalous activity4 quota must be maintained such that it equals the calculated population of a site. Activity translates to population as follows:

Level 1 = 5
Level 2 = 10
Level 3 = 15
Level 4 = 25
Level 5 = 40
Level 6 = 50
Level 7 = 60

The Department of Human Resources shall be tasked with keeping the quota either by releasing staff or relocating anomalies. Issues involving anomalies level 5 or above require the input of at least three personnel with clearance level 4 or above.

The management of the anomalous activity in regards to designated people of interest5 is at the discretion of the Ethics Committee Chairman.

All Site Directors must read the 7th edition of A Comprehensive Guide to Human Resources and Incident Management.

Description: SCP-8827 is an addiction contracted due to exposure to anomalous activity. The strength of the addiction scales with the length and magnitude of exposure. When an addicted individual is subject to expected levels of anomalous activity, symptoms manifest themselves in increased productivity; however, when levels are lower than expected, individuals become paranoid and will attempt to seek out anomalous activity, often resulting in false positives. Physical symptoms can manifest in extreme cases, resulting in ticks, shaking, trouble focusing, mood swings, and headaches.

Addendum 8827-1, Notable Incidents

+Event-8827-6 (Maryland Cult Incident)
- Close

Date: January 1994

Prelude: After a vicious streak of containment breaches caused by SCP-[Data Omitted], it and 3 other particularly hazardous anomalies were moved out of Site-29. Most staff working on the anomalies were sent to the new containment site, and those left were assigned to low-activity entities on site, exacerbating SCP-8827's manifestation in them. While the site’s employee condition report changed positively by an average of ~10% following the relocation,6 this only lasted three months, after which a staggering decline of ~40% occurred within a month. Field agents (of whom Site-29's sector had a disproportionate amount, given its low anomalous activity) saw worse trends, reporting a ~55% decrease.

Target: A small, nascent cult named the Ascendant Church of Our Lord’s Broken Flesh, formed from a breakaway Catholic parish in Churchville,7 Maryland. This sect was especially fond of the Eucharistic sacrament and practiced a small amount of ritual cannibalism, mostly limited to bloodletting. Occasionally, practitioners consumed small amounts of skin offered voluntarily as part of mass.

Action: Two field agents, Dupont and Nicholson, investigated the cult due to a flood of sensationalist local news coverage regarding the group. The agents claim that they infiltrated a mass and witnessed “the classic signs of Sarkic savagery, y’know, like, um, blood, eating people, ominous chanting, creepy shit, the whole 9”, after which they filed a report classifying the cult as a Sarkic indoctrination front.

Site director Brown organized an expedited trial as a pretext to dispatch site security under the guise of law enforcement. The situation garnered national news coverage when a shootout ensued between Foundation forces and cult members. As the situation developed, an inquiry by the Office of Records Administration was launched, though by the time it was determined that the cult was not Sarkic, the church had already been stormed. The Foundation captured 60 civilians while suffering one dead and one wounded. 23 attendees were killed, with a further 9 wounded. Only 15 of the casualties were belligerents.

Result: Since this event occurred before the 4th edition of A Comprehensive Guide to Human Resources and Incident Management was published, all key staff involved were terminated8 immediately. As such, no information regarding the nature of SCP-8827 was garnered. Public management operations were not required as the media attributed the event to the federal government. The United States' Unusual Incidents Unit reached out to the Foundation and questioned them as to their involvement. There are no records of a reply.


+Event 8827-2 (Red Scare Incident)
- Close

[DATA EXPUNGED]9 and Senator Joseph McCarthy shaking hands.

Date: 1949 - 1951

Prelude: Following the twin Seventh Occult War and Second World War, the Foundation saw a marked decrease in anomalous activity from baseline levels worldwide. This, paired with the high expected anomalous levels due to the war, caused pervasive paranoia throughout the Foundation. With the onset of the McCarthy era, Foundation personnel consumed an immense amount of anti-communist propaganda. Internal Foundation media followed suit and began publishing stories of “Soviet SCPs" ranging from fearmongering news coverage to pulp fiction. These were highly popular across all levels of the American Foundation and served to direct SCP-8827-derived paranoia toward the USSR.

Target: All personnel deemed to be Communist.

Action: A series of purges terminating10 individuals suspected of having Soviet sympathies. Casualty figures are unknown as many records were lost or destroyed.

Result: A temporary schism occurred between Soviet and American elements of the Foundation. Many staff members fled the U.S. for the Soviet Union and vice versa due to Soviet retaliation. Once anomalous activity returned to normal levels, the incident was amended with both parties expressing regret over the occurrence. Media restrictions were placed on Foundation personnel to avoid future compromising influences.


+Event 8827-12 (Gulf Coast Incident)
- Close

Date: March 2001

Prelude: Site-88 underwent a period of reduced activity since duties in the sector were largely delegated to the Global Occult Coalition. As a result, the majority of researchers were rendered idle. A sharp increase in mental unwellness was noted. Site Director McCullough attempted to address the issue by building an in-site park, adding amenities to the library, encouraging clubs, upgrading the cafeteria, prescribing medication, and holding optional therapy sessions, which became very popular. These tactics were shown to improve employee condition by ~40% and postpone the onset of serious negative SCP-8827 symptoms for over six months.

SCP-8827 began to manifest in force when an expedition to recover three missing field agents around Biloxi, Mississippi, ended in a fire exchange with locals due to a miscommunication.11Following this incident, McCullough opted to have MTF Beta-2 assist in the search due to their expertise in the area.

As this was occurring, Dr. Flores was independently conducting research into the cause of worsening weather around the Gulf Coast.12 Upon hearing of the encounter, Dr. Flores connected the two events, citing the perceived irrationality of civilians and his interpretation of historical data. After 'confirming' his ideas with colleagues, Flores assembled a preliminary case file for the former SCP-8827.

Target: Civilians on the Gulf Coast, especially the Louisiana-Alabama coastal region.

Action: Containment procedures promptly went into effect, and a mass relocation campaign ensued. In the best cases, communities were crushed by rising costs of living and torn between those who wanted to move and those who wanted to stay. In the most brutal instances, civilians were forcibly uprooted from their homes, treated with Class D amnestics to enforce compliance, and, in case of resistance, killed. Thousands more were subjected to campaigns of manufactured consent either through carefully crafted news cycles or − more directly − through cognito-hazards.

Several skirmishes between MTF teams and locals occurred throughout the operation. After a week, multiple Beta-2 elements refused to continue containment and began aiding civilians against Foundation forces. Gamma-5 was dispatched to deal with the situation. This culminated in the Battle of Butte La Rose, which lasted for one hour and saw five agents killed. The battle ended with the capture and subsequent defection of the remaining nine members of Gamma-5-C. After the battle, O5 command held an emergency meeting and approved further resources for the containment of the former SCP-8827. The Ethics Committee interceded and halted all operations.

Result: All staff involved in containment were detained by the Ethics Committee. With the information acquired after the incident through interviews and – thanks to Dr. McCullough's encouragement of the practice – employee journals, SCP-8827 was hypothesized and confirmed. All relevant site staff and MTF personnel were retrained.

Dr. Flores was terminated and returned to civilian life after amnestic treatment. Dr. McCullough and the O5 were reprimanded; however, due to her effectiveness in dealing with SCP-8827 symptoms prior to the incident, Dr. McCullough was enlisted in the writing of the 7th edition of A Comprehensive Guide to Human Resources and Incident Management. Since the operations launched by Site-88 followed effective public management practices, no coverup was required. All blame was attributed to the U.S. government.13

NOTE: The following is a transcript of Chairman Tejani's interview with Dr. McCullough while she was detained following the Gulf Coast incident.

[BEGIN LOG]

(McCullough enters. Tenjani is seated)

Tenjani: Drink?

McCullough: Um, pardon?

Tenjani: Coffee, tea, et cetera.

McCullough: (pauses) I'd appreciate some tea.

Tenjani: (while pouring) Please, take a seat. I don't bite.

McCullough: (sits) Noted, though, forgive me if I'm hesitant to think so.

Tenjani: Oh, absolutely, I know one of my – how should I say − more famous colleagues got to you first, no?

(McCullough nods)

Tenjani: Very sorry about that. Anyways, I know you must be aching to get out of here, so I'll get right to the point (passes tea). We're all doctors here, and after looking through your file, I can see you have quite the academic record; so, how did this 8827 entry get past you?

McCullough: I wish I could give you a clear answer. I mean, it really did make sense when I first read it. These people were acting irrationally; they kept putting themselves in harm's way, and the data looked like it backed everything up, too. Maybe it was the fact that nothing had happened for months. Maybe it just sounded like a good idea, y'know, save lives and get people away from hurricanes; who would say no? Or maybe I didn't realize what the idea meant, it feels easy to order the relocation of SCP-8827-1s. When you say that, without thinking, it doesn't feel quite real. It doesn't feel good or bad, just clinically correct. I-uh… I just-

Tenjani: (motions toward the tea) Slow down and take a sip, it helps… You're rather introspective, I take it the situation has been gnawing at you.

McCullough: (pauses to drink, coughs slightly) Y-yeah, I mean yes, of course.

Tenjani: Good, good. We could use more of that around here. One last thing and I'll let you go, alright?

McCullough: Yes.

Tenjani: Could you tell me about your interactions with the O5?

McCullough: (hesitates) Am I at liberty to decline?

Tenjani: Yes, though I can also assure you that this is a safe place to speak your mind.

[END LOG]
