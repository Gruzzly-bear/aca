SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES: SCP-9730 is to remain within its concrete block at all times. All testing is to only be carried out after being approved by at least 10 members of the O5 Council. As Site-001 itself is practically impenetrable due to a large assortment of thaumaturgical and physical defensive measures, no other staff are to be assigned to SCP-9730's detail for security reasons.

DESCRIPTION: SCP-9730 refers to a small humanoid entity currently suspended within a large concrete block, which has been fitted into a load-bearing wall in Site-001's foundations. SCP-9730 is perpetually unaging, and analysis using advanced imaging technology has revealed that it has remained in a newborn-like state since its containment in 1876.

Aside from the above anomalies, SCP-9730 has also been conceptually merged with the concept of the Foundation's continued existence through the use of a now-extinct thaumaturgical rite. Through extensive research, the de facto hypothesis regarding SCP-9730 is that as long as it remains trapped in its concrete shell, the Foundation as an organization will continue to survive.

Due to the morally-dubious nature of the SCP-9730 practice, however, a number of conflicts, both within and from outside the Foundation has taken place over the ethics of SCP-9730's continued suspension and containment.

For more information on SCP-9730, please view the history section below.

Chapter 1: The Baby And The Origins Of The Foundation (1876)

According to official Foundation records and the testimonies of the Overseer Council (the predecessor to the current O5 Council), Foundation Administrator and First Overseer Fritz Williams went on an extended sabbatical on 1/1/1876.

Prior to departure, several Foundation Overseers described his demeanor as highly unusual, noting that he often appeared “deeply contemplative” and “lost within his own mind” - with multiple accounts of him shambling around the Williams Manor1 without clear purpose, saying nothing when prompted.

Administrator Williams did not return upon the conclusion of his sabbatical period, and responded to no Foundation correspondence during that time.

SCP-9730 was first discovered by the Overseer Council in the private office of the Administrator at Site-001 on 3/6/1876, alongside the following note.

From The Office Of Grand Administrator I

To my compatriots and partners in the fight for what is just and what is right, I am writing this with quivering hands as I try to force myself to lay bare the truth of what I hath done. I can no longer be a part of your fight. I can no longer be a part of your world.

In my travels, I have often ruminated upon the horrid nature of our Work, as well as the role of Man and his Humanity in it. In recent days, my mind works ceaselessly - depriving me of sleep and senses as I delved deeper and deeper into introspection. I chased long-dead philosophies and sought out dark scholars. I retraced the paths that giants once followed on the path to greater enlightenment, all to understand it all.

I shall not tell you my recently-acquired conclusions. All I shall tell you is that I can only ever come to one unerring truth: the Foundation must not fall, and there is no price high enough to pay for the success of our mission.

All of you who know me knows I love the Foundation as I would a child. I have seen it grow and mature. I see the promise that it holds for the future. It is a love that I thought I would never feel again.

I discovered that I was wrong, at the same time I discovered my final role in all things.

My friends, I was presented with the opportunity to ensure the Foundation’s eternal salvation at an unthinkable cost. I paid the price, and I see myself for what I am in this cruel, cold world.

At the cost of saving the soul of the Foundation, I have condemned that of two other. One guilty, and one innocent. I have made the ultimate sacrifice, and I have resigned myself to flames of woe.

A child for a child. A damnation for damnation.

Your Administrator is a godless, cowardly, selfish man. All I can ask you is your forgiveness for making you all complicit in my darkest deed. I tell myself it was the only way. I hope that it is true.

I leave your future in your hands, now and forever.
Fritz Williams
Administrator

Although our understanding of notions such as conceptual engineering and noospheric embodiments have since developed, none of the technologies relating to it would’ve been available in the time of the Administrator. This helps us narrow down that whatever was used here is probably some complex form of advanced arcane magic.

Multiple attempts were made to cross-reference SCP-9730’s properties with notable branches of magic and anomalous cultures throughout the years, with little results. Comparisons were drawn with the Nalkan practice of “bebegrouding” - a rite practiced in some Sarkic subcultures where newborn children were submerged, not in concrete, but healthy fertile soil to absorb its nutrients. A deep-dive into the well-known practice of Baby-fortified Architecture was deemed irrelevant after Parahistorians determined that the practice was entirely aesthetic.

It wasn't until 1898 when a raid on Sirs Marshall, Carter & Dark Apothecary & Anomalous Goods recovered several dozen pages from a previously-undiscovered section of the Erikesh Codex pertaining to ancient child-related containment procedures that we were able to identify the probable cause: A rite known as "The Ascended Babe" - illustrated with imagery that greatly resembled the depiction on the Administrator's notes. The rite was only mentioned once in the text, where it was described as a "forbidden sacrifice magic, used only when the cost is too high otherwise."

Unfortunately, as the actual methodologies of the rite and its reversal methods were not present in the recovered pages, the raid accomplished little aside from substantiating the Administrator's claims.

However, despite the fact that the raid recovered 42 pages of material, Sirs Marshall, Carter & Dark's manifest showed that there were originally 48 pages stored in the warehouse. Notably, every other listed rite included an in-depth list of instructions, with the exception of "The Ascended Babe."

According to Overseer records, the Overseer Council would hold several dozen votes over the next few years on whether to free the baby from its imprisonment within the concrete cube, or not. These motions often failed, after the induction of Foundation Scribe Samson Presley into the Overseer Council granted the Pro-Baby-Internment faction a valuable one-vote edge.

In 1898, the Ninth and Twelfth Overseers both perished in a duel meant to decide the construction site of the Emerson Vault (now known as Site-19) - which led to the First Overseer appointing their seats with Pro-Baby-Internment candidates, giving the faction a super-majority. In 1906, a vote was then held to officially shutter all future discussions and votes regarding the immortal baby. After the verdict, the Second Overseer would give the following statement:

The Second Overseer, leader of the Council Pro-Baby-Internment Faction.

Do not misunderstand me, while I sympathize with my fellow Overseers across the aisle, those filled with concerns over the role of the immortal child, and I understand their perspective, this is a choice that must be made for the betterment of our organization. However, despite the above statement, Foundation historians now speculate that the reason the vote was held was so that the now majority Pro-Baby-Internment faction of the Overseer Council could get ahead of a potential confrontation with the fledgling Committee Of Ethical Containment (now known as the Ethics Committee), which according to The First Overseer, would harbor "unacceptable extremist ideals regarding immortal child liberation."

This strategy appeared to have been successful. Over the next few years, departing members of the Anti-Baby-Internment faction of the Overseer Council, bound by silence, would be replaced by candidates hand-picked by the First Overseer, and though future Overseers would not be notified of the immortal child (unless they specifically inquired) - all candidates would have to score a perfect grade on the mandatory "Councilmen Candidacy Examination" - which included the following item:

Question 1,541: You are given the choice to ensure the Foundation's eternal prosperity. HOWEVER - You must subject an immortal newborn child to an eternity in a concrete bucket. What would you do? Write a 5,000 word essay to justify your answer.

Profile: Dr. Nicole Richmond

Dir. Richmond, circa 1936.

Initially working as a researcher for the Site-17's humanoid wing, Dir. Richmond was assigned as temporary replacement for Site Director Gregory Seltzer when Dir. Seltzer was assigned to Project BLUE MARCH. Under Dir. Upon being notified of SCP-9730, Dir. Richmond was able to rally the Ethics Committee into enacting Primary Action. This course of action, however, failed, as the majority of the Ethics Committee Board Of Directors was suddenly dispatched by Mobile Task Force Alpha-1 ("Red Right Hand") in the midst of drafting a letter asking for the O5 Council's resignation. Profile: Dr. James Bernard Schafer

Dr. Schafer, circa 1925.

Inducted into the Foundation in 1921 after having met O5-6 during a venture in his hometown in North Dakota, Dr. Schafer rose through the ranks as one of the Foundation's finest thaumaturgists, and was appointed as a lead researcher of the Department Of Continuity, before his work earned him the title of Chief Ethics Overseer on the Ethics Committee.

For the last few years prior to his defection from the Foundation, Schafer had repeatedly voiced that, in his view, the Foundation had consistently failed to strive toward the betterment of humanity. His associates reported him becoming increasingly "disillusioned" with his work at the Foundation, becoming more and more politically active, and constantly reminiscing about his previous position in North Dakota.

During the Foundation Civil War, it was revealed that Dr. Schafer had been amassing a number of devout followers who shared in his philosophies for at least a decade. These followers, along with a number of Ethics Committee members led by Dir. Richmond would make-up the majority of the splinter group's numbers during the war.

Profile: Royal Fraternity of Master Metaphysicians

A meeting of Foundation Metaphysicians.

During his tenure at the Foundation, Dr. Schafer had also founded the Foundation Society Of Metaphysics, a small club of Foundation-affiliated Metaphysicians based out of Site-17. While on the surface, it served as a fraternity of like-minded individuals, it was also served as a recruiting apparatus for Dr. Schafer's own splinter group. During the Foundation Civil War, it was estimated that a large amount of the defecting Foundation personnel had, at some point in their past, held membership at the Society.

In January 1938, Dr. Schafer, Dir. Richmond, and the remnants of the Foundation Society Of Metaphysics would resurface publicly, rebranding itself as the Royal Fraternity of Master Metaphysicians. Their first official act would be the purchase of the 24-acre Vanderbilt Estate "Idle Hour" mansion, later renamed "Peace Haven" and used as their base of operations.

Operating publicly as a "diaper brigade," in secret, Dr. Schafer had begun converting new followers to a militant infant-based apocalyptic cult focused around the arrival of a "baby messiah" that would bring order to the world. Dr. Schafer's inner circle would adopt the title of "Storks" with Schafer himself adopting the title of "Chief Stork." Purportedly, Dr. Schafer's devotion to this pet project would lead to a developing schism with the faction of the splinter group lead by Dir. Richmond.

Despite their above-Veil operation and blatant appropriation of Department Of Continuity management techniques, the Royal Fraternity of Master Metaphysicians remained under the Foundation's radar for over a year, as the group was then indistinguishable from non-anomalous Metaphysics clubs and/or pro-natalist organizations.

Profile: Baby Jean Gauntt - "The Immortal Baby"

Baby Jean, circa 1939.

In November 1939, Dr. Schafer finally resurfaced publicly, announcing his organization's intentions to adopt Baby Jean, the newborn child of Catherine Gauntt, a poor waitress. He explained he would raise Baby Jean into an "immortal being" through the use of metaphysics, a special vegetable diet, and by disallowing anyone from mentioning any terms relating to the concepts of death or disease in her presence. By doing this, he explained, Baby Jean would be preserved from old age and death.

Immediately after the publication of Dr. Schafer's interview by The New York Times, the O5 Council went into high-alert, as Dr. Schafer had divulged the basic steps of Erikeshan conceptual engineering to an audience of millions. This, coupled with his proposal of creating an immortal baby led to the conclusion that somehow, he had come into possession of the missing pages of "The Ascended Babe."

It was unclear if Dr. Schafer publicizing his plans was meant to be perceived as a threat, a trap, or an invitation for co-operation. Regardless, the O5 Council would decide that an enemy organization with its own SCP-9730 instance would be too dangerous to exist. As such, proposals for a raid on Peace Haven was approved the following week.

Upon MTF Omega-8 “Baby Snatchers” entering the Peace Haven grounds, it was quickly discovered that the Royal Fraternity of Master Metaphysicians had used multiple temporal-spacial anomalies in order to enlarge the mansion's interior significantly. The difficulty in navigating the mansion as well as various encounters with pockets of temporal disruption greatly increased the time length of the mission.

Notable incidents included a member of the task force accidentally killing his own grandson who was also his paternal grandfather, constantly reanimating hostile members of Dr. Schafer's "Storks" personnel, and at least 3 instances of agents accidentally wandering into a different time period.

By the time MTF Omega-8 was able to reach Dr. Schafer's control room, what was to be a 1-day mission had extended to a year-long operation in objective time, with at least 3 billion years of subjective time in-between.

Entry 6:

It's a beautiful morning.

I see the sun shining over the horizon for the first time in my life, after only seeing it in the old archival files. The feeling and sight is indescribable. I feel like I’m experiencing something that mere humans were never meant to enjoy, and maybe that’s somewhat true.

The grass is green. The shade is more vivid than any that I’ve ever seen. I have never seen nature in this way, so vivid and clear. The sky is so, so blue. Words fail me so completely.

I smell flowers around me, hear the buzz of bees and birds. I don’t know where they were all hiding, but they’re all so abundant now. It’s like nature’s rising out of the ground as quickly as possible to compensate for all the previous decades of suppression.

Dr. Schafer survived his imprisonment in the giant concrete baby completely intact, only to have fallen onto and been impaled through the groin by a particularly sharp dead tree. He had apparently been bleeding out for several hours before I woke up, and had tried to minimize his suffering by chewing through his wrist, which might have not worked very well for him.

Everything is alright in the world.

I lie back and soak in a serenity not seen in a hundred years or so. It’s so beautiful, I could die here and have been content with that. But there’s still work to be done. The world isn’t fixed quite yet. There are so many, many things to do. But it’s okay, because now I know what I’m doing it for. I'll be reminded of it every time the sun rises.

And speaking of things to do, I hear it then. Something in the background. I turn my head and it is there. She is there. There was a nearby Foundation installation that we could walk to, where there would be food, and maps, and computers that could be used to communicate with other Sites.

But in the mean time, I take off my cloak, and wrap her in it.

“Hello,” I say to her, “Nice to meet you, baby.”

“Goo goo ga ga,” she says. “Nice to see you, too.”

One last thing to do, then. One more time, for bad times' sake.
