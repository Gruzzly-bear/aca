Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8387 is to be contained in Chamber 23 of Site-67. The containment chamber must be paired with an observation room and a Scranton Reality Anchor (SRA) attached to the ceiling. No musical instruments or any devices capable of audio playback are permitted in the containment chamber, except during tests. The SRA must undergo monthly maintenance; no testing is allowed during this period.

Before any testing regarding SCP-8387 is scheduled, all songs to be played during the testing period must be approved in advance and clearly documented. D-Class participants in the experiment should be capable of performing the music to an adequate level. Should no suitable D-Class be available, any D-Class with musical aptitude is permitted to be trained in drumming to a level dependent on the testing requirements.

During tests, the main SRA must be deactivated remotely. Meanwhile, the secondary SRA must be reactivated to reduce SCP-8387's area of effect around the containment chamber. At least 4 security personnel must enter the SCP-8387 containment area. In the event of a security flaw, the SRA must be reactivated from the observation room.

Description: SCP-8387 is a drum kit created by "Entertainers Inventory"1. The drum kit is composed of: a bass drum, a snare drum, a floor tom, a high tom, a mid tom, three crash cymbals, a hi-hat, and a ride cymbal.

SCP-8387

SCP-8387 is capable of modifying reality within a maximum radius of 2¬†km: while any song is played on the drum kit, depending on the themes, lyrics, and media connected to the song (if it has any of these elements), SCP-8387 will alter the surrounding environment in a manner that mirrors the characteristics previously mentioned of the music, initiating a sequence of specific effects, including the creation of living beings, designated as SCP-8387-1.

SCP-8387's effects remain in place during the duration of the song and after it. The only way to remove those effects is through the use of reality-bending objects, such as SRAs.

When a song is used on SCP-8387, there's a possibility that, depending on the themes of the song, SCP-8387-1 instances will be created. Those instances do not appear to be sentient and instead follow the same themes present in the song, and both the physical structure and behavior of these instances vary from song to song.

SCP-8387-1 instances, depending on the song played on SCP-8387, are often physically superior and hostile towards humans. SCP-8387-1 instances present a wide range of potentially catastrophic anomalous properties, which happen only thanks to the song's support and manipulation on SCP-8387-1 instances, and immediate action should be taken to terminate them.

SCP-8387-1 instances present only skin as a biological component, but they don't present any health problems besides the absence of sentience.

Addendum 8387.1: Discovery

SCP-8387 was discovered on 12/09/2023. A Foundation satellite located and tracked numerous reality alterations in the area of Saratoga, San Jose. MTF Lambda-5 "White Rabbits" reconnaissance squad tracked the origin of the anomalous occurrence, while other Foundation forces evacuated and restricted access to the area to minimize victims. After reconnaissance, it has been confirmed that at least 17 people were killed by SCP-8387-1 instances during the event.

At the center of the anomalous occurrence, Lambda-5 operatives found 4 uninjured PoI hidden in the same house where SCP-8387 was located. The PoI were:

  • PoI #1 - Robert Wooler
  • PoI #2 - Alan Foxall
  • PoI #3 - Samantha Iron
  • PoI #4 - Roy April

After SCP-8387's effects were nullified with SRAs, the 4 PoIs were sent to Site-67 for interrogation about SCP-8387; any communication about SCP-8387 taking place outside Site-67 was blocked, every media post about the incident was blocked from publishing or deleted, and the citizens of Saratoga were administered Class-A amnestics. All the instruments of the PoI were brought to Site-67 to analyze them with an Anomalous Scanner Type 51G2 to see which of them was anomalous. It was later discovered through the scanner that SCP-8387 has a level of 5 Hm.

Addendum 8387.8: Brief

Following the interview on 21/10/2023 about SCP-8387, Director William Larn requested to Senior Researcher Jeremy Wellington a meeting to recap the studies on SCP-8387 and decide on the next actions to take regarding SCP-8387 and the four PoI connected to the anomaly.

VIDEO LOG, DIRECTOR'S OFFICE, CAMERA 1

DATE: 21/10/2023, 20:41

NOTE: Director William Larn is still making a final decision about the request brought by Senior Researcher Jeremy Wellington. The site director himself will transmit the final decision.

[BEGIN LOG]
(Researcher Wellington enters the director's office and sits in a chair.)

Researcher Wellington: Sorry if I'm late, sir. I have finished filling out my reports about SCP-8387; here's everything you need.

Director Larn: Excellent work, Wellington.

(Director Larn starts reading the various documents.)

Director Larn: I see you made some progress in the study of SCP-8387.

Researcher Wellington: Yes, sir. However, we still didn't have a clue about the place of origin of SCP-8387. I'm afraid that we may have to intervene in one of the Entertainers Inventory industries to obtain other information.

Director Larn: Nah, I don't think so. Some studies may take months or even years to discover what we need, so we must be patient. Anyway, I want to check those other files.

(Director Larn takes out other documents to read.)

Director Larn: I see you connected the kids' files to the SCP-8387 group. All of your colleagues created another set of files for the psychology sector on PoI.

Researcher Wellington: I united those files with the SCP-8387 group because you directly requested that months ago; Those kids tried their best on the research on SCP-8387, while also not being at the center of the study, and they stayed under my supervision every day.

Director Larn: Oh, right! I almost forgot about this‚Ä¶ I think they did enough: I read the various reports and updates, and I'm glad of how much they united. We still need to complete the procedure, though: administer them Class-A amnestics, and bring them back to Saratoga. About SCP-8387, continue your work.

Researcher Wellington: Well, director, we can do those kids a little favor.

Director Larn: And what is this favor?

Researcher Wellington: As I was on my way here, I realized that Site-67 will be 50 years old since the day it was operational.

Director Larn: Yes, I know that: I've worked here for almost 30 years. What does this plan consist of?

Researcher Wellington: Those kids are part of a rock group, and they also made a contribution, even if they're unspecialized. For me, a good way to say "thank you" is if they will play various songs for the 50-year festival. I'll take full responsibility for the turn shifts, the presence of security, and the multiple characteristics of the festival. I even helped them make a little poster along the way here.

(Researcher Wellington puts the poster on the table.)

Researcher Wellington: Also, I wanted to point out that they asked for it.

Director Larn: Hm‚Ä¶ You surely got affected by those kids, Wellington. I generally don't do this kind of stuff here, but I could let it pass. You know that this facility is my home, but we need to organize everything perfectly, or else we are vulnerable.

(Director Larn takes all the files and puts them on a shelf.)

Director Larn: You can end your work, Wellington, but don't you dare spoil the possible surprise for the kids.
