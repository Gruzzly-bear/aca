SCP-9964

{$caption}

"Brazil: Love it or Leave it", the government's slogan from 1960 to 1970.

Item #: SCP-9964

Object Class: Babel1

Security Clearance: Level 5/Thaumiel

Special Containment Procedures: In a joint effort with the Central Intelligence Agency and the Federal Government of Brazil, the Department of Continuity has established a program of surveillance throughout all Brazilian broadcast services, in order to intercept any instances of SCP-9964. All intercepted broadcasts are to be infused with the Antimemetic Agent God, Nation, and Family to mitigate the aftereffects of a possible exposure.

In combination with this, the Brazilian Branch of the SCP Foundation is to investigate and confront anti-Veil organizations operating within the country to disrupt the chain of events following exposure to SCP-9964, with particular attention given to the activities of Insurgência do Caos – Sol Vermelho,2 which declared war on Normalcy in 1983.

Should an anti-Veil movement seize political power in Brazil, the country’s Armed Forces shall immediately operate under the direction of the Department of Continuity.

Description: SCP-9964 is the collective designation for anomalous television broadcasts of Brazilian clubs and National Team football matches from 1967 to 1982. These transmissions manifest spontaneously in national television signals after the conclusion of sports or cultural programming. 7,323 instances of SCP-9964 have been detected as of writing, becoming more prominent after each year.

Although SCP-9964 broadcasts closely resemble their non-anomalous counterparts and often lead to the same outcomes, notable deviations have been identified throughout time. Documented examples may diverge from one another and include, but are not limited to:

• The absence of a crowd;

• If present, the crowd consists of military leaders, landowners, and businessmen from the 1964–85 administration,3 or numerous faceless humanoid figures;

Ademir da Guia, an iconic footballer for Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras, prior to missing a shot against Clube de Regatas Vasco da Gama during an SCP-9964 broadcast. Eyes are absent, and the subject’s jaw is detaching gradually.

• The commentators are unaware of the players' names;

• The footballers and coaches from both teams are wounded,4 severely disfigured or missing limbs;

• The players do not break for half-time;

The Brazilian team before their match against Peru at the 1970 World Cup, as seen in an SCP-9964 instance. The Brazilian players never kicked off in this anomalous iteration, remaining unmoving for the entire match while Peru took advantage and won 41–0.

• There are no referees, although fouls are still given automatically;

• A player may spontaneously distance themselves from the field and stare at the stands, whether or not a crowd is present, thus remaining absent for the rest of the match;

• A player may deliberately fail to score when presented with a clear opportunity;

• A player may deliberately begin to walk off the field aimlessly while the match is still ongoing;

• The transmission lacks sound.

Upon exposure, 91% of subjects reported severe distress regarding the conditions of Brazilian workers under the business-military regime and subsequently engaged in deeper study of Brazilian history from a far-left perspective. Of these, 80% would later join an organization aligned to that political spectrum.

For all intents and purposes, the circumstances described above render SCP-9964 a high-risk political instrument for anti-Normalcy groups.

A transcription of an SCP-9964 broadcast is attached below.

1977 Brazilian Championship Final

Atlético Mineiro 0 - 0 São Paulo

STADIUM: Mineirão, Belo Horizonte - Minas Gerais

SÃO PAULO: Waldir Peres; Getúlio, Tecão, Bezerra, Antenor; Chicão, Teodoro (substituted by Peres), Darío Pereyra; Viana (substituted by Neca), Mirandinha, Zé Sérgio; coach Mário Juliato.

[BEGIN LOG]

00:03: After a draw in regular time, both teams gather to prepare for the penalty shootout. All players appear exhausted.

01:45: The stands are shown. The only spectators are Ernesto Geisel,5 Carlos Alberto Brilhante Ustra,6 and Sérgio Paranhos Fleury.7

10:51: Getúlio misses.

13:08: A police officer is now seen outside of the locker room as the noises grow louder.

42:47: The referee is nowhere to be seen.

43:08: Alves remains unmoving.

51:30: The other players await anxiously, although the penalty is never taken.

The broadcast abruptly ended after 19 hours.

Addendum.01: Discovery

1972 Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras team seen through the first discovered instance of SCP-9964.

Following the ending of the business-military cycle, rumors of SCP-9964 began to circulate among the civilian population. Foundation personnel would not succeed in observing the anomaly until December 5, 1999, when it was documented through two main courses of action:

• The capture and execution of two Sol Vermelho operatives, resulting in the confiscation of recorded tapes of the broadcasts

• Investigation via Brazil’s emerging internet to locate the anomaly on paranormal-focused forums such as Parawatch

Excerpts of the latter are provided below.

Addendum.02: Timeline of Events

The following timeline details key events in which the Department of Continuity intervened directly in Brazil’s political sphere to safeguard the Veil and advance the Foundation’s strategic interests.

December 17, 1939

President Getúlio Vargas and Mobile Task Force "Brazil Happy Again" after the Foundation arrived in the country for the first time.

Objective: Establish the first Foundation bases on Brazilian territory under the leadership of the Department of Continuity. Ensure that the industrialization policies of President Getúlio Vargas’ government remain dependent on the United States. Investigate and study Brazil’s paranormal phenomena to gain a deeper understanding of thaumaturgy.

Outcome: Success.

February 13, 1945

Objective: Fund opposition to President Getúlio Vargas as he signals greater independence. Establish the first Brazilian operational facility for D-Class personnel in response to the growth of the local Chaos Insurgency and similar movements.

Outcome: Success.

March 4, 1947

Objective: Support President Eurico Gaspar Dutra in enforcing the re-illegalization of the Brazilian Communist Party. Conduct amnestical studies on unregistered subjects in the rural areas of Brazil. Initiate the integration of favelas into the D-Class network.

Outcome: Success.

September 2, 1961

Objective: Prevent João Goulart from assuming the presidency. Transfer federal control to the Armed Forces. Immediately eliminate the local Chaos Insurgency.

Outcome: Failure. Leonel Brizola allied with local police forces and leveraged his political power as a governor to organize widespread opposition against the Foundation’s interests. He also threatened the Department of Continuity through the use of thaumaturgical capabilities. The Chaos Insurgency is believed to have been responsible for Brizola acquiring thaumaturgical knowledge in the first place.

April 1, 1964

Objective: Coordinate the Armed Forces with the Central Intelligence Agency in the deposal of President João Goulart. Establish the Brazilian Superintendence for the Paranormal. Immediately eliminate all local anti-Veil organizations.

Outcome: Undetermined. Although the junta has seized power, threats to Normalcy have persisted, and anti-Foundation elements successfully expelled MTF "Brazil Happy Again" from the favela complex, ultimately ending the integration project.

May 17, 1969

Objective: Institute thaumaturgy as part of the training for personnel in intelligence services. Expand D-Class facilities. Advance the understanding and application of amnestics to the next thaumaturgical step, by manipulating reality itself to erase the memory of the individual.

Outcome: Success.

November 14, 1973

General Ernesto Geisel and Department of Continuity liaison Frederick Portel Ashmoon.

Objective: Assist the Armed Forces in its operations at the Araguaia Guerrilla War.13 Distribute the newly developed amnestic technology to other Latin American branches in preparation for Operation Condor.

Outcome: Success.

January 3, 1977

MTF "Brazil Happy Again" agent fatally shot in the head by Commander Clarissa Falcão during a conflict.

Objective: Eliminate the Sol Vermelho faction.

Outcome: Failure.

November 18, 1982

Objective: Support and fund emerging democratic movements in opposition to radical factions. Oppose the military-business junta and advocate for direct elections. Commence document disposal.

Outcome: Success.

November 15, 1985

Objective: Ensure that no judicial procedures are carried out against those associated with the business-military junta.

Outcome: Success.

October 2, 2015

Jair Bolsonaro serving as a federal deputy for the Progressists, 2013.

Objective: Support Jair Messias Bolsonaro as a rising political figure opposing the Workers' Party.14 Leverage the opposition’s efforts to impeach President Dilma Rousseff. Suppress SCP-9964.

Outcome: Success.

January 17, 2018

Objective: Ensure the election of Jair Messias Bolsonaro as the next President of Brazil.

Outcome: Success.

Ashmoon coughs, then takes a sip of water from his glass while keeping his eyes on Bolsonaro.

Quarentinha Braga, an iconic Paysandu Sport Club player, attempts to entertain the crowd during an SCP-9964 broadcast.

Bolsonaro gives a slight smile.

BOLSONARO: My middle name is Messias for a reason, my friend. You can count on me.

Addendum.03: The Chaos Insurgency in Brazil

The first Chaos Insurgency units in Brazil were founded in 1939 through an alliance of anarchists, nationalists and communists connected to esoteric practices, in opposition to the persecution by President Getúlio Vargas’s dictatorial government, as well as in response to the arrival of the first Foundation cells.

In the early years of the business-military junta cycle, the organization gained its contemporary notoriety by directing an insurgent process that seized three Foundation operational bases located in Northern Brazil, freeing dissidents who had been detained by the government.

Under the direction of Mirella Cruz and Hugo Lisboa, it is believed that 3,100 personnel were mobilized for armed combat against Foundation officers. In 1976, following the split that gave rise to Sol Vermelho, two of these bases were retained, and 91% of the former D-Class personnel decided to join the faction. The technological innovations currently employed by Sol Vermelho are likely the result of all the material and research stolen during this conflict, including paratechnology likely used to facilitate the manifestation of SCP-9964.

Commanders Mirella Cruz and Hugo Lisboa alongside the recently freed D-Class personnel Clarissa Falcão, following the conquering of a Foundation operative base on June 27, 1969.

Following their declaration of war, Sol Vermelho would advance to seize 17 additional Foundation bases and intervene in all attempts to investigate local supernatural phenomena. Recent studies indicate that the total number of organized members is 19,500.

The Sol Vermelho's main propaganda vehicles are the newspaper The People's Front and clandestine radio channels concealed by paranormal codification. One example of each is available below.

THE PEOPLE'S FRONT

DATE: November 26, 2023

NOTE: The interview below has been recovered from an international issue of The People's Front newspaper. It was conducted by Communist militant Luzia Tchissola, from the Red Faction of Angola's Chaos Insurgency. The interviewed is Commander Mirella Cruz, who had employed advanced thaumaturgy to halt aging, similar to her companions.

TCHISSOLA: What are the main differences between the Sol Vermelho and other Chaos Insurgency groups when it comes to opposition to the Foundation?

CRUZ: Our organization stems from a fundamental divergence regarding the existence of the Foundation itself. To us, the destruction of the Foundation should equate to the destruction of United States' imperialism in its entirety, as the greatest enemy of the Brazilian people for decades now.

TCHISSOLA: Could you explain how that came to be?

CRUZ: Once the first alliances were forged in 1939, the consensus was that the Foundation itself embodied the main contradiction we faced. However, when it thoroughly aided the military in their rise to power — with the backing of our landowners, bankers and industrialists — it became clear that we couldn't oppose their rule while treating them as a separate force from the system.

TCHISSOLA: And how was the Sol Vermelho faction born in the first place?

CRUZ: Following our heroic victories against the reactionaries, a right-wing opposition within our organization argued that we should retreat so as not to “advance recklessly.” Although every subsequent encounter with the Foundation proved successful, the Red Massacre would be invoked as a pretext to undermine our achievements. When a vote was ultimately held to decide whether our current tactics should be abandoned or not, the split materialized. We don't regret it in the slightest.

TCHISSOLA: When the Chaos Insurgency is now described as an “instrument to liberate the people,” does this necessarily imply a violent movement?

The three main symbols incorporated by the Sol Vermelho faction.

CRUZ: We do make history, but not of our own choosing. We make history according to the conditions imposed by the world around us. Make no mistake: we desire the end of all wars. Yet how does one fight an army without an army of their own? Any accusation that the people are violent is itself a confession. Simply put, you cannot vote the O5 Council out.

TCHISSOLA: But when the Chaos Insurgency assists the people in their liberation, is this carried out from an aristocratic perspective?

CRUZ: Never. Transformation simply demands that those who are prepared in advance equally prepare those who aren't. We want to help. Think of the Chaos Insurgency as a seed, while the people constitute the fertile soil that will allow this new world to flourish.

TCHISSOLA: Do you think this revolutionary movement will be successful?

CRUZ: What I think doesn't matter. What matters is that those who fight alongside me continue the struggle, even if the enemies' bullets make me physically disappear. You see, the masses can never be destroyed in their entirety. And no tsunami will be turned back once the earthquake has struck.

TCHISSOLA: If the Brazilian revolutionary movement succeeds, what becomes of the Chaos Insurgency? What can guarantee its victory will last?

CRUZ: History has shown us that movements like ours do not succeed merely by seizing political power. The transformation must be cultural, too. The fundamental mission of our vanguard, once the tools to reshape society are placed in the hands of the masses — the only ones capable of wielding them — is to demonstrate a new way of thinking it.

TCHISSOLA: Could that mean a free Brazil would try to force its neighbors to follow the same path because of cultural tensions?

CRUZ: I'm sorry, but that's laughable.

TCHISSOLA: Could you elaborate?

CRUZ: Of course we want our neighbors to go the same route, but that's not up to us. Brazil will assist the masses against the many imperialist threats — the United States, China, Russia, Israel, the United Kingdom, and so on — but only when a proper mass movement exists to begin with. If the people don't call for a revolution now, if there's no vanguard to guide them, what's the point? We're not adventurers.

TCHISSOLA: What if power is retaken by the old world? Is there any hope for the revolution?

CRUZ: The ideological transformation is intended to prepare the masses for counterattack. Every oppressed individual is a revolutionary, and every revolutionary must know how to wield their weapon.

TCHISSOLA: What are your weapons, Ms. Cruz?

CRUZ: We’ve been training our warriors in thaumaturgy for decades, and we’ve also learned to harness the power of the memories imposed on us by the reactionaries. That, without a doubt, is our greatest achievement so far.

TCHISSOLA: How come?

CRUZ: The rage carried from generation to generation has now taken a life of its own — and it will haunt them. Forever. This is what they’ve named SCP-9964.

TCHISSOLA: Considering everything you've just said, what will happen if the masses are rendered powerless following the revolution?

CRUZ: The new society will be crushed. Simple as that. Terror will rise again, masquerading as a mass movement while crushing the people under its boot. And still, I know hope won’t be lost, because it will find a way to be reborn.

TCHISSOLA: Before we conclude, I'd like to thank you for your time, Ms. Cruz. You're a very kind woman. Do you have any final thoughts you would like to share with our readers?

CRUZ: The pleasure is all mine! Modern times are reminding the people that change is inevitable. We believe the red sun will shine, again and again, as many times as necessary, across the globe. Either we're all free, or no one is.

[END LOG]
