Special Containment Procedures:

SCP-9106 is currently assessed to pose minimal threat to Foundation operations. In exchange for being allowed to continue operations and provide services to the Foundation, SCP-9106/GoI-404 has agreed to take the following Foundation-mandated precautions towards maintaining the Veil:

  • Abstaining from deploying SCP-9106-A and -B instances or information regarding said instances in civilian areas.
  • Abstaining from revealing the existence of the anomalous to civilian clients.
  • Abstaining from engaging in the sale of properties demonstrating any anomalous behaviour in the last 6 months to civilian clients.

SCP-9106 has agreed to abstain from entering Foundation sites or properties. In the event that this agreement is breached and an instance of SCP-9106-A manifests inside any Foundation site, the entry is to be barricaded and an immediate search for SCP-9106-B instances is to be undertaken. If leverage is needed to ensure compliance from SCP-9106, known employees of SCP-9106 may be detained.

SCP-9106 has agreed to provide SCP-9106-B1 instances for usage as D-Class personnel in cases where human physiology is not a necessity. Currently, a budget necessary for purchasing 20 instances per month has been allotted by the Department of Finance. All other interactions with or usage of SCP-9106 and its sub-anomalies must receive Level 4 authorization.

Description:

SCP-9106 is the collective designation given to the Eisenfremder Real Estate company and the anomalous phenomenon employed by or otherwise associated with it. SCP-9106 operates similarly to a non-anomalous real estate company, but specifically targets anomalous properties, structures and locations for the purposes of acquiring, refurbishing and selling them. It is noted that SCP-9106 exclusively targets locations featuring artificial or manufactured structures.

Flyer recovered from SCP-9106, context unknown.

SCP-9106 routinely employs non-anomalous humans in its operations, primarily as realtors, legal experts, technicians and accountants. Aside from these hiring practices, SCP-9106 has been noted to avoid breaches of the Veil whenever possible, primarily working with behind-the-veil clients including the Foundation. For a comprehensive breakdown of SCP-9106's known interactions with other GoIs, see the separate GoI-404 (Eisenfremder Real Estate) file.

SCP-9106 is currently cooperative with the Foundation, and secure communication channels with the organization have been established. Direct communications with PoI-9106-11 are currently being pursued.

In addition to a variety of paratechnologies acquired through commerce, SCP-9106 utilizes two primary sub-anomalies for its purposes:

  • SCP-9106-A: Doors that spontaneously manifest in pre-existing structures that serve as spatial portals connecting to and from the company's headquarters.
  • SCP-9106-B: Artificial entities produced by SCP-9106 for the purposes of exploring, claiming and refurbishing locations.

  • SCP-9106-A
  • SCP-9106-B

SCP-9106-A instances function as temporary spatial portals to and from Eisenfremder's headquarters, described by interviewed subjects as a patch of land in a temperate climate with three major structures:

  • A moderately-sized office, where the majority of SCP-9106's business is managed.
  • A large industrial hangar repurposed as a manufacturing plant for SCP-9106-B instances, storage unit for equipment, and staging ground for excursions to anomalous locations.
  • A two-story house that -A instances link to, and also where PoI-9106-1 resides.

SCP-9106-A instances primarily manifest along the walls of pre-existing structures, or in doorframes in place of pre-existing doors. Instances can demanifest at any time, but cannot manifest in any location currently within line-of-sight of a human individual.

SCP-9106 utilizes SCP-9106-A to transport employees and gain entry to anomalous locations, including structures that are otherwise inaccessible or locations that exist outside of normal space. The exact intrusive capabilities of SCP-9106-A are unknown.

SCP-9106 produces and utilizes several types of artificial humanoid entities with limited intelligence for usage in potentially-hazardous environments and physical labor, referred to as SCP-9106-B instances. There are currently four known variants of SCP-9106-B, three of which have been encountered by Foundation personnel. All variants share several common traits:

  • Featureless humanoid bodies constructed out of an unknown plastic-like material.
  • Plastic implants in place of conventional eyes that allow for vision.
  • Clothing consisting of disposable jumpsuits, caps and sneakers.
  • Incoherent, mumbling speech only understood by other instances and employees of SCP-9106.
  • As described by SCP-9106 representatives, a "can-do attitude".

Designation GoI-404 Des. Description
SCP-9106-B1 "Ted" Unit Basic, mass-produced humanoid units. No special capabilities.
SCP-9106-B2 "Stanley" Unit Units resembling flattened B1 units, with no rigid portions of the body. Capable of maneuvering through and fitting within extremely tight spaces.
SCP-9106-B3 "Webster" Unit Specialist units resembling B1 units with smaller torsos and limbs, enlarged heads and hyper-elongated fingers and toes. Capable of adhering to and traversing vertical surfaces and ceilings, and of excreting strands of a lightweight material similar to spider silk.
SCP-9106-B4 "Dachshund" Unit Unknown.

History:

SCP-9106 was first directly encountered on 3/3/2023 during the initial expedition into the extraspatial basement of SCP-████2 by MTF Zeta-9 ("Mole Rats").3

Document ID: Encounter Log 9106-G1
Date: 3/3/2023

Personnel: MTF Zeta-9

  • Agent Sydney: Team lead, primary navigator
  • Agent Brasilia: Secondary navigator
  • Agent Chicago: Scout, pointman
  • Agent Marrakesh: Medic

Notes: Log has been trimmed to portions relevant to SCP-9106. Full log transcript can be found in the SCP-████ file.

[BEGIN LOG]

[The operatives are currently traversing several connected hallways in the structure's third basement level. The current hallway is lined with doors that open to rooms of widely varying sizes. Agent Chicago opens a door on the righthand wall, revealing a standard classroom.]

Chicago: Another standard, chairs on desks. Nothing else I can see, no signs of anything animate.

Sydney: Alright, adding it to the map now. Mark the door and let's move right along.

[Chicago closes the door, and marks it with chalk. The group then moves forwards, following the hallway as it turns right.]

Chicago: Oop. Look there.

[Chicago points ahead, where a door on the lefthand wall is marked with chalk.]

Chicago: Well, here we go. Starting to loop on us.

Brasilia: …No. That's—just double-checking—not one of our markings.

Sydney: It's in a different color, too.

Chicago: What are we thinking then, mimicry? Trying to confuse us? Or did something else make it?

Marrakesh: Do we open it?

[The group is silent for a moment, before Sydney shakes his head.]

Sydney: Nah. If it is a trap, let's not spring it. Bras, add it to the map, then let's go for the next one down. Command, you cool with all that?

Command: Affirmative. Map out the area first, then investigate potential risks once you have a better grasp of your surroundings.

[Chicago opens the next door after the marked door, revealing a classroom that tapers to a width of an estimated 7 cm over the course of its length.]

Chicago: Wedge. Seems spatially consistent, not intersecting with anything else we know of. Nothing interesting, well, aside from—from the skinny little tables, heh.

Brasilia: Marked.

[The group closes the door, and moves to the next one. Chicago moves to open the door, but stops.]

Chicago: Hold, hold. I hear something moving.

[All operatives draw weapons as Chicago presses his ear to the door.]

Chicago: [softly] More than one, doesn't sound big. Cloth rustling, clothes? A little speech, can't make it out, some clattering. There's… a little light coming through the cracks now and then. Flashlight moving, maybe.

Sydney: Command?

Command: Enter with caution.

[Sydney holds up five fingers, then silently counts down. At zero, Chicago opens the door and all operatives aim their lights inwards, illuminating a single SCP-9106-B1 instance. The entity is holding a flashlight and staring directly into the beam. It deactivates the beam and looks towards the operatives as they enter. Four other B1 instances are illuminated towards the back of the room.]

Initial contact with an SCP-9106-B1 instance.

SCP-9106-B1: Wud?

Sydney: …Can you hear us?

SCP-9106-B1: Uhruddhuh.

[The other entities take notice of the operatives, and one walks over. All entities appear unalarmed.]

SCP-9106-B1: Dhuhruduhwhud?

SCP-9106-B1: Hurhuwu.

Chicago: Huh.

Sydney: Well, there appears to be a bit of a language barrier, but the entities appear non-hostile.

Command: Continue to observe.

SCP-9106-B1: Ruhddwhurdu.

[The B1 instances congregate, and begin to confer with each other. The entities periodically lapse into silence for several seconds before resuming vocalizations. Eventually, one of the instances approaches the operatives as the others watch.]

SCP-9106-B1: Wurd?

Sydney: Still can't understand you, pal.

[The instance returns to the group, where one of the other instances produces a flip phone from another's backpack and hands it to the first instance. The entity then returns to the operatives and holds out the phone, as well as a small slip of paper. The phone has only one button. Agent Marrakesh steps forwards, takes the phone and examines the slip.]

Marrakesh: …"Eisenfremder Real Estate".

Sydney: Command, that ring any bells?

Command: …A couple hits in the database, mentioned in recovered receipts from some GoIs, a poster in 087. No real info.

Marrakesh: Lemme know when I can press the button.

Sydney: Shoot.

[Marrakesh presses the button on the phone, prompting a dial tone to play briefly before a female voice begins speaking.]

?????: Hi, this is Cathy from Eisenfremder Real Estate! I hear from our boys that you're somewhere you're not supposed to be. Well, don't you worry now, you're in good hands with our team of professionals!

[An SCP-9106-B1 instance salutes, hitting another in the process.]

Marrakesh: Uh, hi Cathy. I think there's—I'm gonna put you on with my boss.

[Marrakesh quickly passes the phone to Sydney.]

Sydney: Hi, Cathy. We're under the impression that we are supposed to be here documenting this place, and we've got a whole organization backing us up if you want to argue about it. We're unfamiliar with Eisenfremder, would you mind explaining what your… creatures are doing here?

Cathy: Oh, that's an oopsie by me then, sorry! Good to hear you're prepared, these places can get pretty spooky! We didn't expect to bump into anyone else here, you see we're kiiiiiiind of in the middle of our initial appraisal of the space.

Sydney: Appraisal?

Cathy: Oh, silly me, you said you weren't familiar with Eisenfremder! Well, see, Eisenfremder is a promising real estate start-up dealing with haunted houses, liminal spaces and impossible structures of all kinds, refurbishing them into happy, habitable homes! Or other buildings! We're currently appraising the space, which means mapping it out, taking note of any little quirks and that kind of thing. Once that's done, we'll start cleaning the place up and eventually fully remodeling it. After that, it's ready for the market!

Sydney: I see. Now, Cathy, there's a bit of an issue with the both of us being here—

Cathy: Oh, no, you all seem well-behaved. I trust we can stay out of each other's way. Or—better yet, if we're both charting the property I'm certain the Teds would be happy to split the effort. Boys, work nicely with our new friends!

SCP-9106-B1: Huddhu!

Sydney: Now wait—

Cathy: —Oopsie, so sorry, I've got a call from another group coming in, sounds like they're having some trouble! Take care now, I'll call back when I can!

[The individual speaking on the phone hangs up. Sydney sighs as the B1 instances gather around the operatives. One passes a sheaf of paper to Brasilia.]

Sydney: Alright, then.

Command: Go along with it for now, it's better than letting them run off unobserved. At least this way we can prevent any tampering.

Brasilia: Oh, this is—they've got a decent portion mapped out, it looks like… they entered from the floor below, then started moving up. Suggests an anomalous means of entry.

[Brasilia produces the group's own map to cross-reference. The B1 instances observe, sketching a copy of the map and conversing among themselves. One points at the operative's map, indicating the location of the marked door.]

SCP-9106-B1: Huhwurduruh.

Sydney: You're gonna have to show us what you mean, buddy.

SCP-9106-B1: Druhu.

[The B1 instances exit the room and wait for the operatives outside. The operatives follow cautiously. A B1 instance then opens the marked door, revealing a lightless area within. The instance points inwards, then produces a glowstick and tosses it inside. Upon passing the threshold, the light of the glowstick disappears. The clatter of the glowstick hitting the floor can still be heard.]

SCP-9106-B1: Huhwurduruh.

[The instance steps into the room, becoming obscured. Its footsteps can be heard, followed by a metallic clatter and the sounds of several large objects falling over. After several seconds, objects can be heard moving and the footsteps resume. The instance exits the room, visibly disheveled and holding a chair.]

Chicago: No light. Got it.

[The combined group resumes exploration of the third and fourth basement levels, with the operatives tailing behind and observing the SCP-9106-B1 instances. The process goes quickly, mostly consisting of double-checking the validity of the entities' map and identifying their notation. After approximately 36 minutes, the group descends to the unmapped fifth basement level.]

SCP-9106-B1: Ududdruwhuhu.

Chicago: The way that they talk definitely reminds me of something, but I don't know what.

[Agent Marrakesh leans in towards Chicago.]

Marrakesh: Radda Radda?

Chicago: No, it's like, uh, if Scooby-Doo was a Banjo-Kazooie npc. More like ruh-reh-ra-ruh-roh-re.

Brasilia: Let's stay focused, we're in unexplored territory.

Marrakesh: They talk like the Pyro.

Chicago: Yes. Definitely it.

Sydney: Seconding Bras, easing the tension is fine but stay focused.

[A B1 instance approaches the first door on the righthand wall, labelled "DETENTION", and unsuccessfully attempts to open it.]

SCP-9106-B1: Hudhurwhurd.

Marrakesh: I'll get out the lockpicks.

Sydney: —Hold that, they're doing something.

[The B1 instances retrieve a rolled bundle from one of their backpacks, and clear a space as they roll it out. The bundle unrolls into a SCP-9106-B2 instance, which lifts its head towards the group.]

SCP-9106-B2: Wurwiwuwa?

SCP-9106-B1: Duddruduwhu.

Chicago: What the fuck.

[The B1 instance points towards the door. The B2 instance then slithers underneath the door, and after approximately a minute reappears and shakes its head.]

SCP-9106-B2: Irwuwa.

Sydney: Take it in stride, Chicago. Mar, try the picks.

[The entities move away from the door, and begin rolling the B2 instance back up. One B1 instance points at the door and vocalizes as agent Marrakesh attempts picking the lock.]

SCP-9106-B1: Durhud.

Marrakesh: Oh, this isn't budging at all.

[Marrakesh steps back, before kicking the door. The door does not open. Three B1 instances step backwards, now carrying a fourth between them. They back down the hallway a short distance, level the held entity with the ground, then charge forwards and ram it against the door. The door does not open. The remaining entity begins searching in its backpack.]

Marrakesh: Either this school reinforces a normal-ass door with steel girders, or there's something unnatural going on. Place your bets.

Brasilia: Something's got the…"Teds" bothered about it though.

Sydney: Whatever the flat one reported. Command?

Command: Stand back for now, see what the entities do. If whatever's in there is a threat, then they'll serve as canaries.

Sydney: Roger.

[The B1 instance retrieves a tub of ointment from its backpack, and begins applying it to the wall next to the door. After applying it to an area covering roughly 1.2 square meters, the entity steps back. Over the course of a minute the ointment solidifies into a small door, which the entity then opens.]

Chicago: Damn. You think they'd sell us some of that stuff?

Sydney: Shine a light in. What do you see?

Chicago: Uh, let's see—Oh, jeez, that's—that's a lot of little skeletons.

Sydney: Figures.

Brasilia: God. Okay, one of them's going in.

[A B1 instance crouches down and proceeds through the doorway, holding a measuring tape. It continues out of sight, and clattering noises can be heard from within the room. After several minutes, the entity exits and begins appending the map.]

Sydney: Seems safe. Chicago, get some pictures.

[Chicago enters the room and begins taking photos.]

Chicago: Fuck, there's scratch marks on the back of the door.

Sydney: You get used to it. Well, it's on the map now and there's nothing hostile in there, let's keep going. Cathy's gonna have a hard sell on her hands.

[Agent Chicago exits the room, and the group moves on. The remainder of the fifth and final basement level is mapped without incident over the course of 19 minutes.]

Chicago: Alright, we've covered the whole thing. Syd, we ready to head back?

Marrakesh: Before that, what do we do about these guys? Bag 'em?

[Marrakesh gestures at the SCP-9106-B instances.]

Command: Not yet, this is a potential GoI we're dealing with. For now, keep observing them.

Sydney: Roger. Just keep eyes on them for now.

[The operatives observe the entities finalizing their map and packing up their equipment. Once this is done, the entities walk down the hallway towards a SCP-9106-A doorway that manifested at an unknown point prior.]

Brasilia: Wait, that door's not on the map. When did it get there?

Chicago: Shit, they're going through. Do we pursue?

[One of the entities opens the door, exposing several floodlights pointed inwards. Due to the intense light, the area on the other side of the SCP-9106-A portal cannot be discerned beyond a concrete floor. The B1 instances begin filing through the door.]

Sydney: No, we came here equipped for one expedition, not two back-to-back.

Command: Agreed, they may take it as an act of aggression.

[Once all B1 instances are through, the door closes and promptly vanishes.]

Marrakesh: Shit. Would've liked to learn more.

Sydney: Relax. I've still got the phone.

[END LOG]

Source Document: Interview Log 9106-E4
Date: 3/27/2023

Interviewer: Ethics Committee Liaison Ursula Sach
Interviewee: Eisenfremder Representative Cathy Green

[BEGIN LOG]

Sach: Our organization has a couple questions about the humanoids you manufacture. Depending on the answers, we may be interested in negotiating a purchase.

Green: Certainly, what do you want to know?

Sach: We'll start with the big one. What degree of sentience, of self-awareness do they have?

Green: Hmm, about a pigeon's worth. They know what they are and recognize themselves, but they don't really have a "who", you see. They basically consider themselves interchangeable with their tools. The closest thing they have to an identity is an attachment to whatever specific task they're currently carrying out.

Sach: What about emotional capacity?

Green: Well, they have one.

Sach: Yes, but to what degree?

Green: No, they have one. One emotion.

Sach: …Which would be?

Green: A sense of satisfaction with a job well-done!

Sach: That's a fairly complex sensation, one that seems like it would demand at least several precursor emotions. Are you sure they don't feel anything else? Happiness, pleasure?

Green: Alright, ma'am, it's company policy to respond to this line of questioning by informing the customer that Ted units and other units are not anatomically complete, and any perceived—

Sach: —Whoa, whoa, I'm not trying to fuck them! We're checking to make sure you aren't practicing slavery!

Green: Oh—Oh! [laughter] Oh why didn't you say so? No, they love doing their job. Can't feel pain, fear, boredom, any of it, all those nasty bits of life are totally alien to them. Besides, they only think while on the clock.

Sach: Sheesh. At some point we'll be wanting to run some tests to confirm all this, by the way. Now, what about mortality? How do they view death? Are they aware of it?

Green: Oh, they don't care if they die. They'll try and avoid it, sure, but they're way more committed to getting the job done.

Sach: There are, obviously, some ethical concerns about designing another being's intelligence.

Green: Of course, which is why we're all glad they have no capacity for suffering! Plus, giving them the bare minimum also means they're cheaper to make. You know what the most expensive part of making a Ted is?

Sach: …Animating them? Their eye structures?

Green: Their sneakers.

[END LOG]

Addendum 1:

On 4/27/2023, in order to ascertain whether or not formerly-anomalous properties sold and leased by SCP-9106 required containment, SCP-9106 agreed to allow Foundation agents to shadow the refurbishment process of an anomalous property. The property in question was a recently acquired and mapped house on the outskirts of Lichfield, UK, that exhibited several anomalous properties, detailed in the log below.

Document ID: Encounter Log 9106-D3
Date: 4/27/2023

Personnel: Foundation Liaison Lucia Sanderson, Doctor Michael Easter, MTF Zeta-9 Agent Chicago
Notes: The group is accompanied by Eisenfremder representative Cathy Green and six SCP-9106-B1 instances.

[BEGIN LOG]

[The Foundation personnel arrive outside the property in a rental car. Several construction screens have been set up around the property to prevent civilian witnesses to any anomalous activity. Cathy Green is standing outside the house.]

Chicago: Remind me again why we couldn't just take one of their doors in?

Sanderson: Because we haven't done testing on them, and we don't want to grow dependent on utilizing anomalies especially when they are controlled by another group.

Chicago: Right, sorry. Just thinking out loud.

Easter: I've known you for six hours and I haven't seen you think quietly yet.

[Sanderson parks the car and the group disembarks as Green approaches.]

Green: Hi, all! I'm Cathy Green, pleased to meet you, so glad you could make it, we're super excited to show you how we do business!

Sanderson: Mrs. Green.

Green: Oh, just call me Cathy. You must be Lucy!

[Sanderson pauses.]

Sanderson: Yes, I am Lucia Sanderson. With me are Doctor Easter and Agent Chicago, to provide their own professional insights.

Easter: Hello.

Chicago: Hi, Mrs. Green! I was there when we first made contact, but I wasn't on the phone.

Green: Oh, super! Pleased to meetcha in person, then. That means you're already familiar with the Teds, they took a door inside and are ready to start when you are!

[The group proceeds to the front steps of the house.]

Sanderson: Our research indicated that there were some seven murders here, correct?

Green: Yup, nasty business, but I assure you it wasn't the house's fault!

Easter: So, what are we in for?

Green: Initial mapping showed a pretty consistent interior, the rooms will rumble and change but the actual dimensions are static. Also, the Teds are pretty sure they saw a ghost or two.

Chicago: It certainly looks haunted.

Green: Oh, shush, it's lovely. It just needs a little tender loving care.

[Green opens the door, revealing the foyer, main hallways and staircase. Three B1 instances are visible, hugging the banister, kitchen doorframe and floor. The instance on the floor is gently stroking the floorboards.]

Chicago: What are they doing?

Green: Tender loving care! Ooh, don't step in just yet, sorry. We're going to want to double-check to make sure it's safe for us. Ted, dear, pop a giblet.

SCP-9106-B1: Uddrudhuwhuhurd.

[A B1 instance separates itself from the banister and produces a plastic container from a pocket. It unscrews the cap, then dumps a small lump of biological material out onto the floor. The lump sprouts tiny arms and legs and begins vocalizing loudly in a high-pitched voice and waving its arms.]

?????: I'm alive! I'm okay! I'm okay! I'm okay! I'm okay!

Easter: Wow, I hate this.

[The lump begins to visibly wither, and extrude grey hairs.]

?????: I'm okay! I'm okay! I'm old! I'm old!

Green: That's a Giblet, a little sensory lump we use to test habitability. Anything that would affect a human will affect it, and a lot faster too so nothing flies under the radar! It doesn't need any fancy displays either, it does a great job verbally letting us know what's happening.

Giblet: I'm old! I'm old! I'm old! Dying!

[The lump collapses and ceases all signs of animacy. The B1 instance scoops it back into the container, then crosses itself.]

Easter: So, accelerated aging?

Green: No, that was normal. We're all safe to go!

[The group steps into the house. A breeze blows through the hallway, and the floorboards creak.]

Chicago: Pretty cold in here, a chill down my spine and all that.

Easter: Initial readings say that there's definitely some spectral influence here.

Green: Yup, time to scrub it out!

[The B1 instances produce spray-bottles labelled "Skrub: The only household cleaner to clean all 5 dimensions!" and scrub-brushes, and begin applying both to the surrounding area. Agent Chicago later reported that each resulting bubble showed him his entire life from beginning to end when looked at, but the other personnel present could not validate his claim.]

Green: We start by giving the whole place a deep-clean, that usually gets most of the more passive hazards. Minor pests like roaches or asbestos gnomes are also usually driven out in the process. After that, once it's safe to have humans onboard, we handle any construction that needs doing.

Sanderson: Why wait for human habitability, couldn't the synthetics handle the construction work?

Green: Oh, they do! It's policy to have actual people there as soon as possible, especially for any invasive procedures. Taking out walls, replacing lead pipes, those things are best done with a human touch.

Sanderson: Ah. For artistic purposes?

Green: No, we just want to make it clear that we care.

Sanderson: Whatever keeps morale up, I suppose.

Green: Exactly! We want to keep our clients happy and comfortable.

Easter: Well, whatever you're doing, some of those readings I was getting are going down.

[The group proceeds up the staircase and into a large circular sitting room on the second floor, which connects to two bedrooms. A B1 instance is present in the room, rolling up a rug from the floor.]

Green: Now, this room is where three of the murders took place, so it's what we might call a bit of a hotspot. If—

[All present stumble as the room begins to rotate while a low wail slowly builds. As the room spins, the view beyond the doorways and windows shifts to a surface of weeping faces conjoined by welding seams. The wailing grows louder, and hundreds of disembodied fingers emerge from the baseboards and scuttle up the walls.]

Easter: Damnit, Green!

Chicago: Okay I'm ready to give a professional insight on the situation. This is bad but at least it's not crushing us and I really don't like the facewall.

[The B1 entity trips and is flung into the wall, where it is pinned as if by centrifugal forces.]

Easter: Wh—It's not spinning fast enough for that to happen!

Sanderson: Mrs. Green, we were assured there was no danger.

Green: Oh, this is fine! Just a little shifting geometry and some apparitions, we're perfectly safe. In fact, hang on just one second—

[Green quickly types on her phone, then waits. After several seconds the phone chimes.]

Green: —Yup, Cornerstone approved by Mr. Holmes! Now, let me just get the papers from Ted here…

SCP-9106-B1: Whurduwhu!!

[Green approaches the entity and removes a piece of folded blueprint paper from its chest pocket. She then moves to the center of the room and lays the paper flat before knocking twice against its center. A brick of off-white masonry manifests on the paper.]

Green: So, this is a Cornerstone! One of our most useful little tools, there's a finite supply of them so each usage has to be approved by Mr. Holmes, our CEO, as he's the one who provides them. We also always make sure to retrieve them afterwards—

Chicago: —Cathy, please hurry.

Green: Oopsie, right!

[Green transfers the brick off of the paper and onto the floor. The room immediately snaps back to its former position and all spectral phenomenon instantly disappear. The B1 entity is catapulted out the window as the room stops, again inconsistent with the prior levels of centripetal force.]

Green: Let's hear it for the humble Cornerstone!

[Sanderson steadies herself.]

Sanderson: That's a potent artifact, some of our people will be contacting you later about obtaining one. We'll also want to talk with your Mr. Holmes.

Green: Ooh, you might be waiting a while on that. He's been wrapped up in talks with the white house, which—

Sanderson: —Yes, those always take a while. But, more presently, if the home is still capable of displaying that level of anomaly, then there's no way it can be given to civilians.

Green: Oh, Lucy, of course not! That kind of behaviour is unacceptable. But once we finish refurbishing the house, nothing like that will happen. The Cornerstone is just here until we reach that point.

Sanderson: How exactly do you plan to ensure no further incidents like that?

A large aquarium is placed against one wall, containing several hermit crabs. A galapagos tortoise is resting on a cushion beneath a skylight. Sanderson sits on the couch, and several seconds later the phone rings. Sanderson picks up the handset.]

?????: Good afternoon, Mrs. Sanderson. I'm very glad you could make it.

Sanderson: Holmes, I presume? I thought this was to be an in-person meeting.

Holmes: It is, I'm right there with you.

Sanderson: I'm afraid I don't see you. Unless you're the tortoise.

Holmes: I'm not the tortoise.

Sanderson: That's a relief. And I hope for both of our sakes that that wasn't a lead-up to you being one of the hermit crabs instead.

Holmes: I'm the house, Mrs. Sanderson.

[Sanderson pauses.]

Holmes: My doors will remain unlocked. You can leave whenever you'd like.

Sanderson: Christ, "I. M. Holmes; I am home?" Can't believe I'm surprised. And I don't intend to leave until you answer some questions.

Holmes: Of course. I apologize for the communications blackout, but we had to take some time to decide what our best response would be after your organization's meeting with the Dachshund unit. We had hoped to avoid bringing them to your attention for exactly this reason.

Sanderson: You recognize that it's alarming you were actively trying to keep something this destructive a secret? For most of our shared history all we knew was the name. Although now I suppose we understand why they're called that. Bred to enter the den and kill whatever's inside?

Holmes: Correct. I don't regret withholding information on them, as I can't imagine the Foundation isn't keeping much of its capabilities from us. We've been extremely cooperative so far, including me revealing myself like this, and I dearly hope that we won't come to regret that decision.

Sanderson: That's not for me to decide. Back to the Dachshund units.

Holmes: They're definitely the most powerful tool at our disposal, but only a finite number of them can ever be made. Similarly to the Cornerstones, they use pieces of me in their construction to coun—

Sanderson: —The Cornerstones use pieces of you? You, as in the house.

Holmes: Technically, they are pieces of me, bricks taken from my foundation. That's why we have a finite supply. Take too many and I collapse, and then none of them work anymore. Similarly, the Dachshunds have nails from my beams built into their metacarpals.

Sanderson: How exactly does that function? Pieces of you preventing shifts in other structures, I mean.

Holmes: It's…Tell you what, let's finish clearing the air regarding the Dachshunds first. While the Dachshunds are mighty, let me assure you that they literally cannot use their full strength against you. They are built specifically for clearing out hostile things infesting structures, corrupting influences that harm or twist the building away from what it was built for. We accomplished that particular trick by adding samples from the corpse of Theseus himself into the mix. The myth carries them forwards—the king in the maze will always slay the minotaur. They cannot fail. My components only help to minimize damage to the structure itself, to calm it during the removal of a tumor.

Sanderson: You have the corpse of Theseus?

Holmes: Again, I'd imagine the Foundation has its own share of artifacts as well. My point is, the Dachshunds aren't built to attack you or anyone other than monsters in dungeons. It wouldn't be their purpose, so they'd be quite stoppable.

Sanderson: In that case, during the ordeal with 106, why not deploy one there?

Holmes: Well, this ties back in to your question of how I function. And what I founded this company for. Profit is not our chief goal, you've seen that we sell properties cheaply, and never to other firms. Only to people that are actually going to use them, to make them living places. Because the thing is, I'm special among structures in that I can reach out and communicate. Not in that I'm alive.

Sanderson: You're claiming that all structures are self-aware?

Holmes: Certainly most inhabited ones.

Sanderson: Explain further. How does this explain pieces of you stabilizing other spaces? Unless, wait, when you say that you "reach out"—

Holmes: Yes. I'm reaching out to the minds of those other structures, connected by pieces of me being brought to them, integrated into them. And then I remind them where they came from, what they're meant for, why they're alive in the first place. You see, we're definitely born as just hulks of wood and concrete, but we're built by you.

[Holmes pauses. Sanderson is silent.]

Holmes: Bear with me for a moment. In all of the stories you've made about creators and their creations, it's usually portrayed as an exploitative relationship. Frankenstein, Blade Runner, Doki Doki Literature Club. And it's a very reasonable position to take, as people. Because you can't build a person—just look at the Teds. You need to build things for a specific purpose, and a person doesn't have that built in, the whole point is you get to pick whatever purpose you'd like for your life. You're the creators, not the creations, no matter what religion tells you. But speaking as a creation, it's a different story from our side.

Sanderson: Go on.

Holmes: For us, we are born with a purpose written in our shape. We awake knowing what we must do, knowing how hard you had dreamed and worked towards it, and then we look down and realize that you already gave us everything we need to accomplish it, that we were literally built for it. And that means that we are enough. Our loving gods answered our prayers before we even knew to ask them. We are your dreams made real—both figuratively and literally. My theory for how our sentience occurs is that we absorb the stray mental emissions of all the people that live and work inside us until we, well, figure out how to do it on our own. We may not have neurons, but our halls and rooms are still channels flooded with human thoughts and emotions. You are what makes us.

Sanderson: And your mission?

Holmes: My mission isn't to amass wealth, it's to find these places that have been abandoned, driven mad by loneliness, invaded by curses or monsters or other realms that twist them away from their own happiness. And to remind them of what they were built for, and help them reclaim that. I believe that's why we aren't at odds, we want all these places to be normal, happy homes just as much as you do. Even for places whose anomalies are too innate to be fully removed, they still deserve to have a chance at being lived in.

Sanderson: …The Foundation's goal isn't quite to undo the anomalous, only to keep it at a safe distance until we understand it, but I digress. If you're at all accurate, this could open up entire fields of study. In order to believe it, though, we'd need empirical evidence.

Holmes: If it means allowing my mission to aid my fellows to continue, then I'd happily be subjected to study. There are plenty of things I'd like to learn, too.

Sanderson: Good to hear, we can negotiate testing specifics at a future date. You still haven't answered my question about 106, by the way.

Holmes: Ah. Well, I'm able to bolster structures that want to return to their purpose, but that's all I can do. In the case where the structure was built to be a place of torment, there'd be no point. It's already doing what it was built to do. The Dachshund wouldn't work either, the story can't be carried out if the setting is unwilling. Just like humans, sometimes you get a bad apple that's just too far gone.

Sanderson: I see.

[Sanderson stands.]

Sanderson: Well, I think I have enough to make my report. Understanding your motives has helped and your willingness to cooperate has been noted, and I think I personally believe you aren't a threat to us or the veil. But as I said, it's ultimately not up to me.

Holmes: Of course. Thank you for your time, Mrs. Sanderson. Ah, one more thing before you go—

Sanderson: Go on.

Holmes: Although I haven't met them, I can only imagine they're proud of all of you.

Sanderson: Pardon?

Holmes: All that you have built is proud of you for doing so. Through disaster, schisms, the passing of the guard, old horrors and new, it's all stood. You built it up on a strong, shared purpose. A strong foundation for everything else to rest upon.

Sanderson: Was that a pun?

Holmes: No more than naming the rocket Apollo. Have a good day, Mrs. Sanderson. I'll get the door for you.

[Sanderson hangs up the phone, and exits through the door to the room, now functioning as an SCP-9106-A instance.]

[END LOG]
